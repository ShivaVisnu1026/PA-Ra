# Major Reversals (Part 4): Bottoming, Head and Shoulders, and Failed Reversals

## Major Reversal Bottoms

### Key Signals
- **Trend Line Break**: First need to see downtrend line broken upward with strength.
- **Strong Bounce**:
  - **Characteristics**: Bounce preferably lasts 5-10 bars or more, contains strong bull bars, and touches moving average.
  - **Moving Average Gap Bar (MA Gap Bar)**: If a bar appears completely above the moving average, this is a very strong bullish signal, showing buying power is sufficient to control the market.
- **Final Leg Down**: After the above strong bounce appears, the subsequent decline is usually the final leg of the bear market.

### Trading Logic
- **Looking for Entry Points**: When the market tests prior lows, look for reversal rally signals.
  - **Higher Low Major Reversal**
  - **Double Bottom Major Reversal**
  - **Lower Low Major Reversal**
- **Note**: If the prior decline was a very narrow channel (almost no buying pressure), the first bounce is likely just a minor bounce. The market will likely enter a trading range rather than directly reversing into an uptrend.

## Head and Shoulders Patterns

### Structural Breakdown
- **Head & Shoulders Top**: Essentially a combination of two major reversal patterns.
  - **Left Shoulder to Head**: Usually a "higher high" major reversal.
  - **Head to Right Shoulder**: A "lower high" major reversal. The right shoulder's inability to surpass the head is a key signal of bullish power exhaustion.
- **Head & Shoulders Bottom**: Opposite logic to head and shoulders top.
  - **Left Shoulder to Head**: Usually a "lower low" major reversal.
  - **Head to Right Shoulder**: A "higher low" major reversal. The right shoulder higher than the head is a key signal of bearish power exhaustion.
- **Nested Structure**: In large head and shoulders patterns, their components (left shoulder, head, right shoulder) often contain smaller-level double tops/bottoms or mini head and shoulders patterns.

## Failed Reversals

### Identification and Probability
- **High Failure Rate**: Major reversals only have approximately 40% probability of ultimately forming new trends, meaning approximately 60% of attempts will fail.
- **Failure Signals**: When a reversal pattern (such as head and shoulders top) forms, price does not break in the expected direction but instead breaks in the original trend direction (e.g., breaking up through the right shoulder of a head and shoulders top).

### Trading Implications
- **High-Probability Trend-Following Signal**: A failed reversal is a very reliable **trend-following trading** signal.
- **Behind the Dual Force**:
  - **Counter-Trend Traders Stopping Out**: Traders attempting reversals (such as going short on head and shoulders top) have stops triggered (shorts covering by buying), adding fuel to the breakout.
  - **Trend-Following Traders Entering**: They see the reversal failed and confirm the original trend will continue, so they actively enter.
- **Trading Targets**:
  - When a reversal fails, expect price to move in the original trend direction for at least **two-legged movement**.
  - Can use **Measured Move (MM)** to estimate price targets, usually based on the height of the failed pattern.

## Summary Principles
- **Bottoming Requires Buying Pressure**: Effective major reversal bottoms need convincing proof of buying power, such as strong bounces and moving average gap bars.
- **Head and Shoulders Is Power Transition**: The essence of head and shoulders patterns is gradual transition of trend power. The relative high/low point of the right shoulder is the key confirmation signal.
- **Failure Is a Stronger Signal**: When a pattern fails, it usually evolves into a stronger, more reliable trend-following trading opportunity due to stops of original participants.
