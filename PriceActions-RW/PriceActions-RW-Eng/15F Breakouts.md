# Failed Breakouts: Trend Resumption or Reversal

## Core Concept of "Failed Breakout"

### Definition & Outcomes
-   **Concept**: In Al Brooks' context, "failed breakout" refers to market's counter-direction force **attempting** to reverse it after a breakout (like one strong trend bar) occurs. This is essentially a **pullback or consolidation**.
-   **Two Outcomes**:
    1.  **Trend Resumption (Failure of Failure)**: Reversal **attempt** fails; pullback evolves into flag (bull flag or bear flag); original trend continues.
    2.  **Trend Reversal (Success of Failure)**: Reversal **attempt** succeeds; original breakout completely reversed; market trend changes direction.

### Basic Perspective
-   Every bar on chart can be interpreted as: **Breakout**, **Attempt to make breakout fail (pullback)**, or **Successful reversal**.

## Trading Decisions & Analysis

### Power Contrast is Key
-   **Core Method**: Continuously compare **breakout strength** (bar body size, close position, continuity) with **reversal attempt strength**.
-   **Market Interpretation**:
    -   **Strength Unclear**: If upward breakout and downward reversal attempt equally strong or both weak (like overlapping bars, long tails), market in confused state; likely enters **sideways range (tight range)**. Should maintain patience, wait for clearer signals.
    -   **One Side Dominates**: When one side (bulls or bears) shows overwhelming strength (like huge trend bar, close far beyond prior bar range, with good follow-through), market direction clear; with-trend trading probability higher.

### Importance of Follow-Through
-   **Confirmation Signal**: A strong breakout needs **confirmation by subsequent bars (Follow-through)**.
-   **Beware Traps**: If a seemingly strong breakout lacks good follow-through but is quickly reversed, this may be a **trap**, predicting breakout failure and reversal.

### Time Factor
-   **Momentum Decay**: After a breakout occurs, if subsequent pullback/consolidation lasts too long (e.g., over 10-20 bars), original breakout urgency and momentum gradually disappear.
-   **Probability Change**: As consolidation time lengthens, market gradually shifts from favoring original trend direction to **50/50 neutral state**; original trend resumption probability decreases.

## Special Pattern: Narrow Channel Breakout

### First Reversal Attempt = Minor Reversal
-   **Scenario**: When a long-lasting (like 20 bars) **narrow channel trend** (like narrow bear channel) is first broken upward.
-   **High-Probability Result**: This breakout likely just a **Minor Reversal**, probably evolving into bear flag; original downtrend resumes.
-   **Trading Strategy**: Aggressive traders will treat this first breakout as **sell opportunity (Fade)**, betting it fails.

### Second Reversal Attempt = Major Reversal
-   **Scenario**: After first reversal attempt (minor reversal) fails and trend resumes, if market **attempts reversal second time** (usually forming some double bottom/top structure).
-   **High-Probability Result**: Second attempt has higher probability of success, forming **Major Reversal**, thus completely changing market trend.

## Summary Principles
-   **Redefine Failure**: View every pullback as an "attempt to make breakout fail"; its success or failure determines market's next direction.
-   **Compare Strength**: Assess real intent and strength of bulls and bears by comparing breakout and reversal attempt strength, judging whether market continues trend or enters consolidation.
-   **Beware First Breakout**: For very persistent narrow channel trends, first counter-direction breakout is often trap (minor reversal), opportunity to fade highs/lows.
-   **Seek Second Opportunity**: True trend reversals (major reversals) usually occur on second reversal attempt, often accompanied by double bottom/top formations.
