# Protective Stops and Trailing

## Core Principle: Stops Based on Important Highs/Lows

### Definition and Logic
-   **Price Action Stop**: Set stops based on price action on the chart, not fixed ticks or percentages.
-   **Important High/Low (Major Swing High/Low)**:
    -   **Uptrend**: An important low is the starting point of an uptrend that triggered a strong breakout or made a new high.
    -   **Downtrend**: An important high is the starting point of a downtrend that triggered a strong breakdown or made a new low.
-   **Trend Premise**:
    -   The key to an **uptrend** is continuously forming **higher important lows**. If an important low is broken, the uptrend premise is no longer valid.
    -   The key to a **downtrend** is continuously forming **lower important highs**. If an important high is broken, the downtrend premise is no longer valid.

### Trading Application
-   **Initial Stop Placement**: After entering a with-trend trade, initial protective stop should be placed beyond the most recent **important high/low**.
    -   When long, place stop below the most recent important low.
    -   When short, place stop above the most recent important high.
-   **Purpose**: This location provides sufficient room for the trade to tolerate normal pullbacks while defining the point where trend fails. Breaking through this point means market structure has changed (transitioned to trading range or reversal).

## Trailing Stops

### Definition and Operation
-   **Concept**: As trend continues, traders dynamically adjust stop placement to protect existing profits and reduce risk.
-   **Method**: When market forms a **new strong breakout** in your position's direction and makes new highs/lows, the original starting point becomes a **new important high/low**.
-   **Execution**: Traders should "trail" or move stops beyond this **new important high/low**.

### Trading Implications
-   **Risk Management**: Gradually lock in profits, reducing potential losses.
-   **Discipline**: Provides an objective, price-action-based rule for managing positions, avoiding premature or delayed exits due to emotions.

## Stop Placement Selection: Trade-off Between Wide and Narrow

### Two Common Choices
1.  **Narrow Stop (Price Action Stop)**:
    -   **Location**: Tight against the other end of the signal bar. For example, buying above a buy signal bar, place stop below its low.
    -   **Advantages**: Small risk amount, high risk-reward ratio.
    -   **Disadvantages**: Lower win rate, easily stopped out by normal volatility.
2.  **Wide Stop (Swing Stop)**:
    -   **Location**: Placed beyond the most recent **important high/low**.
    -   **Advantages**: Higher win rate, can tolerate deeper pullbacks, less likely to be stopped out prematurely.
    -   **Disadvantages**: Large risk amount, relatively lower risk-reward ratio.

### Trading Implications
-   **Similar Results**: Long-term, as long as both stop placements are reasonable, their overall profitability is similar. Traders can choose based on their style and risk tolerance.
-   **Avoid Extremes**: Stops **should not be too narrow or too wide**. Too narrow leads to frequent losses; too wide leads to excessive single-trade losses; both are losing strategies.

## Stop Management at Trend End

### Identifying Trend Exhaustion Signals
-   **Trading Range Characteristics**: Bars begin overlapping significantly, breakout strength weakens (e.g., long upper/lower tails), pullback depth exceeds prior breakout points.
-   **Exhaustion Gap**: In late trend (e.g., after 20+ bars), the **largest** bar in the trend direction appears, typically a signal of trend termination rather than acceleration.
-   **Wedge Top/Bottom**: Three-push or multi-push exhaustion patterns appear.

### Trading Strategy Adjustment
-   **Abandon Reliance on Distant Stops**: When trend exhaustion signals appear, continuing to rely on important high/low wide stops is no longer the best strategy.
-   **Active Exit**: Should more actively look for **reasons to exit early**, such as selling for profit when reversal bars appear, rather than waiting for stops to be hit.
-   **Purpose**: When market structure transitions from trend to trading range or reversal, should switch to trading range thinking (sell high, buy low) to protect profits.
