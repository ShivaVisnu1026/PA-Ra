# Rounded Tops and Bottoms

## Redefinition: Name is Not Important, Behavior is Key

### Core View
-   **Nature is Trading Range**: Al Brooks emphasizes that so-called rounded tops or bottoms are essentially **trading ranges** formed after trends stall.
-   **Name is Not the Focus**: He rarely uses descriptive terms like "rounded top/bottom", instead preferring more precise, actionable terminology such as **Head and Shoulders**, **Lower High/Higher Low Major Trend Reversals**, **Triangles**, etc.
-   **Focus on Market State**: The focus of trading is understanding that the market has shifted from trending to **bull-bear balance**, and identifying specific trading signals, rather than getting stuck on pattern naming.

## The Endless Pullback/Bounce

### Definition and Evolution
-   **Concept**: A pullback (or bounce) in a trend that lasts too long (usually **more than 20 bars**) causes its nature to change. It is no longer a simple flag, but evolves into an independent, opposite-direction **tight channel**, or a horizontal **trading range**.
-   **Probability Shift**: Once a pullback/bounce lasts more than 20 bars, the market enters a **balanced state**. The probability of original trend continuation drops from high (e.g., 60%) to **50%**, while reversal probability correspondingly rises to **50%**.

### Trading Implications
-   **Entering Breakout Mode**: When an "endless pullback/bounce" is identified, realize the market is in **breakout mode**, with equal probability of breaking up or down.
-   **Caution with Trend Trading**: At this stage, the original trend's advantage no longer exists; do not blindly look for trend entries. Be especially alert to opposite breakouts of tight channel patterns.
-   **Wait for Confirmation Signals**: The key to trading is patiently waiting for one side to prevail. This manifests as **strong breakout bars** (such as consecutive large bull/bear bars) and **good follow-through**, which are reliable entry signals.

## Trading Psychology & Behavior

### Rounded Tops and Distribution
-   **Behavioral Characteristics**: In the consolidation at the end of an uptrend, **strong bears** consider price too high and will actively **sell rallies** (especially large bull bars), betting that upward momentum will fail. Meanwhile, **strong bulls** also stop chasing highs due to high prices, choosing to wait or wait for deeper pullbacks.
-   **Result**: When only weak bulls following the trend remain, the market easily breaks down under strong bear selling pressure, forming a reversal.

### Rounded Bottoms and Accumulation
-   **Behavioral Characteristics**: In the consolidation at the end of a downtrend, **strong bulls** consider price low enough and treat every decline as an opportunity to **buy dips** (accumulation). They continuously build positions at low levels, betting that downward breakouts will fail.
-   **Result**: Shorts find price unable to continue declining when shorting (due to bull accumulation and shorts' own profit-taking), eventually losing confidence and giving up. When shorts exit and bulls take control, the market breaks up.

## Summary Principles
-   **Behavior Over Naming**: Understanding that rounded tops/bottoms are states of **trading ranges** and **bull-bear balance** is more important than remembering their names.
-   **Time Changes Probability**: A pullback/bounce lasting **more than 20 bars** is a key signal, indicating the market has entered **50/50 breakout mode**.
-   **Trade Based on Confirmation**: Regardless of pattern, final trading decisions should be based on clear **breakouts and follow-through**, which confirm the direction of bull-bear imbalance.
-   **Insight into Market Psychology**: Recognizing the institutional behavior of "distribution" at tops and "accumulation" at bottoms helps understand the struggle patterns and final breakout tendencies within these areas.
