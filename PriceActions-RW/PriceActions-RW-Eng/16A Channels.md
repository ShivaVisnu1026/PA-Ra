# Channel Basics & Drawing Principles

## Basic Concepts & Multi-Timeframe Nature of Channels

-   **Definition**: Price runs between two (usually parallel) lines. Any market, any timeframe, price runs in channels, even if not easily visible.
-   **Components**:
    -   **Trendline**: Bottom line in uptrend; top line in downtrend.
    -   **Channel Line**: Line opposite to trendline, forming channel's other boundary.
-   **Multi-Timeframe Nature**: Trend channel on small timeframe may just be a pullback (like bear flag or bull flag) on larger timeframe. Traders need to understand current channel's meaning in larger context.

## How to Draw Effective Channel Lines

-   **Drawing Principle**: Drawing lines is to better understand market structure, assist trading decisions. Lines don't have to be perfect.
-   **Overshoot & Undershoot**:
    -   **Overshoot**: Price briefly breaks channel line then returns.
    -   **Undershoot**: Price reverses before touching channel line.
-   **Selection Preference**: When choosing between two drawing methods, usually prefer one that produces "overshoot" because it highlights price structure better.
-   **Dynamic Adjustment**: As new bars appear, traders should reassess and adjust drawn lines based on new information (like new important highs/lows).
-   **Parallelism**: More parallel channel lines are more reliable because computers and professional traders pay more attention to them, forming more effective support and resistance.

## Channels & Market Cycle

-   **Market Cycle Evolution**: Market usually follows "**Breakout → Channel → Trading Range**" cycle.
-   **Channel's Fate**: Approximately 75% of trend channels ultimately evolve into trading ranges.
-   **View as Counter Flags**:
    -   **Bull Channel**: Can be viewed as **Bear Flag**, 75% probability of being broken downward.
    -   **Bear Channel**: Can be viewed as **Bull Flag**, high probability of being broken upward.
-   **Trading Strategy**:
    -   Look for with-trend trading opportunities at channel lower edge (like buying at bull channel lower edge).
    -   Take profits at channel upper edge, or consider counter scalping in sufficiently wide channels.

## The 70% Rule: Channel Breakout & Reversal

-   **Core Concept**: When a channel is broken (whether upward or downward), approximately 70% (or 75%) probability this breakout will **fail and reverse within about 5 bars**, price will test other side of channel.
-   **Key Application Condition: Maximum Timeframe**:
    -   This rule applies to **maximum timeframe chart where this channel can be clearly drawn**.
    -   **Example**: A breakout lasting 100 bars on 1-minute chart may be just 2-3 bars on 30-minute chart. Should apply "reverse within 5 bars" rule on 30-minute chart, not on 1-minute chart.

## Channel Line Validity & Symmetry

-   **Symmetry Principle**: Connected points should have certain symmetry in time and space. If large number of bars between points 1 and 2, but only few bars between points 2 and 3, this line likely invalid.
-   **Ignore Invalid Lines**: When strong breakout appears, prior small-scale swings and lines drawn based on them become irrelevant. Computers and professional traders focus on lines formed by important highs/lows (usually points that triggered strong breakouts).
-   **Different Channel Forms**:
    -   **Parallel Channel**: Most common, reliable.
    -   **Converging Channel (Triangle)**: Channel lines getting closer.
    -   **Diverging Channel (Expanding Triangle)**: Channel lines getting farther apart.
    -   Regardless of form, their boundary lines provide traders reference points for observing price reactions.

## Summary Principles
-   **Drawing Lines is Core**: Market always in channels. When drawing lines, prefer those producing "overshoot" and maintaining parallelism; dynamically adjust as market develops.
-   **Channels are Flags**: View bull channels as bear flags, bear channels as bull flags; this helps predict probable breakout direction.
-   **Understand 70% Rule**: Approximately 70% of channel breakouts fail, but must apply this rule on **maximum timeframe that can accommodate this channel** (reverse within about 5 bars).
-   **Focus on Symmetry & Importance**: Valid trendlines/channel lines connect symmetrical and important turning points, not arbitrary points.
