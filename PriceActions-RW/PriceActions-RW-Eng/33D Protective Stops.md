# Stops and Premise Changes

## Trend Premise and Important Highs/Lows

### Premise Definition
-   **Uptrend Premise**: Market continuously creates higher highs and higher lows. As long as important support lows are not broken, this premise holds.
-   **Downtrend Premise**: Market continuously creates lower highs and lower lows. As long as important resistance highs are not broken, this premise holds.
-   **Premise Importance**: Traders enter based on current market premise (e.g., "this is an uptrend"). Once price action indicates the premise is no longer valid, traders must change their trading approach.

### Identifying Important Highs/Lows
-   **Important Point**: The turning point that triggered strong, sustained price movement and made new highs/lows. For example, in an uptrend, a low followed by strong rally making new highs makes that low important.
-   **Minor Point**: A turning point that only causes brief, weak bounces/pullbacks. Even if broken, it typically doesn't change overall trend premise; may just be a more complex pullback/bounce structure.

## Evolution from Trend to Trading Range

### Evolution Signals
-   **Premise Failure**: In uptrends, an **important low** is broken; or in downtrends, an **important high** is broken. This is the most critical signal.
-   **"Big Rally, Big Selloff, Big Confusion"**: Strong trend movement is quickly reversed by equally strong counter-movement, indicating balanced forces between bulls and bears, market entering confusion state, i.e., trading range.
-   **Poor Follow-Through After Breakout**: After price breaks prior high/low, subsequent bars are weak or even show counter-trend bars, indicating insufficient breakout momentum, market tending to trade sideways.

### Trading Strategy Adjustment
-   **Stop Trend Trading**: When trend premise fails, abandon original with-trend swing holding strategy.
-   **Switch to Range Trading**: Adopt sell-high-buy-low, scalping strategies. Buy at range support, sell at resistance, take profits quickly, because market is unlikely to go far.
-   **Exit Timely**: Once signals of premise change appear, exit immediately or tighten stops, rather than waiting for original, distant stops to be hit.

## Identifying Trend Exhaustion

### Buy/Sell Climax
-   **Definition**: The strongest, fastest price movement in late trend (typically consisting of consecutive large-body bars).
-   **Characteristics**: This is typically not a signal of trend acceleration, but an **exhaustion gap**, the final frenzy before trend ends.
-   **High-Probability Reversal**: Buy/sell climaxes have over 60% probability of failing, market will reverse and at least retrace to the starting point of that climax move.

### Trading Implications
-   **Beware Chasing Highs/Lows**: Don't chase in late-trend acceleration moves; this is likely a trap.
-   **Look for Reversal Signals**: After buy climax, once poor follow-through appears (e.g., counter-trend bars), exit decisively. Aggressive traders can look for reversal opportunities here.
-   **Case Analysis**: A triangle or wedge lasting 20+ bars, broken in late trend, is typically a **final flag**, with high probability of breakout failure and reversal.

## Summary Principles
-   **Premise Is Core**: Every trade is built on a premise. Your primary task is to judge whether the current premise is still valid.
-   **Important Highs/Lows Are Key**: Focus on whether important highs/lows are broken; this is the core basis for judging whether trend premise has changed.
-   **Price Action Reveals Everything**: When price action (e.g., "big rally big selloff," exhaustion climax, breakout failure) contradicts your trend premise, believe what you see, not what you hope.
-   **Flexible Adaptation**: Market state (trend, trading range) changes dynamically. When market changes, immediately adjust your trading strategy, exit decisively and reassess; this is key to protecting profits and controlling losses.
