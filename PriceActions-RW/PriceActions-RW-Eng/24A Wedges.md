# Wedges (Part One)

## Definition and Identification of Wedges

### Basic Concepts
-   **Definition**: A price structure with **3 or more pushes** in one direction. It is essentially an **upward or downward sloping converging triangle**.
-   **Structure**: Usually consists of 5 legs, where 3 legs move in the main trend direction and 2 are counter-trend pullbacks.
-   **Characteristics**:
    -   **Convergence**: The upper and lower channel lines that form the wedge typically get closer together.
    -   **Bar Overlap**: Subsequent pullbacks usually enter the price range of previous legs, which is a signal of weakening trend.
    -   **Imperfection**: Wedges in reality are rarely perfect; recognizing the essence of **three failed attempts** is more important than precise line drawing.

### Two Main Types of Wedges
-   **Reversal Pattern**:
    -   **Wedge Top**: Appears at the end of an uptrend, formed by three upward pushes, signaling a potential top reversal.
    -   **Wedge Bottom**: Appears at the end of a downtrend, formed by three downward pushes, signaling a potential bottom reversal.
-   **Continuation Pattern / Flag**:
    -   **Wedge Bear Flag**: A small, upward three-push bounce in a downtrend, signaling the downtrend is likely to resume.
    -   **Wedge Bull Flag**: A small, downward three-push pullback in an uptrend, signaling the uptrend is likely to resume.

## Market Psychology Behind Wedges

### Momentum Exhaustion and Climactic Behavior
-   **Seeking Fair Value**: The market seeks a fair price acceptable to both bulls and bears through "overshoot" movements up or down. A wedge represents momentum exhaustion after repeated attempts.
-   **Climax**: Each push in a wedge is usually accompanied by **buying/selling climaxes**, where bulls and bears trade based on momentum rather than value.
-   **FOMO (Fear of Missing Out)**: This momentum trading attracts many traders, forming a **crowded trade**.

### Dynamics of Reversal
-   **Unwinding of Crowded Trades**: Once momentum disappears, crowded trades quickly unwind, causing everyone to rush for exits, triggering sharp reversals.
-   **Trapped Traders**:
    -   **Trapped Inside**: Trend-following traders who didn't exit in time (such as longs in a wedge top) will exit on any pullback after the reversal, exacerbating the reversal.
    -   **Trapped Outside**: Counter-trend traders who missed the best entry point will actively enter on pullbacks after the reversal.
-   **Dual Forces**: These two forces together drive price, often causing at least a **two-legged move** after the reversal.

## Probabilities of Wedges, Channels, and Triangles

-   **Up Wedge/Channel**:
    -   Usually treated as a **bear flag**.
    -   Has approximately **75%** probability of breaking **down** or **sideways** (entering a trading range).
-   **Down Wedge/Channel**:
    -   Usually treated as a **bull flag**.
    -   Has approximately **75%** probability of breaking **up**.
-   **Horizontal Triangle**:
    -   Probability of breaking up or down is **50/50**.
    -   First breakout has approximately **50%** probability of **failing** and reversing.
-   **Trading Insight**: When a pattern's slope is between horizontal and clearly sloped, its reversal probability also lies between 50% and 75%.

## Drawing and Interpreting Wedges

### How to Draw Lines
-   **Multiple Choices**: Since wedges have at least 3 touch points, connecting point 1-2 or point 1-3 produces two different trendlines.
-   **Overshoot vs. Shortfall**:
    -   **Shortfall**: The third point fails to reach the trendline, indicating early momentum exhaustion.
    -   **Overshoot**: The third point breaks through the trendline and pulls back, signaling **failed acceleration**.
-   **Drawing Preference**: Usually prefer the drawing method that produces **"overshoot"**, as it more clearly reveals the key behavior of the market's failed attempt to accelerate.

### Recognizing Imperfect Wedges
-   **Focus on Essence**: Don't demand perfect patterns; the key is recognizing the essence of the market **failing after three attempts** to push.
-   **Nested Structure**: Large wedges often contain small wedges inside, which increases the probability of reversal.
-   **Multiple Timeframe Perspective**: A wedge that looks unclear on the current timeframe may be a very clear structure on a smaller timeframe.

## Summary Principles
-   **Core is Three Pushes**: A wedge is the result of the market making three pushes in one direction and then exhausting momentum.
-   **Distinguish Context**: Judge whether it is a reversal pattern or continuation pattern (flag) based on the wedge's position and size within the overall trend.
-   **Understand Psychology**: The powerful reversal dynamics of wedges stem from the unwinding of FOMO-driven crowded trades and the exit/entry behavior of trapped traders.
-   **Probability Edge**: Sloping wedges/channels provide approximately 75% probability edge; traders should trade in the direction of the higher probability.
-   **Lines Serve Analysis**: Choose the drawing method that produces "overshoot" (Overshoot) to better identify the key signal of failed acceleration.
