# Scaling In at a Loss (Scaling In Part 2)

## Basic Logic and Purpose of Scaling In at a Loss

### Definition and Goals
-   **Concept**: When a trade shows floating loss after entry, but original trading premise (e.g., trend direction) remains valid, add position at more favorable price point.
-   **Core Purpose**: Manage a troubled trade; primary goal is **avoiding loss**, not pursuing higher profits.
-   **Layered Goals**:
    -   **Minimum Goal**: Exit at average cost of all positions, achieving **overall breakeven**.
    -   **Better Goal**: Close first position at initial entry point (breakeven), letting added portion profit.
    -   **Ideal Goal**: If trend strongly resumes, hold all positions, achieving overall profit.

### Prerequisites
-   **Trading Premise Still Valid**: Chart's big picture hasn't changed. For example, in uptrend, price has pulled back but still above key higher low.
-   **Plan in Advance**: Must plan scaling in possibility, approximate location, and final protective stop before entry.
-   **Discipline**: If price action severely contradicts expectations (e.g., unexpected strong reversal), should abandon scaling in plan and exit early.

## Strategy and Execution of Scaling In at a Loss

### Core Principle: Position Management
-   **Risk Control Is Key**: Success or failure of scaling in at a loss strategy **completely depends on strict position management**.
-   **Reduce Initial Position**: Since need to set a wider final stop, **initial position must be significantly reduced** (e.g., reduce to 1/3 or 1/2 of normal position).
-   **Constant Total Risk**: No matter how many times you add, total risk of all positions combined (total amount from average entry to final stop) **must never exceed your normal risk per trade**.
-   **Act Within Means**: If your account size or psychological capacity cannot support reducing position to necessary level, **must abandon using this strategy** and wait for other opportunities.

### Scaling In Location Selection
-   **Based on Technical Analysis**: Add at key support/resistance levels, 50% retracement levels, or when clear reversal bar patterns appear.
-   **Ensure Effective Spacing**: Distance between addition point and previous entry should be **at least greater than minimum profit of one scalp trade** (e.g., at least 1 point in ES, at least 10 pips in forex); ideally 2-3 times that distance to ensure cost reduction effect is sufficiently obvious.

## Exit Management and "Stinky Stops"

### Reasonable Exit Strategy
-   **Exit When Disappointed**: When pullback depth and duration exceed expectations, making you think market has transitioned from trend to trading range, should aim for **breakeven** or **small profit**, exiting when price bounces to average cost or initial entry price.
-   **Hold When Trend Resumes**: If market strongly reverses and resumes original trend after adding, can change plan and continue holding all positions until new trend-ending signals appear.

### Must Avoid "Stinky Stops"
-   **Definition**: Stops placed between **early reasonable exit point** and **final protective stop**, at illogical positions.
-   **Nature**: These stops are **emotional decisions** traders make due to inability to bear floating loss torture, typically located in the middle of trading ranges, which is the worst exit point.
-   **Harm**: Selling when institutions and smart money are buying (on pullbacks) is a typical bad habit causing account losses.
-   **Correct Approach**: **Either exit early based on clear signals, or firmly trust your initially set, chart-structure-based final stop**. Never stop out arbitrarily between the two due to fear.

## Summary Principles
-   **Scaling In at a Loss Is a Risk Management Tool**: Its nature is a rescue strategy, not an offensive strategy.
-   **Position Management Determines Success**: Must proportionally significantly reduce position size based on expanded stop distance, keeping total risk constant.
-   **Stick to Trading Premise**: Only consider scaling in when original trading logic hasn't been broken.
-   **Eliminate Emotional 'Stinky Stops'**: Strictly adhere to preset exit rules; either exit early or wait for final signal, don't waver in between.
