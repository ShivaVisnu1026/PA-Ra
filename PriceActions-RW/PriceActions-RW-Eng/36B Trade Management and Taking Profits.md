# Trade Management and Profit-Taking Default Standards

## Dynamic Profit Target Adjustment

### Recognizing Premise Failure
-   **Concept**: Even when traders have set a reasonable profit target (e.g., measured move MM target), when market shows strong counter-trend bar signals, it means the "premise" that original trend will continue has failed or probability greatly decreased.
-   **Action**: Must abandon sticking to original target and exit decisively. Placing stop to close at counter-trend bar's high (when short) or low (when long) is wise response to sudden reversals.

## Risk-Based Profit-Taking Strategy

### Initial Risk vs. Actual Risk
-   **Initial Risk**: Distance from entry point to initial stop point (e.g., other end of signal bar or important swing point). Often used to calculate position size.
-   **Actual Risk**: After entry, maximum unfavorable movement of market against your position. In strong breakouts, actual risk is typically far smaller than initial risk.

### Reasonable Target vs. Optimal Target
-   **Mathematically Reasonable**: In high-probability trades, using 1 times or 2 times *actual risk* profit as profit-taking target is mathematically positive and "reasonable."
-   **Potential Problem**: When actual risk is extremely small (e.g., a few ticks) while move potential is huge, this "reasonable" target is not the "optimal" choice. Prematurely locking in tiny profits causes you to miss substantial returns from trends.
-   **Core Principle**: Don't abandon potentially more substantial targets judged from current price action (e.g., strong momentum, measured gaps) just because a target is mathematically "feasible."

### Default Profit-Taking Standards
-   **Minimum Standard**: 2 times *actual risk*.
-   **Better Standard**: 2 times *initial risk*. This is a more robust default profit-taking reference point, especially in swing trading.
-   **Best Practice**: Combine price action (e.g., MM targets, channel lines, exhaustion signals) to set and adjust targets, and consider scaling out (e.g., close half at 2 times risk, trail remaining position with trend).

## Recognizing Trend Exhaustion and Exit Timing

### Key Exit Signals
-   **Reversal Near Target**: Market repeatedly approaches key target (e.g., MM target) but fails to reach it, then shows reversal signs.
-   **Exhaustion Gap**: In late trend, an unusually large trend bar appears (e.g., largest in past 40-50 bars), typically a signal of trend exhaustion.
-   **Trend Evolution**: Strong one-sided trend (spike) evolves into channel, then evolves into narrow trading range lasting over 20 bars. Once entering narrow trading range, reversal probability has risen to equal trend continuation (about 50%), should immediately consider closing with-trend positions.

## Trading Psychology and Goal Setting

### Avoid Micro-Profit Mindset
-   **Large Move Potential**: When price action (e.g., strong breakout) indicates potential for large moves, should prepare to hold positions to capture larger swings.
-   **Scalping Misconception**: Avoid only taking micro scalping profits (e.g., less than 10 pips in forex) in moves with huge potential; this doesn't fit risk-reward principles.

### Profit Target Milestones
-   **Important Psychological Threshold**: For professional traders, daily profit reaching $5,000 is an important psychological and financial milestone, as it typically corresponds to annual income of one million dollars, a goal many traders pursue.

## Summary Principles
-   **Premise Overrides Target**: When price action shows trend premise has failed (e.g., strong reversal appears), exit immediately rather than stubbornly holding to original target.
-   **Distinguish Reasonable from Optimal**: 2 times risk is a "reasonable" mathematical target, but "optimal" target should be dynamically judged combining price action (e.g., MM targets, trend strength).
-   **Beware Trend Transitions**: When a trend evolves from strong one-sided move to narrow trading range lasting over 20 bars, this is a key signal to close with-trend positions, as reversal risk has greatly increased.
-   **Pursue Large Moves**: In high-probability large trend contexts, should plan to hold positions to capture larger profits, avoiding premature exits due to satisfaction with minimum "reasonable" profit standards.
