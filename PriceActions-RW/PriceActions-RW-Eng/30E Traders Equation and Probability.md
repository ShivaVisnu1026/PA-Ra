# Mathematical Expectation and Probability (Part Five)

## The 90% Certainty Paradox at Trade Targets

### Phenomenon
-   When a trade's price is only 1 tick away from the preset profit target, potential remaining profit is minimal, but the risk traders bear (distance to initial stop) is very large.

### Trader Psychology
-   Under this extremely unequal reward/risk ratio, if trader chooses to continue holding rather than immediately closing, they are actually indicating through action that they **have at least 90% confidence** the market will hit target price before hitting their stop.
-   This reveals traders' common psychological bias and irrational risk assessment when approaching profit targets.

## Core Principle: The 40-60 Rule

### Definition
-   **90% of Time**: On any chart, 90% of bars are in **channels or trading ranges**, not strong one-sided breakouts.
-   **40-60% Probability**: During this 90% of time, market probability of rallying X points or declining X points always hovers between **40% and 60%**. Neither side has absolute advantage.
-   **10% of Time**: Only about 10% of bars are in strong breakout states, when trend continuation probability truly **exceeds 60%**.

### Trading Implications
-   **Win Rate Unreliable**: In the vast majority of market environments, traders cannot rely on win rates exceeding 60%.
-   **Management is Key**: Since win rate is limited, the key to profitability shifts from "accurately predicting direction" to **"excellent money management"** (setting reasonable stops and profit targets).

## Profitable Strategy: Swing Trading & Money Management

### The Scalping Trap
-   **Definition**: Trading approach pursuing small, quick profits.
-   **Problem**: Due to small profits, extremely high win rate (e.g., 70%-90%) is needed to cover losses and profit, which is unrealistic for the vast majority of traders and creates huge psychological pressure.

### The Swing Trading Advantage
-   **Strategy**: Adopt **swing trading**, targeting profits at least **twice** initial risk (reward/risk ratio R:R ≥ 2:1).
-   **Positive Mathematical Expectation**: According to the 40-60 rule, any trade has at least **40%** win rate. When profit is twice risk, even with only 40% win rate, trading system's mathematical expectation is positive and can profit long-term.
-   **Universality**: This makes it possible, in 90% of market time, whether going long or short, as long as money management is proper, to design profitable trading strategies.

## Probability Applications Within Trading Ranges

### Range Division and Win Rate
-   **Top Third**: Higher win rate for shorts (approximately 60%). Favorable area for "selling rallies".
-   **Bottom Third**: Higher win rate for longs (approximately 60%). Favorable area for "buying dips".
-   **Middle Third**: Win rates for both sides are 50%, no clear advantage, trade mathematical expectation approaches zero.

### Trading Strategy
-   In clear trading ranges, should look for counter-trend trade opportunities at range ends (top/bottom), and consider taking profits when price approaches range midpoint or opposite side.

## Summary Principles
-   **Accept 40-60 Rule**: Acknowledge that market direction is unclear 90% of the time; win rate in either direction is between 40-60%.
-   **Management Over Prediction**: Key to profitability lies in money management, especially maintaining good reward/risk ratio, not pursuing high-win-rate predictions.
-   **Swing Trading is Core**: For most people, pursuing **2:1 reward/risk ratio** swing trading is the best path to consistent profitability.
-   **Utilize Range Probabilities**: In choppy moves, shorting near tops and going long near bottoms can effectively improve trade win rate.
-   **Beware Psychological Traps**: Understand psychological biases in trading process (such as 90% certainty paradox), avoid making decisions under unfavorable reward/risk ratios.
