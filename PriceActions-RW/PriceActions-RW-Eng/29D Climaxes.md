# Climaxes (Part Four): Consecutive Climaxes and Market Psychology

## Consecutive Climaxes

### Definition and Characteristics
-   **Core Concept**: A series of trend bars (i.e., climax bars) appearing consecutively in a trend with almost no pullbacks between them. Each trend bar itself is a small breakout and climax.
-   **Pattern Characteristics**:
    -   Usually forms a **parabolic wedge**, consisting of three or more consecutive pushes.
    -   Rally process shows a series of large bull bars; decline process shows a series of large bear bars.
-   **End of Climax**: When dojis, opposite bars, or bars with significantly smaller bodies appear, it indicates the current climax wave has temporarily ended.

### Market Psychology and Causes
-   **Weak Trader Behavior**:
    -   **Weak Shorts/Longs Being Squeezed**: In uptrends, weak shorts trying to "pick the top" continuously short, forced to stop out at higher levels (covering by buying), adding fuel to the rally. Same logic applies to weak longs trying to "buy the bottom" in downtrends.
    -   **Weak Trend Followers Chasing Highs/Lows**: Hesitant trend-following traders, seeing consecutive strong bars, finally enter emotionally at the highest/lowest points due to fear of missing out.
-   **Strong Trader Behavior**:
    -   **Trend Side Taking Profits**: Strong longs who entered at low levels view consecutive climaxes as "market gifts" and begin selling at high levels in batches.
    -   **Counter-Trend Side Beginning to Build**: Strong shorts (in rallies) identify consecutive climaxes as trend exhaustion signals and begin building positions in batches at resistance levels they consider high (such as prior highs, channel upper bounds).
-   **Result**: When weak traders' ammunition is exhausted, and both strong longs and strong shorts are selling, buying dries up, and the market naturally goes sideways or reverses.

## Channels as Flags

### 75/25 Rule
-   **Core View**: Any channel is essentially a flag in the opposite direction.
-   **Up Channel = Bear Flag**: An up channel has **75%** probability of ultimately breaking sideways or down. Only **25%** probability of accelerating upward breakout, entering a new rally phase.
-   **Down Channel = Bull Flag**: Similarly, a down channel has **75%** probability of breaking up.

### Power of Failed Flags: Measuring Gaps (Measured Move)
-   **Surprise Breakout**: When low-probability events (25%) occur, for example, an up channel (bear flag) is strongly broken upward, this traps many traders.
-   **Forming Measuring Gap (MM)**: This surprise strong breakout usually forms a measuring gap, and the market is likely to continue rallying to a **Measured Move target** (usually double the height of the channel or the pre-breakout consolidation range upward).
-   **Trading Implication**: This MM target acts like a "magnet" attracting price, because trapped shorts will cover, and trend-following longs will actively buy.

## Exhaustion Climaxes at Trend Ends

### Identification and Implication
-   **Phenomenon**: At the end of a mature trend that has persisted for a very long time (e.g., more than 100 bars), the **largest buying/selling climax** of the entire move appears.
-   **Interpretation**: This is usually not the start of a new, stronger trend, but more likely a **signal of trend exhaustion**, forming an **Exhaustion Gap**.
-   **Trading Consequence**: Exhaustion gaps are likely to be filled; price usually falls back near the starting point of this climax.

## Trading Strategies After Climaxes

### Wait for Clear Signals: TBTL Rule
-   **Principle**: After a series of consecutive climaxes causes reversal, strong trend traders will not immediately re-enter.
-   **TBTL (Ten Bars, Two Legs)**: They usually wait for the market to experience at least **10 bars, two-legged** pullback or consolidation, confirming selling pressure has weakened, before considering buying with trend again.
-   **Application**: If the first pullback leg is unusually violent (such as a bear microchannel), then longer time and more complex consolidation structure are needed.

### Breakout Mode
-   **20-Bar Rule**: When consolidation after climax lasts more than **20 bars**, the market enters "breakout mode".
-   **50/50 Probability**: At this point, probabilities of original trend resumption and trend reversal become close to **50/50**. Traders should stop fixating on a single direction, waiting for the market to give clear direction through strong breakout bars and good follow-through.

## Summary Principles
-   **Consecutive Climaxes are Exhaustion Signals**: A series of trend bars without meaningful pullbacks usually signals trend momentum is about to exhaust.
-   **Channel = Opposite Flag**: Default up channels are bear flags (75% probability of breaking down), down channels are bull flags (75% probability of breaking up).
-   **Beware Exhaustion Gaps**: The largest climax at trend ends is usually the final frenzy, not a new beginning.
-   **Patiently Wait for TBTL**: After climax reversals, don't rush to buy bottoms or chase highs; patiently wait for at least 10 bars and two-legged adjustment.
-   **Understand Market Psychology**: Climaxes are results of battles between strong and weak players; understanding their behavior patterns is key to predicting price direction.
