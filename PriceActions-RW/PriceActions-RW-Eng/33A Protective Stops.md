# Protective Stops (Part 1)

## Why Protective Stops Are Essential

### Core Purpose: Self-Protection
-   **Concept**: Stop orders are orders preset after entry, with the core purpose of protecting traders.
-   **Protection Targets**:
    -   **External Surprises**: Sudden extreme volatility, network/computer failures, broker or even exchange issues.
    -   **Internal Emotions**: Most importantly, protecting traders from being hurt by their own emotions (such as hope, fear, denial). Beginners tend to hope when losing, unwilling to admit mistakes, leading to expanding losses.

### Enforcing Discipline
-   **Non-Negotiable**: Stops are part of the trading plan; once set, they should not be arbitrarily moved or questioned. They are the last line of defense against emotional decisions.
-   **Accepting Losses**: Losses are a normal part of trading. The purpose of stops is to control losses within acceptable ranges, preventing small losses from evolving into catastrophic large losses.

## Logic of Stop Placement

### Based on Price Action, Not Entry Point
-   **Dynamic Management**: Stop placement should be determined by **current market structure**, not by your entry price.
-   **Important Highs/Lows**: In trends, when market strongly breaks out and forms a new, important swing point (such as an important pullback low in an uptrend), all with-trend traders should move stops to the other side of that swing point.
-   **Trading Premise**: If price touches that important swing point, it means your trading premise at entry (e.g., "market is in an uptrend") may no longer be valid. Exit immediately.

### Different Types of Stops
-   **Price Action Stop**: Set stops based on chart structure, such as signal bar lows, pattern lows (e.g., wedge flags), or recent important swing lows. This is the most common and logical method.
-   **Fixed-Amount/Tick Stop**: Set stops based on fixed ticks (e.g., 10 ticks) or dollar amounts (e.g., $100). This method is somewhat arbitrary and may not align with market structure.
-   **Wide Stop**: Set a very wide stop, typically placed at the entire trend's starting point. Suitable for traders expecting market to enter a trading range or planning to scale in.
-   **Trailing Stop**: As price moves in your favor, continuously move stop to behind new, important swing points to lock in profits.

## Beginner Trader Stop Psychological Traps

### Personifying the Market and Self-Justification
-   **Wrong Perception**: Beginners often view the market as a person who makes mistakes and will "correct itself." When market moves against their expectations, they think the market is wrong and "tolerantly" give the market time to correct.
-   **Finding Excuses**: To hold losing positions, beginners constantly find various reasons to deny the obvious decline (e.g., "this is a bear trap," "this is still a higher low").

### Fear Is the Root Cause
-   **Core Psychology**: Behind all excuses is **fear of admitting losses**. Beginners fear accepting losses because it makes them feel like "losers" and worry their trading dreams will be shattered.
-   **Decision Paralysis**: This fear prevents them from making correct management decisions, preferring to hold on to hope rather than exit decisively.

### Surrendering at the Worst Point
-   **Vicious Cycle**: Beginners constantly find excuses while losing, until losses become unbearable, finally stopping out in panic and despair at the most unfavorable price (typically at the end of a swing).
-   **Professional Perspective**: Ironically, the point where beginners panic out is often where professional traders identify "sell climax" or "buy climax" and prepare to enter in the opposite direction.

## Stops, Risk, and Position Management

### Stop Determines Risk, Risk Determines Position Size
-   **Core Formula**: **Stop distance → Risk per contract → Position size**.
-   **Risk Consistency**: Before trading, first determine the logical stop location. This distance determines your risk. Your goal is to ensure **each trade bears roughly the same total risk amount**.
-   **Position Adjustment**: If a trade signal's stop distance is twice normal, your position size should be halved to maintain constant total risk.

### "Don't Care" Position Size
-   **Trading Mindset**: Traders must use positions small enough that a single loss won't significantly impact emotions. Only with a "don't care" mindset can objective, rational decisions be made.
-   **Risk and Reward**: For high-probability trades, a common goal is to capture profits equal to risk (stop distance) (1:1 risk-reward ratio).

## Summary Principles
-   **Stops Are the Foundation of Discipline**: Their primary purpose is to protect you from emotional decisions and enforce rules.
-   **Stops Based on Market Structure**: Correct stop placement is determined by current price action, not your entry price.
-   **Risk Determines Position Size**: Stop distance directly determines your risk; you must adjust position size accordingly to ensure constant risk per trade.
-   **Accept Small Losses**: Overcome fear of losses. Decisively accepting small, controllable losses is core to professional trading; this is the only way to avoid catastrophic account damage.
