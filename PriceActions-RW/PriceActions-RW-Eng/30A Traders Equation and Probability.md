# Mathematical Foundation of Trading: Mathematical Expectation and Probability

## The Core of Trading: Seeking a Mathematical Edge

### Perfect Trades Don't Exist
-   **Core Concept**: The market does not offer trading opportunities that are perfect in all three aspects: win rate, profit, and risk.
-   **Trader Goal**: Don't pursue perfection, but construct trades to gain a small but sustainable **mathematical edge**.
-   **Profit Foundation**: Consistently applying strategies with mathematical edge over time will accumulate small edges into substantial profits.

### Trade-off Between Win Rate & Reward/Risk
-   Traders must make trade-offs between probability and reward/risk; you cannot have both.
-   **High Win Rate Trades**:
    -   **Characteristics**: Usually enter after trend is clear (such as after strong breakout).
    -   **Cost**: Risk is usually larger (stop is further), potential profit space is smaller, resulting in **poor reward/risk ratio**.
-   **High Reward/Risk Trades**:
    -   **Characteristics**: Usually enter early in trend or at reversal points (such as counter-trend picking tops/bottoms).
    -   **Cost**: Risk is smaller (stop is closer), but success **probability is lower**.

## The Trader's Equation: Mathematical Expectation

### Definition and Formula
-   **Concept**: Before entering, traders must (at least subconsciously) judge whether a trade's mathematical expectation is positive.
-   **Formula**: A reasonable trade must satisfy the following condition:
    -   `(Probability of Profit × Potential Profit) > (Probability of Loss × Potential Risk)`
-   **Variable Analysis**:
    -   **Known Items**: Potential profit (target) and potential risk (stop) can be set by traders.
    -   **Unknown Item**: Probability cannot be precisely known but can be reasonably estimated based on market structure (usually between 40% and 60%).

### Key to Profitability
-   **Positive Expectation**: As long as mathematical expectation is positive, the trading strategy is mathematically sound and will be profitable if executed consistently.
-   **Low Win Rate Strategies Can Also Profit**: Even with win rate below 50% (e.g., 40%), as long as profit when winning far exceeds risk when losing (e.g., reward/risk ratio of 2:1 or higher), the overall result of the trading portfolio is still profitable.
-   **Reality is Often Better**:
    -   Winning trades may far exceed preset targets.
    -   Losing trades may not all hit full stops, exiting with small losses, small profits, or breakeven.

## Market Cycles & Strategy Adaptation

### Cycles of Choppy Action and Trends
-   **Market Nature**: Markets always alternate cyclically between **trading ranges** and **trends**.
-   **Transience of Edge**: Any trading strategy's edge is temporary because market conditions change. For example, strategies of selling rallies and buying dips in trading ranges fail when markets break out into trends.
-   **Strategy Adaptation**: Traders must identify the current market stage and adopt corresponding strategies (such as scalping in ranges vs. swing trading in trends).

### The "Almost Perfect" Trade: The Breakout
-   **Definition**: The initial stage of a strong breakout is the closest to a "perfect trade" in the entire market.
-   **Characteristics**: In early breakout phase, profit probability is high (e.g., 60%), risk is relatively small, potential profit space is large.
-   **Transience**: This opportunity is fleeting. As breakout bars get larger, when everyone sees the opportunity, risk (stop distance) rapidly increases, while remaining profit space decreases, worsening reward/risk ratio.

## Summary Principles
-   **Give Up Perfection, Seek Edge**: The core of trading is not finding perfect entry points, but always constructing trades with **mathematical edge**.
-   **Understand Trader's Equation**: All trading decisions should be based on **positive mathematical expectation**, i.e., expected profit must significantly exceed expected risk.
-   **Balance Win Rate and Reward/Risk**: Choose strategies favoring high win rate or high reward/risk based on personal style, and understand the corresponding costs.
-   **Adapt to Market Cycles**: Recognize that markets cycle between choppy action and trends; any edge is temporary and strategies must be flexibly adjusted.
