# Wedges (Part Four)

## Parabolic Wedges

### Definition and Characteristics
-   **Concept**: A trend with increasing slope (getting steeper), usually in the form of three pushes (3-push), essentially a series of consecutive buying or selling climaxes.
-   **Pattern**:
    -   **Accelerating Movement**: The trendline gets steeper, signaling momentum is about to exhaust.
    -   **Tight Channel**: Although it's a wedge, the channel is usually very narrow, sometimes appearing more like a microchannel.
    -   **Leg Gaps**: In strong parabolic wedges, pullbacks typically don't reach the previous leg's high/low, creating gaps.
    -   **Final Breakout**: The third push (or final push) often accelerates through the channel line, then reverses.

### Trading Implications
-   **Market Overheating Signal**: Indicates the market has moved "too far, too fast" and is unsustainable.
-   **Counter-Trend Trading Opportunity**: Aggressive traders will bet on reversal, but risk is high.
    -   **Early Entry**: Selling below the first reversal signal bar (or buying above), risk/reward is good, but win rate is low.
    -   **Confirmed Entry**: Waiting for a strong reversal bar (such as consecutive bear/bull bars) to appear before entering, win rate is high, but stop is far, risk/reward ratio is poor.
-   **Psychological Battle**: Reversals at parabolic tops usually trap longs who chased the high, forcing them to stop out, which exacerbates the decline.

## Wedge Traps in Strong Trends

### Phenomenon and Misconception
-   **Phenomenon**: In very strong, extremely tight trends (i.e., "microchannels"), seemingly "wedge" three-push structures constantly appear.
-   **Misconception**: Novice traders treat these small structures as reversal signals, continuously attempting counter-trend trades (such as constantly trying to buy the bottom in a strong down microchannel).

### Core Principle (80% Rule)
-   **Rule**: In strong trends, 80% of reversal attempts will fail.
-   **Nature**: These so-called "wedges" are not true reversal patterns, but merely flags in the trend continuation process.
-   **Judgment Criteria**:
    -   **Channel Width**: The channel is too narrow, indicating extremely strong trend force and lack of meaningful counter-trend pressure.
    -   **Higher Timeframe**: On higher timeframe charts, this narrow channel usually appears as just one or two strong breakout bars.

### Correct Strategy
-   **Abandon Counter-Trend**: In narrow channels, absolutely do not consider counter-trend trading.
-   **Trade with Trend**: Treat every pullback as a flag, waiting for the pullback to end and a trend signal to appear before entering (e.g., after a bounce ends in a downtrend, sell below the bear bar).

## Evolution from Climax to Reversal

### Endless Pullback
-   **Concept**: After a strong trend (such as a parabolic climax), a narrow channel pullback that lasts too long (such as more than 20 bars) appears.
-   **Implication**: The original bull flag/bear flag gradually loses momentum, the market enters a balanced state (50/50), and the probability of trend reversal significantly increases.
-   **Trading Strategy**: Patiently wait for this "endless pullback" channel to be clearly broken. Once a strong breakout and good follow-through appear, the end of the original trend and the start of a new trend can be confirmed.

### Identifying Valid Reversals
-   **Prerequisite**: Not all wedges can successfully reverse. Final trend reversals usually require stronger counter-trend signals.
-   **Key Signals**:
    -   **Buying/Selling Pressure**: Strong reversal bars appear (such as full-bodied bull/bear bars), showing real counter-trend force.
    -   **Structure Confirmation**: Wait for a more reliable reversal structure, such as a double top/bottom, or a higher low/lower high after the first strong reversal wave (i.e., High 2/Low 2 signals).
-   **Caution Against Bottom/Top Picking**: In narrow channel trends, counter-trend trading based solely on a small three-push wedge is low-probability behavior. Must wait for clear evidence of significantly weakened trend strength.

## Wedge and Channel Probabilities

### Basic Probabilities
-   **Wedge Top (Up Channel/Bear Flag)**: Approximately **75%** probability of breaking down (at least evolving into a trading range), only **25%** probability of accelerating upward breakout.
-   **Wedge Bottom (Down Channel/Bull Flag)**: Approximately **75%** probability of breaking up, only **25%** probability of accelerating downward break.

### Trading Application
-   **Breakout Doesn't Equal New Trend**: A channel breakout (75% probability event) only indicates the original channel has ended, the market may enter a trading range, not necessarily immediately start an opposite trend.
-   **Focus on Support and Resistance**: Reversal targets are usually prior key highs/lows or Measured Move targets. At these levels, trend traders will take profits, possibly causing pullbacks or consolidation.
-   **Beware Low Probability Events**: Although low probability, when upward/downward acceleration breakouts (25% probability events) occur, they usually trigger very strong trend moves.
