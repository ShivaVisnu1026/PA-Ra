# Breakout Traps & Counter-Trading

## Scalper Traps

### Definition & Cause
-   **Concept**: Market suddenly reverses just before reaching scalpers' profit targets, causing these traders to get trapped.
-   **Cause**: Due to order execution mechanism (like stop orders for entry, limit orders for exit), traders need market to move distance typically 1-2 ticks/pips more than target profit to ensure profit. When market moves distance just insufficient to ensure execution, trap forms.

### Specific Cases
-   **S&P Index Futures (ES) "5-Tick Trap"**
    -   **Background**: Scalpers often target 1 point (4 ticks).
    -   **Mechanism**: To ensure 4-tick profit, market typically needs to move 6 ticks (1 tick entry slippage + 4 ticks profit + 1 tick to ensure limit order execution).
    -   **Trap**: When market moves only 5 ticks then reverses, long traders can't take profit and get trapped, forming "5-Tick Trap." Similarly, 2, 3, 4-point targets may correspond to "9, 13, 17-Tick Traps."
-   **Forex "11-Pip Trap"**
    -   **Background**: Scalpers often target 10 pips.
    -   **Mechanism**: To ensure 10-pip profit, market typically needs to move 12 pips.
    -   **Trap**: When market moves only 11 pips then reverses, traders get trapped, forming "11-Pip Trap."

### Trading Implications
-   **Form Support & Resistance**: These "trap" levels (like 5 ticks above signal bar or 11 pips below) become strong support or resistance areas.
-   **Multiple Forces Converge**: At these levels:
    -   **Trapped Traders**: Forced to panic close (like trapped longs selling), exacerbating reversal.
    -   **Counter-Traders**: Anticipating trap, place limit orders here (like placing short orders at long target level), combining with trapped traders' exit behavior.

## Fading Weak Setups

### Core Concept
-   When a trading setup appears weak in current market context, rather than trading with setup direction, counter-trading (Fading) often has higher success rate.

### Trading Strategy
-   **Shorting Weak Buy Signals**
    -   **Identification**: In strong downtrend, weak buy signal bar with poor formation and strength appears.
    -   **Operation**: Traders can place limit sell orders near weak signal bar's high, betting buy breakout will fail.
    -   **Risk Management**: Usually place stops above downtrend start point, may scale in after price slightly rises.
-   **Longing Weak Sell Signals**
    -   **Identification**: In weak downtrend or potential bottom formation (like three-push wedge bottom), hesitant sell signal bar appears.
    -   **Operation**: Traders can place limit buy orders near weak signal bar's low, betting sell breakout will fail.
    -   **Risk Management**: This is advanced strategy commonly used by experts, usually with scaling; beginners should use cautiously.

## Summary Principles
-   **Targets are Key Areas**: Scalpers' regular profit target levels (like 4 ticks, 10 pips) are natural support and resistance areas in market.
-   **Use "Traps" for Trading**: When market reverses "just short" of key target level, is high-probability trading opportunity because multiple forces jointly push price.
-   **Weak Signals are Strong Counter Signals**: A poor trading signal itself is clue to a good trade—just in opposite direction. Identifying and counter-trading these weak signals is effective professional trading strategy.
