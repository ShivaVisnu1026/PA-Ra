# Reversals (Part 4): Win Rate, Targets, and Double Top/Bottom Structures

## I. Mathematical Expectation and Win Rate Reality of Major Reversal Trading

- **Core Viewpoint: Major reversal win rate is approximately 40%**
  - Al Brooks emphasizes that even well-formed, clearly-signaled **major reversals**—those attempts to reverse current trends and form counter-trends—have only approximately a **40%** direct success probability (i.e., ability to smoothly develop into a substantial swing trade).

- **The Other 60% Possibility**:
  - In the remaining **60%** of cases, such major reversal attempts may evolve into:
    1. A **Trading Range**: Price does not form sustained counter-trend movement after reversal attempt, but consolidates sideways within a range.
    2. **Original Trend Resumes**: Reversal attempt fails, market returns to the prior uptrend or downtrend.
  - This means many times, major reversal trade results may be **small profits, small losses, or roughly break-even**, rather than the expected large profits.

- **Key to Profitability: Positive Mathematical Expectation and Reward/Risk Ratio**
  - Since major reversal win rates are not overwhelming (only about 40%), to achieve long-term profitability through reversal trading strategies, the key is ensuring overall **mathematical expectation is positive**.
  - This requires that **potential reward of a single successful trade must be significantly greater than the initial risk of a single trade**.
  - Al Brooks suggests that for major reversal swing trades, traders should pursue a **minimum reward/risk ratio of at least 2:1** (i.e., target profit at least twice initial risk).
    - **Example (Stock Index Futures, e.g., E-mini S&P 500)**: If initial stop risk for a reversal trade is set at 2 points, target profit should be at least 4 points. Assuming 10 such trades:
      - 4 successes: 4 × 4 points/trade = 16 points profit.
      - 6 failures: 6 × 2 points/trade = 12 points loss (here assuming each failure loses full stop amount; in reality, some failed trades may be small losses or break-even).
      - Net profit: 16 points - 12 points = 4 points.
      - Therefore, even with low win rate, as long as reasonable reward/risk ratios and good trade opportunity selection are maintained, long-term profitability is still possible.
    - **Example (Forex Markets)**: Same logic. If initial risk is 20 pips, target profit should be at least 40 pips.
  - **Actual Mathematical Expectation May Be Better**: The above calculation is a conservative estimate assuming each failure reaches maximum stop. In actual trading, many unsuccessful reversal attempts may end with smaller losses or break-even, so actual profitability of consistently following this strategy may be higher.

- **Trading Implications**:
  - **Patience and Selectivity**: Given major reversal win rate characteristics, traders must be patient enough to wait for **high-quality reversal opportunities with excellent patterns and signals**, rather than recklessly entering at every seemingly reversal sign.
  - **Discipline and Consistency**: Adhering to established risk management principles (such as fixed initial stops) and reward/risk targets is crucial.

## II. Minimum Swing Target for Major Reversal Trades: "Two Legs, Ten Bars" (TBTL)

- When Al Brooks evaluates and decides to take a major reversal swing trade, his **minimum expected target** for trade success is usually: the market can at least produce **two effective, clear counter-trend legs, with each leg lasting approximately 10 bars** (TBTL: Ten Bars, Two Legs).
- This is his basic magnitude and duration requirement for a reversal move worth participating in.

## III. Common Feature in Reversal Patterns: Containing Double Top/Bottom Structures

- **Core Observation: Most major reversal patterns, within their structures, usually contain or can be identified as some form of "Double Top" or "Double Bottom" elements.**

- **Specific Manifestations**:
  1. **Wedge Reversals**:
     - A typical **Wedge Top** usually consists of three upward pushes. The peaks of these three pushes (especially the second and third, or first and third) can be viewed as components of a (possibly slightly sloped or imperfect) **Double Top**.
     - Similarly, a **Wedge Bottom** often contains traces of **Double Bottom** structure.
  2. **Higher High Major Reversal / Lower Low Major Reversal**:
     - These patterns themselves are variants or extensions of double tops/bottoms. For example, a "Higher High Major Reversal" means the market makes a new high (second top higher than first) but fails to continue upward, instead falls sharply and effectively breaks prior support, forming a reversal. This is essentially a "failed higher high" double top structure.
     - Similarly, "Lower Low Major Reversal" is a "failed lower low" double bottom structure.
     - **Expanding Triangle Top** is also a special form of higher high major reversal, with peaks gradually rising and troughs gradually falling, eventually breaking down.

- **Trading Significance**:
  - **Signal Confirmation and Enhancement**: When analyzing a potential reversal pattern (such as a wedge), if double top/bottom substructures can be identified within it logically, and these substructures are accompanied by clear entry signal bars (e.g., strong bearish engulfing bar at the second top of a small double top), this **enhances overall reversal signal reliability**.
  - **Assisting Entry Decisions**: Traders can use neckline breaks of these internally formed small-level double tops/bottoms, or signal bars formed at their second tops/bottoms, as more precise entry points.
  - **Understanding Market Behavior Depth**: Recognizing this pattern nesting helps understand the details of bullish-bearish contest and power transformation during reversal processes more deeply.

## IV. Summary: Patience, Selectivity, and Mathematical Expectation Are the Foundation of Successful Reversal Trading

- Reversal trading (especially major reversals) is not a high-win-rate strategy, but through strict risk management, reasonable reward/risk targets, and patient selection of high-quality trading opportunities, positive mathematical expectation can be achieved long-term.
- Understanding common structural features in reversal patterns (such as contained double tops/bottoms) can help traders better grasp entry timing and improve judgment accuracy.
