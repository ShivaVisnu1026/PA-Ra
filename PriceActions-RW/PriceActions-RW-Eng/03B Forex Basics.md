# Forex Trading Fundamentals (Part 2)

## Trading Costs & Broker Selection

-   **Profit Models**: Brokers primarily profit through two methods:
    -   **Spread**: The difference between bid and ask prices. This is the main revenue source for commission-free platforms.
    -   **Commission**: Fixed fees charged in addition to the spread.
-   **Cost Impact**: Excessive trading costs (like 2-3 pip wide spreads) make profitability very difficult.
-   **Broker Comparison**:
    -   **Commission-Free Platforms**: Typically have wider spreads with higher hidden costs.
    -   **Commission-Based Platforms** (like IB mentioned in videos): May offer extremely tight spreads (like 0.5 pips), and even with commissions added, total trading costs may actually be lower.
-   **Core Recommendation**: Choose reputable, large, well-known brokers as they typically offer lower total trading costs, making profitability more achievable.

## Currency Pairs & Terminology

-   **Currency Pair Structure**:
    -   **Base Currency**: The first currency in the pair (like **EUR** in EUR/USD), the actual object being traded (bought or sold).
    -   **Quote Currency**: The second currency (like **USD** in EUR/USD), used to price the base currency.
-   **Trading Meaning**:
    -   **Going Long EUR/USD**: Means buying euros while simultaneously selling dollars, expecting the euro to appreciate against the dollar.
    -   **Going Short EUR/JPY**: Means selling euros while simultaneously buying yen, expecting the euro to depreciate against the yen.
-   **Professional Terminology**: Use standard currency codes, avoid jargon like "Cable" or "Gopher" as professional traders rarely use them.

## Market Risks & Integrity Issues

-   **Historical Issues**: In the past, large banks and companies had risks of market manipulation using the "London 4 PM Fix" window. They conspired to set artificially high or low prices within a short time for profit.
-   **Current Status**: With strengthened regulation and extended fixing windows (from 1 minute to 5 minutes), such manipulation has significantly decreased. Current market integrity is high; ordinary traders need not worry excessively about such systemic cheating risks.

## Price Quotes: Pips & Fractional Pips

-   **Definition**: A "Pip" is the smallest standard price movement unit in forex, similar to a "Tick" in stock markets.
-   **Standard**:
    -   **Most Currency Pairs** (like EUR/USD): Pip is the **4th decimal place**.
    -   **Yen-Related Pairs** (like USD/JPY): Pip is the **2nd decimal place**.
-   **Fractional Pips**: Modern platforms provide more precise quotes by adding extra decimals after pips (like the 5th or 3rd place). However, these change too rapidly and have little significance for trading decisions; they can be ignored.

## Trading Performance Measurement Standards

-   **Common Language**: Traders habitually discuss profits and losses in "pips" rather than specific amounts (e.g., "made 25 pips today").
-   **Main Purposes**:
    -   **Privacy Protection**: Avoid revealing personal account size and specific profit/loss amounts.
    -   **Standardized Comparison**: Provides a universal standard for measuring trading skill, unaffected by position size.
