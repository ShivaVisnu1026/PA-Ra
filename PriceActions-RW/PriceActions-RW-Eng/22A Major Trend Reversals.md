# Major Trend Reversals (Part 1)

## Minor vs. Major Reversals

### Definition and Distinction
- **Minor Reversal**: Brief counter-trend movement in a trend, usually considered a flag or evolving into a trading range. In most cases, the original trend continues. Traders typically only scalp.
- **Major Reversal**: A pattern with sufficient strength to potentially reverse the current trend into an opposite trend or at least an opposite swing. This is the most important reversal pattern, providing swing trading opportunities.

### Key Characteristics
- **Number of Bars**: Major reversal structures usually consist of enough bars (such as 20 or more). Patterns with few bars (such as small head and shoulders) are usually only minor reversals.
- **Prerequisite**: Usually begins with a powerful trend line break, followed by the original trend attempting to resume but failing. This is a key signal of major reversal.

## Trading Logic and Mathematical Expectation of Major Reversals (Trading Logic & Trader's Equation)

### Probability and Reward/Risk Ratio
- **Success Rate**: A reasonable major reversal entry point has approximately a **40%** probability of successfully evolving into a counter-trend swing, i.e., profit at least twice the risk.
- **Failure Rate**: Approximately **60%** probability that the market will enter a trading range or resume the original trend. In these cases, traders can usually exit with small profits, small losses, or break-even.
- **Trader's Equation**: Although the win rate is only 40%, extremely high reward/risk ratios (≥ 2:1, or even higher) make the mathematical expectation of such trades positive, one of the core profit models for professional traders.

## Entry Strategies for Major Reversals

### Strategy One: Early Entry (Low Win Rate, High Reward/Risk)
- **Timing**: Enter when the major reversal pattern has just formed, trend reversal not yet confirmed (e.g., buying above signal bar of higher low reversal).
- **Advantages**: Stop close, small risk, extremely high potential reward/risk ratio.
- **Disadvantages**: Lower win rate (approximately 40%), because trading "possibility of reversal" rather than "confirmed reversal".

### Strategy Two: Entry After Confirmation (High Win Rate, Low Reward/Risk)
- **Timing**: Wait for the market to show strong, well-followed breakout bars (strong bull/bear bars), then enter in the breakout direction.
- **Advantages**: Higher win rate (over 60%), because reversal has been confirmed by the market.
- **Disadvantages**: Stop farther (usually below the start of the breakout move), larger risk, causing reward/risk ratio to decline. This is the cost of trading high risk for high win rate.

## Common Major Reversal Patterns

- **Lower High / Higher Low Major Reversal**: The most common pattern. After trend line break, market fails to make new highs/lows, forming lower highs or higher lows.
- **Higher High / Lower Low Major Reversal**: Market makes new highs/lows first, then suddenly reverses sharply. Often appears as wedge tops or expanding triangles.
- **Head and Shoulders Top/Bottom**: Large head and shoulders patterns are typical major reversals. The right shoulder is essentially a lower high / higher low major reversal.
- **Transition via Trading Range**: Most trend reversals will experience a trading range. The confusion caused by "large rallies, large declines" is typical of trading ranges. Traders should look for two-way trading opportunities within ranges.
