# Breakouts and Traps (Part 4)

## Second Leg Trap

### Definition & Identification
-   **Concept**: After market completes one leg, following pullback/rally, forms an apparently very strong second leg that seems to continue trend but ultimately fails and reverses.
-   **Common Context**: Extremely common in **Trading Ranges** or wide channels.
-   **Purpose**: Lures traders into mistakenly thinking trend is starting, thus chasing long at highs or short at lows, ultimately getting trapped.
-   **Identification Signals**:
    -   **Strong Appearance**: Second leg usually consists of one or two large trend bars, looking like clear breakout intent.
    -   **Key Failure**: After breakout lacks strong **Follow-through**; instead quickly shows strong reversal bar.

### Trading Implications
-   **Maintain Skepticism**: When seeing seemingly strong breakout within trading range, first suspect it may be second leg trap.
-   **Counter-Trade**: Once trap identified (i.e., poor follow-through and strong reversal signal after breakout), consider counter-trading (Fading), i.e., sell at top, buy at bottom.
-   **Watch Context**: "Look left" is crucial. If left side shows clear trading range, any breakout failure probability is high.

## Judging Breakout Success or Failure

### Core Concept
-   **Probability Thinking**: Market has no 100% certainty; most situations have 40%-60% probability. Must always prepare for unexpected situations.
-   **Market Nature**: Market searches for fair value through constant breakout attempts. Therefore, judging whether breakouts will succeed or fail is the foundation of all trading decisions (entry, stops, targets).
-   **Failure is Normal**: Every trend ultimately ends with a failed breakout. Long tails on bars themselves are mini, failed breakouts.

### Characteristics of Strong Breakouts
-   **Continuity & Strength**: Appears consecutive bars with full bodies, closes near extremes.
-   **Urgency**: Gaps appear (e.g., bull bar open higher than prior bar close), showing buyers/sellers very urgent, unwilling to wait for pullbacks.
-   **Weak First Pullback**: After one strong breakout, first pullback/rally typically weak; high probability just a flag (bull flag/bear flag), signal of trend continuation rather than reversal.

### Signals of Weak Breakouts (Failures)
-   **Surprise Bar**: One huge counter-direction bar completely swallows prior breakout effort, instantly trapping with-trend traders.
-   **Poor Follow-through**: After strong breakout bar, immediately followed by counter-direction bar or weak doji, showing momentum failed to continue.
-   **Trapped Traders**: When surprise reversal occurs, with-trend traders get trapped in, while counter-trend traders waiting for pullback get trapped out; this combined force typically pushes price to continue in reversal direction.

## Trading Strategy in Strong Trends

### Multiple Entry Methods
-   **Market Order**: When trend strength and urgency very obvious, directly enter at market, pursuing timeliness.
-   **On Close Entry**: Enter at moment of strong trend bar close. Can even enter on weak counter bar close, betting reversal attempt will fail.
-   **Stop Order**: Place orders beyond extremes (highs/lows) of pullback bars, for capturing moment of trend resumption.
-   **Limit Order**: During trend movement, place limit orders at pullback levels, betting small-scale reversal attempts will fail (i.e., buy the pullback).

### Core Mindset
-   **Assume Trend Continuation**: In confirmed strong trends, default any pullback to just "flag," entry opportunity rather than exit signal.
-   **Always Ready**: Must be proactive, constantly adjusting orders based on new bars, always ready to enter.

## Summary Principles
-   **Beware Second Leg Traps**: Especially in trading ranges, seemingly perfect breakouts are often traps.
-   **Breakouts Look at Context, Continuation Looks at Follow-through**: Breakout success rate extremely depends on market context it appears in; follow-through bar after breakout is key to verifying success or failure.
-   **First Pullback in Strong Trends is High-Probability Opportunity**: First pullback after strong breakout, high probability will fail, providing excellent entry point for with-trend traders.
-   **Flexibly Use Multiple Entry Methods**: Based on trend strength and urgency, flexibly choose market orders, on-close, stop orders, or limit orders to enter.
