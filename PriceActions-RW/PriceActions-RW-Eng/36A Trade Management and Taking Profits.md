# Trade Management and Profit-Taking (Part 1)

## Core Principles of Trade Management

### Definition and Premise
-   **Definition**: A series of operations on positions after entry.
-   **Core Principle**: The only basis for trade management is whether **current price action** still supports your **premise** at entry.
    -   **Premise Valid**: If price action matches expectations, follow original plan.
    -   **Premise Invalid**: If strong counter-trend signals appear, causing original trading premise to no longer be valid, then **must exit**, whether currently profitable, losing, or breakeven.

### Trade Management Is Independent of Entry Point
-   **Unified Decision**: For the same trend, regardless of where traders enter (early or late), when a signal strong enough to reverse the trend appears, all with-trend traders' exit decisions should be the same.
-   **Basis**: Management decisions should only be based on stops, targets, and premise, unrelated to specific entry price.

## Reasonable Risk and Reward Management

### Decisions Based on Mathematical Expectation
-   **Mathematical Expectation**: Any trading decision should be based on positive mathematical expectation, i.e., `(win rate × potential profit) > (loss rate × potential risk)`.
-   **Default Profit-Taking Strategy**: When uncertain, a mathematically reasonable default strategy is to take profit at **twice initial risk (2R)**. Even with only 40% win rate, long-term adherence can be profitable.

### Initial Stop and Trailing Stop
-   **Initial Stop**: Should be placed at key location that can **invalidate trading premise**, typically important swing starting points, not minor swing points.
-   **Trailing Stop**: As trend develops, should move stop to beyond new, important higher lows (in uptrends) or lower highs (in downtrends) to protect profits and reduce risk.

### Scaling Out
-   **Purpose**: When trend has moved a long distance, compared to current price, reasonable stop location (e.g., latest major swing low) may be very far, causing excessive floating profit retracement risk. Should actively reduce risk by **reducing position size (i.e., partial profit-taking)**.
-   **Institutional Behavior**: Institutions typically scale out at **measured move targets** or other key resistance levels; their collective selling is the main cause of market pullbacks.

## Trading Psychology and Common Misconceptions

### "Other People's Money" Fallacy
-   **Concept**: A wrong belief that floating profits (unrealized profit) in account don't count as your own money, so can tolerate large retracements or even complete disappearance.
-   **Correct Understanding**: As long as funds appear in account (whether principal or floating profit), it **is your money**. You must manage and protect profits like protecting principal. Viewing floating profits as "other people's money" is "foolish" and leads to irrational holding behavior.
-   **Reverse Logic**: Traders never view floating losses as "other people's money" because losses are real. Similarly, floating profits should be viewed as real.

### "Forward-Looking" Mindset
-   **Accept Losses**: Losses are an inevitable part of trading; even traders with 90% win rates will have losses.
-   **Don't Dwell on Past**: Don't obsess over previous trade's loss or profit. Excessive emotion distracts you, causing you to miss the next excellent trading opportunity. Missing one great opportunity may cost far more than a previous small loss.

## Summary Principles
-   **Premise Is Key**: Core of trade management is judging whether holding premise is still valid. Once premise fails, exit immediately.
-   **Management Independent of Entry**: Your exit decisions should be based on current price action, not your entry point or current profit/loss status.
-   **Floating Profit Is Your Money**: Firmly reject "other people's money" misconception; strictly manage floating profits like protecting principal.
-   **Dynamic Risk Adjustment**: Dynamically manage risk and protect profits through trailing stops and scaling out at key levels.
-   **Focus on Present, Look to Future**: Accept losses, don't dwell on past, always ready to seize next opportunity that fits trading system.
