# Types of Trends, Traps & Breakouts (Part 3)

## Two Basic Phases of Trends: Breakout vs. Channel

### Breakout
-   **Concept**: Strongest trend formation, showing rapid price movement. All trends begin with some form of breakout.
-   **Characteristics**:
    -   **Bar Formation**: Composed of series of strong trend bars (large bodies, small tails).
    -   **Urgency**: Little overlap between bars, even gaps appear, showing urgency and dominance of one market side.
    -   **Time Dimension**: Breakout can also be "breakout in time," i.e., many small bars slowly and continuously forming a narrow channel over long period (like 20-30 bars).

### Channel
-   **Concept**: Weaker trend formation with two-way trading opportunities; both bulls and bears active within certain range.
-   **Characteristics**: Price oscillates between two roughly parallel trend lines; common trend development stage after breakout (Spike), forming "Spike and Channel" pattern.

## Trading Traps & Novice Mistakes

### Novice Trap: Counter-Trend Trading
-   **Behavior Pattern**: Newbies tend to counter-trend in strong trends (e.g., repeatedly trying to short in strong uptrend).
-   **Psychological Reason**: Only focus on **risk** (counter-trend stops seem small and close) while completely ignoring **probability** (success probability extremely low).
-   **Consequence**: Though single risk seems controllable, frequent low-probability trades lead to sustained small losses, ultimately accumulating into huge losses, and missing great with-trend trading opportunities. This is typical "trapped" situation.

### Professional Trader Response
-   **Core Thinking**: Balance three variables of **Trader's Equation**: risk, reward, and **probability**.
-   **Trading Strategy**: In strong trends, professional traders will:
    -   **Accept Imperfect Signals**: With-trend entry signal bars in strong trends often poorly formed, but they know with-trend probability is higher.
    -   **Use Correct Stops**: Place stops below true start point of trend leg, even if this means stops farther away.
    -   **Adjust Position Size**: Manage increased single-trade risk from wider stops by using **smaller position size**.

## Channel Breakout Probabilities

### Bull Channels are Bear Flags
-   **Rule**: Every (wide) bull channel should be viewed as a **bear flag**.
-   **Probability**: It has **75%** probability of eventually being **broken downward**, after which typically evolves into trading range or complete reversal.

### Bear Channels are Bull Flags
-   **Rule**: Every (wide) bear channel should be viewed as a **bull flag**.
-   **Probability**: It has **75%** probability of eventually being **broken upward** and testing channel start point, after which also often evolves into trading range.

## Summary Principles
-   **Identify Trend Phases**: Understand trends consist of strong **breakout** phase and weaker **channel** phase; most common pattern is "Spike and Channel."
-   **Avoid Novice Traps**: Never counter-trend in strong trends because "stop is close." This behavior of only seeing risk while ignoring probability is the root of losses.
-   **Adopt Professional Strategy**: When trading with-trend in strong trends, dare to accept imperfect signals, use correct (typically wider) stops, and control risk through **small position size**.
-   **Apply Channel Rules**: (Wide) bull channels are bear flags; (wide) bear channels are bull flags; they likely break in opposite direction of formation.
