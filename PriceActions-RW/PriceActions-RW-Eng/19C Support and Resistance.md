# Support and Resistance (Part 3)

## Multi-Timeframe Resonance and Key Time Node Support/Resistance
- **Core Idea**: Key price levels on higher timeframes (such as monthly, weekly) will have significant impact on intraday trading on smaller timeframes (such as 5-minute charts) at specific time nodes (such as the last day of the month, the last trading hour of Friday), forming "magnetic effects" or becoming important battle points.
- **Key Price Level Types**:
  - **Monthly Bar Related**: Last month's high, low, open, close; this month's open, current high/low.
  - **Weekly Bar Related**: Last week's high, low, open, close; this week's open, current high/low.
- **Trading Application**:
  - When doing intraday trading at these key time nodes, actively check higher timeframe charts to identify if such potential support/resistance exists nearby.
  - Observe specific price action (such as reversals, breakouts, consolidation) on small timeframe charts when price approaches these large timeframe key levels.
  - **Example 1 (ES Index Futures - Month-End Effect)**: On the last trading day of the month, 5-minute chart price repeatedly battles around monthly-level prices such as "this month's open", "last month's close", "last month's high", which become important intraday support or resistance.
  - **Example 2 (EUR/USD - Month-End Effect)**: On the last day of the month, 5-minute chart price may consolidate around "last month's low" for a long time, eventually choosing a breakout direction, showing the strength of monthly-level support and the market's attention to it.

## Detailed Explanation of Common Support and Resistance Types

### 1. Previous Trading Ranges
- **Support Turns to Resistance**: When price breaks below the bottom of a previous trading range (original support area), that bottom may transform into resistance in subsequent bounces.
- **Resistance Turns to Support**: When price breaks above the top of a previous trading range (original resistance area), that top may transform into support in subsequent pullbacks.

### 2. Moving Averages
- Moving averages represent average price over a period and are dynamic support and resistance.
- **EMA20 (20-Period Exponential Moving Average)**: Commonly used for trend following on smaller timeframes (such as 5-minute, 4-hour charts). Price pullbacks to near EMA20 are often opportunities to enter in the trend direction.
- **SMA (Simple Moving Average)**: On daily charts, long-term traders and institutions often focus on MA50, MA100, MA150, MA200.
  - In uptrends, price pullbacks to near these MAs may find support.
  - In downtrends, price bounces to near these MAs may encounter resistance.

### 3. Round Numbers
- **Source of Importance**:
  - **Psychological Factor**: People tend to focus on round numbers.
  - **Order Placement**: Many traders place stop orders, profit targets, or limit orders near round numbers.
  - **Option Strike Prices**: Many option strike prices are themselves round numbers (or specific values close to round numbers).
- **"Magnet Effect"**: Price is often attracted to important round numbers and reacts near them.
- **Round Number Types in Different Markets**:
  - **Stock Index Futures (e.g., ES)**: Round numbers at 10-point intervals (e.g., 2090, 2100, 2110) and hundred-point milestones (e.g., 2000, 2100). These often correspond to option strike prices of ETFs like SPY (ES price ≈ SPY price × 10).
  - **Stocks (e.g., Apple AAPL)**: When stock price approaches $100, $100 itself becomes an extremely important psychological and technical level. After breaking through, $100 may transform from resistance to support.
  - **Commodity Futures (e.g., Crude Oil CL)**: Large round numbers in $10 units (e.g., $40, $50). "Handle" concept, such as price falling from above $40 to the $30+ area, is called entering the "30 handle".
  - **Forex (e.g., USD/CHF, EUR/GBP)**: Specific round numbers (e.g., 0.9000, 0.9100, 0.8000, 0.7000) or round number levels every 100 pips.
- **Impact of Option Sellers**: Institutions, as major option sellers, want options to expire worthless. Therefore, as option expiration approaches, they may trade the underlying asset (stocks, futures, forex, etc.) to "fix" price near strike prices that would cause the maximum number of options (both calls and puts) to expire worthless, further strengthening the role of round numbers as support/resistance.

## A More Comprehensive Support and Resistance List
- Trend Lines and Channel Lines
- Prior Highs and Prior Lows
- Measured Move Targets (MM)
- Moving Averages - any type (EMA, SMA) and any period validated as effective by the market
- Bar Pattern Related:
  - In uptrends, the low of the **entry bar** for longs (is support)
  - In downtrends, the high of the **entry bar** for shorts (is resistance)
  - The high of a **signal bar** for longs (is the entry trigger point, after breaking through it becomes minor support)
  - The low of a **signal bar** for shorts (is the entry trigger point, after breaking through it becomes minor resistance)
- Entry Price Itself - Due to average cost effect, traders may scale in or exit near the initial entry point.
- The **Closing Price** of any leg high or low.
- Scalpers' Profit Targets.
- Intraday Key Levels: Today's high/low/open/close; Yesterday's high/low/open/close.
- Key Levels of Weekly/Monthly/Yearly Bars (high/low/open/close) - Especially near the close of the corresponding period.
- Intraday Pivots - (Al Brooks himself doesn't use this much)
- Major Round Numbers - Such as Dow Jones 20,000 points.
- Fibonacci Retracements & Extensions - Al Brooks emphasizes that 50% retracement and 100% extension (i.e., MM target) are very important and common, and is reserved about other Fibonacci levels.
- Various "Band" Indicators - Such as Bollinger Bands.

**Core Summary of This Section:**
1. Key price levels on higher timeframes (such as monthly open) can influence small timeframe charts at specific times (such as month-end).
2. Moving averages and round numbers (including option-related) are very important sources of support and resistance.
3. There are many types of support and resistance. Traders should learn to identify and judge comprehensively.
