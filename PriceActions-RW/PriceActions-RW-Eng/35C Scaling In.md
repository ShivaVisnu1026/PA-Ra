# Scaling In Strategy Examples

## Example Strategy One: Single Addition (Total Two Entries)

### Strategy Description
-   **Concept**: Divide total position in half. Enter with half first; if market moves unfavorably, add the other half.
-   **Application Scenario**: Using ES futures as example, planning to trade 2 contracts. Enter with 1 contract first; if price pulls back specified distance (e.g., 1 point), add 1 contract.
-   **Ideal Situation**: In strong trends, market may move directly in your favor after first entry; no need to add, can directly take profit or hold for larger swing.

### Trading Logic and Risk
-   **Worst Case and Response**: Assume buying 1 contract at highest point of uptrend, then market pulls back. Trader will add 1 contract at preset lower position (e.g., 1 point lower).
-   **Poor Risk-Reward**: This operation results in initial risk far exceeding potential profit. For example, total risk might be 32 ticks, while target profit is only 4 ticks (i.e., letting added portion profit 1 point, initial position breakeven).
-   **High Win Rate Support**:
    -   **Trend Inertia**: In strong trends, first pullback is likely just a small pullback, not a reversal. Market has high probability of retesting prior highs.
    -   **Psychological Advantage**: Professional traders believe that even buying at the highest point, they have high probability of exiting breakeven. This high win rate compensates for poor risk-reward.

### Trade Management
-   **Adjust Target Due to Disappointment**: If pullback depth or duration exceeds expectations, traders may feel "disappointed" about trend strength.
-   **From Profit to Breakeven**: At this point, trading target may shift from "capturing swing" to "exiting all at initial entry price." This ensures initial position breakeven while added portion profits.

## Example Strategy Two: Multiple/Pyramid Scaling In (Position Divided into Four)

### Strategy Description
-   **Concept**: Divide total position into four equal parts, entering in three stages.
-   **Execution**: Using 400K forex position as example.
    1.  **First Entry**: Buy 1/4 position (100K).
    2.  **First Addition**: If price pulls back, add 1/4 position (100K).
    3.  **Second Addition**: If price continues pulling back, add remaining 2/4 position (200K) at key signal point (e.g., when bull bar appears).

### Trading Logic and Management
-   **Scaling In Logic**: First addition can be at fixed distance (e.g., 10 pips), but subsequent additions (especially heavier positions) are best when clear price action signals appear (e.g., adding above bull bar), with sufficient distance from previous entry (e.g., 2-3 times scalp target).
-   **Exit Strategy**: Similar to Strategy One, if pullback depth is disappointing, traders will choose to exit all near initial entry price.
-   **Profit Settlement**: Result of this operation: initial position breakeven, first and second additions both profit. Since second addition is double size, its profit contribution is also doubled.

## Core Principles and Warnings for Scaling In Trades

### Purpose and Cost
-   **Core Purpose**: **Improve win rate**. By adding at better prices, dramatically increases probability of entire trade ultimately being breakeven or profitable.
-   **Heavy Cost**: **Extremely poor initial risk-reward**. Traders must accept trading huge potential risk for small but high-probability profits.

### Trader Psychology and Position Management
-   **Not Suitable for Beginners**: Scaling in is an **advanced technique** requiring extensive experience, strong emotional control, and flexible trade management ability. Beginners easily emotionally collapse under pressure of increasing floating losses, making wrong decisions.
-   **Position Must Be Small Enough**: This is the **absolute prerequisite** for scaling in success. Traders must use positions far smaller than their normal size (e.g., only half of comfortable position) to remain objective and calm when market moves toward stop.
-   **Correct Decision Basis**: Beginners often focus only on "risk" due to fear, thus panic selling at worst times. Professional traders comprehensively consider **"risk, profit, probability"** three elements to manage trades.

### Flexible Trade Management
-   **Active Risk Control**: Professionals rarely let price hit initially set "disaster" stops; they exit early based on intraday changes.
-   **Dynamic Target Adjustment**: Flexibly adjust exit targets based on market's response to entry—from pursuing swing profits, to breakeven exit, to small profit.
-   **Improve Risk-Reward**: Although single scaling in trade's risk-reward may be poor, professional traders can significantly improve account's overall profit-loss situation by holding partial positions for large swing profits.
