# Introduction to Trends

## Definition & Channels

### Basic Concepts
-   **Definition**: Trend is a state where price primarily moves continuously in one direction, for example from bottom-left to top-right (uptrend) or from top-left to bottom-right (downtrend).
-   **Major Trend vs. Swing**: Instructor typically uses "trend" to refer to **major trends** that last longer and have clear direction on chart. Shorter, secondary moves counter to or aligned with main trend are called "swings" or "legs."
-   **Channel**: All trends run within some kind of channel. Channels consist of two lines:
    -   **Trend Line**: Line connecting lows in uptrend; line connecting highs in downtrend.
    -   **Trend Channel Line**: Another line roughly parallel to trend line, forming channel boundary.

## Uptrends: Higher Lows

### Core Characteristics & Stop Management
-   **Core Characteristic**: Higher lows are the most critical feature of uptrends; more important than higher highs because they determine trend validity and trader stop placement.
-   **Major Swing Low**: A low after which a **strong bull breakout** appears and **makes new high** becomes a "major swing low." This is the **correct location** for swing traders and scalpers to place stops.
-   **Minor Swing Low**: A low after which no strong breakout appears or fails to make new high. Placing stops below this is **wrong** because market likely continues up after breaking below it, causing unnecessary stops.
-   **Trend Change Signals**:
    -   **Breaking Below Minor Low**: Trend hasn't ended but may weaken, showing as channel becoming wider and more gradual.
    -   **Breaking Below Major Low**: Uptrend premise invalid; market likely has transformed into **trading range**, or in rare cases directly reversed into downtrend.

## Downtrends: Lower Highs

### Core Characteristics & Stop Management
-   **Core Characteristic**: Lower highs are the decisive feature of downtrends; basis for bear stop placement.
-   **Major Swing High**: A high after which a **strong bear breakout** appears and **makes new low** becomes a "major swing high." This is the **correct location** for bears to place stops.
-   **Minor Swing High**: A high after which no strong bear breakout appears. Placing stops above this is easily swept before market resumes decline.
-   **Trend Change Signals**:
    -   **Breaking Above Minor High**: Downtrend may be weakening; channel widens and flattens.
    -   **Breaking Above Major High**: Downtrend premise invalid; market likely has entered **trading range**.

## Trading Strategies Within Trends

### With-Trend Trading
-   **In Uptrends**: Bulls look for buy opportunities at pullbacks to trend line or middle area of prior rally leg. They expect bear reversal attempts to fail and view these failed attempts as scaling-in signals.
-   **In Downtrends**: Bears look for short opportunities at rallies to trend channel line, betting bull rallies will fail and evolve into bear flags.

### Channel Behavior
-   **Channel Breakout Failures**: When price accelerates breaking a **tight channel** (selling climax or buying climax), there's up to 75% probability it will fail and bounce/pullback to other side of channel or enter trading range. This is usually a sign of trend exhaustion rather than start of new trend.
-   **Dynamic Adjustment**: Trend lines and channel lines are rarely perfect; traders need to dynamically adjust lines as new bars appear to better reflect current market structure.

## Summary Principles
-   **Highs/Lows are Key**: Uptrend's life force lies in holding important higher lows; downtrend in holding important lower highs.
-   **Distinguish Major from Minor**: Correctly identifying **major** swing highs/lows is crucial for setting effective stops that won't be triggered by market noise.
-   **Trend End is Usually Range**: Strong trends rarely directly V-reverse. When trend's key highs/lows are broken, market most likely enters **trading range**, providing bulls and bears a stage to rebalance forces.
-   **Channels are Behavior Guide**: Use channels to identify high-probability trading zones (trade with trend at channel edges) and be alert to reversal risks from trend acceleration (channel breakouts).
