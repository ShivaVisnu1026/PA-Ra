# Double Tops and Bottoms (Part Two)

## Double Tops/Bottoms as Tests of Prior Highs/Lows

### Core Concept: Test and Reversal
-   **Testing Support (Double Bottom)**: After a decline, the market usually needs to **test the prior low** (support level) again before forming an effective upward reversal. When price reaches this area a second time, if buying emerges, a **double bottom** forms, confirming the effectiveness of support.
-   **Testing Resistance (Double Top)**: Similarly, after a rally, the market usually needs to **test the prior high** (resistance level) again. If selling appears again, a **double top** forms, confirming the effectiveness of resistance.
-   **Reversal Logic**: When the market **attempts twice** to break a certain level (up or down) but both fail, it usually moves in the opposite direction, creating reversal trading opportunities.

### Trader Psychology and Behavior
-   **Double Bottom (Bullish Psychology)**:
    -   In a downtrend, the first bounce is usually minor.
    -   On the second test of the low, early shorts who are trapped will take the opportunity to **cover (buy)** to exit without loss.
    -   Smart longs see this as a high-probability **second entry opportunity** and will buy here.
    -   The convergence of buying (short covering + long entries) pushes price up.
-   **Double Top (Bearish Psychology)**:
    -   In an uptrend, the first pullback is usually minor.
    -   On the second test of the high, longs who chased the high and are trapped will take the opportunity to **sell** to exit without loss.
    -   Smart shorts see this as a high-probability shorting opportunity and will sell here.
    -   The convergence of selling (long exits + short entries) pushes price down.

## Failed Reversals and Measured Moves

### Failed Double Tops/Bottoms
-   **Concept**: Any pattern can fail. When a pattern that should reverse instead breaks in the original trend direction, it is called a failed reversal.
-   **Failed Double Top (Upward Breakout)**: When a double top pattern does not break down below the neckline but instead **breaks up above the prior high**, it becomes a strong **bullish signal**.
-   **Failed Double Bottom (Downward Break)**: When a double bottom pattern does not break up above the neckline but instead **breaks down below the prior low**, it becomes a strong **bearish signal**.
-   **Hidden Failure**: Sometimes, even without forming a perfect double bottom pattern, if a strong rally leg is completely reversed and breaks below its starting point, this can also be viewed as a "failed double bottom attempt", signaling further decline.

### Application of Measured Moves (MM)
-   **Calculation Method**: Measure the pattern's height (vertical distance from high/low to neckline), then extend the same height from the breakout point in the breakout direction to get the **Measured Move target (MM)**.
-   **Universal Application**: This method applies to both **successful reversals** (e.g., double top breaking down below neckline) and **failed reversals** (e.g., double top breaking up above high).
-   **Trading Significance**: MM targets are extremely important **magnetic areas**, usually key locations for traders to set **profit targets**.

## Trading Range Perspective

### Identifying Trading Ranges
-   **Time Standard**: When a pullback lasts **more than 20 bars**, it's best to treat it as a **trading range** rather than a simple pullback.
-   **Probability Change**: In a trading range, the probabilities of trend resumption and trend reversal are nearly equal (approximately 50/50), with balanced bull and bear forces.
-   **Characteristics**: Trading ranges usually contain both reasonable long signals (such as double bottoms) and short signals (such as double tops).

### Breakouts Within Trading Ranges
-   **Failure is Normal**: **Most breakout attempts within trading ranges fail**.
-   **Opposite Movement**: A **false breakout** in one direction often triggers strong movement toward the other side of the range. For example, after a false upward breakout of a double top fails, price is likely to quickly drop toward the bottom of the range.

## Summary Principles
-   **Test is Key**: The nature of double tops and bottoms is the market's **test** of prior important resistance and support levels.
-   **Failure is Also a Signal**: All patterns can fail. Failed reversal patterns themselves are strong trading signals, signaling price will move in the **opposite direction** by a measurable amount.
-   **Utilize Measured Moves**: Whether the breakout is successful or failed, using pattern height to calculate **MM targets** is an effective method for setting profit targets and anticipating move endpoints.
-   **Distinguish Pullback from Range**: When adjustment time is too long (>20 bars), switch to **trading range** thinking mode, beware of false breakouts and trade from both ends of the range.
