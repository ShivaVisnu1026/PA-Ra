# Support and Resistance (Part 4) - Key Closing Prices and Breakout Pullbacks

## Support/Resistance Role of Key Closing Prices in Trends

### 1. Resistance Effect of Prior Highest Close in "Buy-the-Close" Uptrends
- **Background**: In strong "buy-the-close" uptrends (longs buy at or near the close of each bar, expecting trend continuation), when the trend first shows a significant pullback or the trend line is broken.
- **Long Psychology and Behavior**:
  - Longs who "bought the close" near trend highs may initially think this is just a small adjustment in the early pullback, will scale in on dips, hoping price returns to their initial entry point (prior highest close area) to break even or exit with small profits.
  - When the pullback magnitude is large or duration is long, these longs realize the strong trend may have ended, turning into a trading range or bull flag. Their strategy shifts from "trend trading" to "range trading" or "scalping".
  - When price bounces back near the prior highest close (their initial entry area), these previously trapped or scaled-in longs will choose to sell and close positions.
- **Resistance Formation**: Due to the selling behavior of the above longs, supply forms near the prior highest close, thus creating resistance, preventing price from easily making new highs, increasing the probability of market reversal or entering more complex consolidation in this area.

### 2. Support Effect of Prior Lowest Close in "Sell-the-Close" Downtrends
- **Background**: Opposite to the above situation, in strong "sell-the-close" downtrends.
- **Short Psychology and Behavior**:
  - Shorts who "sold the close" near trend lows may scale in on bounces when price first shows a significant bounce, hoping price returns to their initial entry point (prior lowest close area) to break even or exit with small profits.
  - When price bounces back near the prior lowest close, these shorts will choose to buy to cover positions.
- **Support Formation**: Due to the buying behavior of the above shorts, demand forms near the prior lowest close, thus creating support.

## Breakout Pullbacks - The Magnet Effect of Breakout Points
- **Definition**: After the market breaks through a key price level (such as prior high/low, trend line, trading range edge), it often retests that breakout point.
- **Magnet Effect**: The breakout point thus becomes a "magnet", attracting price to return to it.
- **Support/Resistance Transformation**:
  - **In Uptrends**: After breaking up through resistance, that resistance level (breakout point) transforms into support.
  - **In Downtrends**: After breaking down through support, that support level (breakout point) transforms into resistance.
- **Trading Implication**: Traders often enter in the breakout direction when price retests the breakout point and shows confirmation signals (such as reversal bars).
- **Depth of Pullback**: The pullback may precisely touch the breakout point, or may slightly exceed or not reach it. Sometimes the pullback is to the entire edge of the prior trading range, not just a single bar's breakout point.

## Failed Breakouts and Traps
- **Definition**: Price attempts to break through a support or resistance level but quickly reverses, causing the breakout to fail.
- **Trap Formation**:
  - **Bull Trap**: Price breaks up through resistance then quickly falls back below resistance, trapping longs.
  - **Bear Trap**: Price breaks down through support then quickly bounces back above support, trapping shorts.
- **Trading Implication**: Failed breakouts are often strong counter-trend signals. Trapped traders are forced to stop out, exacerbating the counter-trend move.
  - For example, a failed upward breakout of a 20-bar trading range high usually causes price to fall to the low of that trading range.

## Other Related Concepts
- **Micro Channels**: In the early stages of strong trends, multiple consecutive bars have almost no overlap, forming extremely narrow channels, which are early signals of strong trends.
- **Support/Resistance Within Bars**: The open, close, high, low, and 50% position of important bars (such as large body breakout bars, reversal bars) can all become short-term support or resistance.
- **Vacuum Effect**: When price breaks through a key point, if there is no significant support/resistance on the other side, price may quickly move toward the next target in a "vacuum".

**Core Summary of This Section:**
1. In the first pullback/bounce after strong trends, closing prices at trend extremes form support/resistance due to trader behavior.
2. Breakout points are important support/resistance when retested.
3. Failed breakouts and traps are strong counter-trend trading signals.
