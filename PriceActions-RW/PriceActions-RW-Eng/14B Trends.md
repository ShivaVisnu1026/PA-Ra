# Trends (Part 2)

## Identifying Trends and Trading Ranges

### Basic Principles
-   **Core Criterion**: If you can't read the chart and can't clearly determine bull/bear direction, then market is in **Trading Range**.
-   **Trading Range Characteristics**:
    -   Both bulls and bears have seemingly reasonable trading reasons.
    -   Chart simultaneously contains early features of both uptrends and downtrends (e.g., both lower highs and higher lows).
-   **Ranges Within Trends**: Strong trends also contain small trading ranges, i.e., **Pullbacks** or pauses, more obvious on smaller timeframe charts.

### Trading Range Evolution
-   **Time Factor**: When a trading range lasts over **20 bars**, original trend reversal probability gradually approaches 50%, equaling trend continuation probability.
-   **Trading Strategy**: Before clear trend breakout occurs, should trade using trading range strategy.

## Trend Structure: Breakout and Channel

### Breakout Phase
-   **Definition**: The strongest, clearest part of trend, typically showing as one or more bars with huge bodies, significantly breaking above prior consolidation area.
-   **Trading Implications**:
    -   **Market State Change**: Once strong breakout appears, means market changed from range to trend; probability of continuing in breakout direction extremely high.
    -   **High Win Rate vs. High Risk**: Breakout is high-probability entry signal but typically accompanied by huge bars, causing distant stops with large risk.
    -   **Response Strategy**: Must participate but manage risk by **reducing position size**. Don't wait for pullback due to fear of large stops, or may miss entire trend.

### Channel Phase
-   **Definition**: After breakout, trend continues in relatively narrow channel form with brief, shallow pullbacks.
-   **Characteristics**:
    -   Pullbacks typically last only 1-2 bars.
    -   Counter-trend signal bars (like short signals) usually weak.
    -   On higher timeframe charts, this narrow channel itself is a **breakout**.

## The 80% Rule: Why Counter-Trend Trading is a Trap

### Core Rule
-   **Principle of Inertia**: Market has inertia. Just as 80% of trading range breakouts fail, in strong trends, **80% of reversal attempts fail**.
-   **Failed Reversals = With-Trend Flags**: In strong uptrends, all structures that look like top patterns (like three-push wedge tops, double tops) are extremely likely to fail, ultimately evolving into **Bull Flags**, becoming trend continuation signals.

### Common Newbie Mistakes
-   **Mistaking Bull Flags for Tops**: Newbies see pullbacks or reversal patterns in strong trends and think trend is about to end, thus counter-trend trading too early.
-   **Obsessed with Small Risk**: To pursue small stops, newbies repeatedly counter-trend in strong trends. Though single risk is small, due to extremely low win rate (about 20%), repeated stops lead to "**death by a thousand cuts**" losses.
-   **Psychological Fallacy**: Thinking "after rising too much, must fall," using this as trading basis while ignoring overwhelming with-trend evidence on chart (like narrow channels, no decent counter-trend bars).

## Correct Trend Trading Strategy

### Trade With-Trend
-   **Only With-Trend Trades**: In strong one-way trends, should **only look for same-direction trading opportunities**. In strong uptrends only go long; in strong downtrends only go short.
-   **Act Immediately**: When seeing strong breakout, decisively enter (market orders, breakout orders), don't hesitate and wait.
-   **Use Pullbacks**: View pullbacks as **buy opportunities**, not reversal signals. Bull profit-taking causes pullbacks, which provide entry points for bulls who missed out.

### Risk and Position Management
-   **Set Hard Stops**: Must set actual protective stop orders in system, not just in mind. Reasonable stops below signal bar or below swing start point.
-   **Position Adjustment**: When stop distance increases (like breakout moves), control actual risk within acceptable range by **reducing position size**. This is one of core differences between professional traders and newbies.

### Summary Principles
-   **Unclear Means Range**: When market direction unclear, default to trading range.
-   **Must Follow Breakouts**: Strong breakouts are high-probability trend continuation signals; should participate by reducing position size rather than watching.
-   **80% Rule Supreme**: Firmly believe in strong trends, vast majority of reversal attempts will fail and become good opportunities for with-trend trading.
-   **Only Do High-Probability Things**: Avoid low-probability counter-trend trading in strong trends, even if single risk seems small. Continuous low-probability bets are the source of steady losses.
