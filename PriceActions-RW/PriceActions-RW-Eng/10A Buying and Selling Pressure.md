# Buying and Selling Pressure (Part 1)

## Buying and Selling Pressure Basics

### Definition & Identification
-   **Core Concept**: By observing bar patterns and price action, determine whether bulls (buyers) or bears (sellers) are stronger currently.
-   **Buying Pressure**: Indicates bulls are actively, strongly buying, typically signaling price will rise.
    -   **Signs**: More and larger bull bars; consecutive bull bars; bull bars closing high (near highs or at least above midpoint); bars with lower tails; tight bull channel; breaking above prior highs; creating gaps; bull bars increasing in size; consecutive bear bars decreasing in number and size.
-   **Selling Pressure**: Indicates bears are actively, strongly selling, typically signaling price will fall.
    -   **Signs**: More and larger bear bars; consecutive bear bars; bear bars closing low (near lows or at least below midpoint); bars with upper tails; tight bear channel; breaking below prior lows; creating gaps; bear bars increasing in size; consecutive bull bars decreasing in number and size.
-   **Institutional Footprints**: Significant buying or selling pressure typically reflects institutional trader behavior and consensus, increasing probability of trend continuation.

## Gaps

### Definition & Types
-   **Broad Definition**: Any "space" between support and resistance levels.
-   **Common Types**:
    -   **Opening Gap**: Large gap between opening price and previous close.
    -   **Bar Gaps**:
        -   Bar's low is higher than prior bar's high (gap up).
        -   Bar's high is lower than prior bar's low (gap down).
    -   **Closing Price Gaps**:
        -   Bar close is higher than prior bar's high.
        -   Bar close is lower than prior bar's low.
    -   **Negative Gap / Body Gap**:
        -   After breakout pullback, tails briefly overlap breakout point but bar bodies don't overlap.
        -   Indicates trend still strong but not as reliable as true gaps.
-   **Al Brooks' Special Definition**: Sometimes views certain areas between highs/lows as "gaps" (detailed in next unit).

### Gap Importance
-   **Trend Strength Signal**: Gaps (especially bar gaps and closing price gaps) are important indicators of strong trends. Shows one side far stronger than the other; price can continue without retracement.
-   **Measured Move (MM) Target**: Gaps (including negative gaps) often serve as starting points for measuring target levels. For example, after breaking necklines or failed patterns (like failed double bottoms), often use gap or pattern height to project targets.
-   **Support/Resistance Conversion**: Gap areas formed by breakouts transform former resistance to support, former support to resistance.
-   **Gap Filling**: Gaps sometimes get filled. If gap fills quickly, may mean trend strength weakening, market possibly entering range.

### Failed Patterns & Gap MM
-   **Failed Double Bottoms/Tops**: When market attempts to form double bottom but fails and breaks below low (or attempts double top but fails and breaks above high), can project pattern height downward (or upward) as MM target.
-   **Implied Failed Patterns**: Even without obvious rally forming double bottom, if price attempts to hold at prior low and fails to break below, can view as "failed double bottom attempt" and apply MM principle.

## Summary
-   Continuously observing changes in buying/selling pressure helps judge trend strength and possible breakout direction.
-   Gaps are important tools for measuring trend strength and predicting price targets.
