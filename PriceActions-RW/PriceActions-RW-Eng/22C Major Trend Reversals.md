# Major Reversals and Structure (Part 3)

## Moving Average Gap Bar

### Definition and Identification
- **Concept**: During trend movement, a bar whose body and shadows are completely on the opposite side of the moving average (EMA20), forming a visually apparent "gap" with the moving average.
- **Example**: In an uptrend, a bar appears whose high is also below EMA20.
- **Signal Meaning**: This is the first important signal of **strengthening counter-trend force**. It shows the counter-trend side can not only push the close past the moving average but can even suppress the entire price range outside the moving average.

### Trading Implications
- **Trend End Warning**: After a moving average gap bar appears, the next resumption move of the original trend (such as another rally in an uptrend) has a high probability of being the **final leg of the trend**.
- **Prepare for Reversal Trading**: When this "final leg" rally fails near prior highs, the market is very likely to transition to a trading range or directly start a major reversal. Traders should watch closely and prepare to look for reversal signals.

## Double Test Structures at Tops and Bottoms

### Core Principle
- The vast majority of tops are variants of **double tops**, and the vast majority of bottoms are variants of **double bottoms**.
- Markets rarely reverse in one move. Usually need to **test the trend extreme a second time**.
- **First Reversal Attempt**: Usually a minor reversal, high probability of failing, evolving into consolidation or pullback/bounce.
- **Second Reversal Attempt**: When the trend resumes and **tests again** the prior high/low and fails, the probability of major reversal significantly increases (approximately 40%).

### Structural Variants
- **Top Structure (Double Top Variants)**:
  - **Lower High Major Reversal**: Second top lower than first top.
  - **Higher High Major Reversal**: Second top slightly higher than first top.
  - **Perfect Double Top**: Two tops at exactly the same height, relatively rare.
- **Bottom Structure (Double Bottom Variants)**:
  - **Higher Low Major Reversal**: Second bottom higher than first bottom.
  - **Lower Low Major Reversal**: Second bottom slightly lower than first bottom.
  - **Perfect Double Bottom**: Two bottoms at exactly the same height, relatively rare.

## Building the Context for Reversal

### What Does a Successful Reversal Require?
- **Strong Counter-Trend Force**: The counter-trend side must prove through price action that it has the ability to control the market.
- **Ways Force Manifests**:
  - **Price**: Sharp, large-magnitude counter-trend moves appear (such as consecutive large bear/bull bars).
  - **Time**: Long duration (such as more than 10-20 bars) preventing original trend resumption, dragging market into sideways consolidation.
- **Key Context Signals**:
  - **Breaking Trend Lines**: Shows weakening momentum of original trend.
  - **Appearance of Moving Average Gap Bars**: As mentioned above, signals strengthening counter-trend force.
  - **Forming Double Test Structures**: Provides a high-probability reversal entry framework.

## Trading Strategy Trade-offs

### Entry Point Selection
- **Early Entry (Low Probability, Low Risk)**:
  - **Timing**: Enter when second test point of double top/bottom shows reversal signal.
  - **Characteristics**: Lower win rate (approximately 40%), but stop close, good risk/reward ratio. Need to accept possibility of multiple failures.
- **Entry After Confirmation (High Probability, High Risk)**:
  - **Timing**: Wait for market to show decisive breakout bars (such as three consecutive large bear bars breaking neckline), then chase or enter on subsequent pullback.
  - **Characteristics**: Win rate significantly increases (over 60%), but because entry point is far from extreme point, stops are usually far, reward/risk ratio worsens. This is the cost of trading risk for certainty.

## Summary Principles
- **Moving Average Gap Bar is Key Warning**: It indicates the next resumption move of the trend may be the final leg.
- **Core of Reversal is Double Testing**: The vast majority of major reversals are based on double top or double bottom structures. Focus on second test of prior highs/lows.
- **Assess Counter-Trend Force**: Judge whether counter-trend side is strong enough to support a major reversal through two dimensions: "price" and "time".
- **Trade-off Between Probability and Risk**: Choosing between low probability, low risk early entry and high probability, high risk entry after confirmation is a choice traders must make.
