# Components of Major Reversals

## Definition and Identification of Major Reversals

- **Basic Characteristics**: A successful trend reversal requires sufficient "topping" or "bottoming" time (usually more than 20 bars). Adjustments with only a few bars are usually only minor reversals or continuation flags, not major reversals.
- **Key Elements**: Transition from original trend to consolidation, ultimately breaking through the other side of the trading range, forming a new trend.

### Lower High Major Reversal

- **Pattern**: After an uptrend ends, the market attempts to resume upward but fails, forming a new high lower than the prior trend high. This usually appears as a double top pattern.
- **Confirmation**: When price breaks down through the neckline or key support with strong bear bars (breakout bars), the downtrend is confirmed.

### Higher High Major Reversal

- **Pattern**: After the market makes a new high, bullish power is exhausted, price immediately reverses sharply downward, showing the new high was a "bull trap" or buying exhaustion. Often appears as wedge tops (three pushes to top).
- **Confirmation**: Bull "give-up bar" (Give-up Bar)—i.e., large bear bar closing at lows—appearing is a strong signal that reversal is confirmed.

## Trading Strategies for Major Reversals: Two Entry Methods

### Early Entry (Low Win Rate, Excellent Reward/Risk)

- **Timing**: Enter *before* strong breakout bars appear, based on initial reversal signals of top patterns (such as double tops, wedge tops).
- **Characteristics**:
  - **Relatively Small Risk**: Stops can be set above pattern highs, initial risk is controllable.
  - **Lower Win Rate**: Probability of successfully developing into swing trades is approximately **40%**. The other 60% of the time may exit with small profits, small losses, or break-even.
  - **High Reward**: Once successful, potential profits are usually several times initial risk. Excellent reward/risk ratio makes this a positive expectation trade.

### Entry After Breakout (High Win Rate, Poor Reward/Risk)

- **Timing**: Look for entry opportunities *after* the market shows clear strong breakouts (such as large bear bars breaking key lows).
- **Characteristics**:
  - **Larger Risk**: Market has already moved some distance, stops need to be set above more distant important highs.
  - **Higher Win Rate**: Market direction is clear, profit probability significantly increases (**>60%**).
  - **Strategy Trade-off**: Using higher win rate to compensate for relatively poor reward/risk ratio.

## Trading After Trend Reversal

- **Core Logic**: Once a downtrend is established, follow the trend and treat every bounce as a selling opportunity (bear flag).
- **Key Signals**:
  - **Low 2 Short (L2 Short)**: This is a very reliable trend-following signal. When bulls' second attempt to reverse the new downtrend fails, they often give up. Their covering (buying) exit behavior accelerates price decline.
  - **Bear Flag Pattern**: In descending channels, any bounce (such as small double tops, wedge bounces) that fails is a good shorting opportunity. Traders will place sell orders below the low of signal bars (usually bear bars).

## Trade Management and Psychology

### Stops and Targets

- **Stop Placement**: Initial stops should be placed above the most recent "important high". A high is important usually because it was followed by strong decline and new lows.
- **Profit Targets (Measured Move - MM)**:
  - **Breakout Measurement**: Using the height of the breakout bar (or consecutive breakout bar group) itself as baseline, double downward as first target.
  - **Pattern Measurement**: Using the distance from the reversal pattern high to neckline (or gap) as baseline, double downward as main target.
  - **Two Times Risk**: When profit reaches twice initial risk, consider taking partial profits.

### Trading Psychology

- **Accept Failure**: Recognize that even perfect major reversals only have approximately 40% probability of successfully evolving into large swing moves. Need to be prepared for most trades to be small profits/losses.
- **Maintain Patience and Flexibility**: If exiting mid-trade for any reason (such as being stopped out or panic), but the major reversal premise remains valid, can re-enter when new effective signals appear.

## Summary Principles

- **Structure is Prerequisite**: Major reversals need sufficient time (>20 bars) to form, otherwise likely to be continuation flags.
- **Strategy Has Trade-offs**: "Early entry" pursues high reward/risk, "entry after breakout" pursues high win rate. Both methods can be profitable long-term.
- **Following Trend is King**: Once reversal is established, actively look for trend-following trading opportunities, especially highly reliable low 2/high 2 signals.
- **Expectations Must Be Reasonable**: The core of major reversal trading is using approximately 40% success opportunities to capture returns far exceeding risk, achieving positive mathematical expectation long-term.
