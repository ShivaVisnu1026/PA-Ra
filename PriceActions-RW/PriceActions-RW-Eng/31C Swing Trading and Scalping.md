# Scalping and Minimum Profit Targets (Part 3)

## Scalping: High Difficulty and Beginner Trap

### Definition and Reality
-   **High Difficulty**: Scalping is not suitable for beginners. It requires extremely high win rates to be consistently profitable, which is unrealistic for novices.
-   **Professional Choice**: Most consistently profitable professional traders primarily engage in swing trading, only occasionally scalping.

### Beginner Psychological Misconceptions
-   **Risk Illusion**: Beginners, fearing losses, are attracted to scalping's "small stops, small risk."
-   **Ignoring Probability**: They focus only on risk while ignoring the probability of trade success. Especially in strong trends, beginners tend to counter-trend predict tops/bottoms, and such reversal attempts have failure rates as high as 80%.
-   **Formula for Failure**: Low win rate + small profits = consistent losses. Even with 50-60% win rates, due to excessively small profits, they will still lose money.

## With-Trend vs. Counter-Trend Scalping Strategies

### With-Trend Trading: High-Probability Choice
-   **Core Principle**: Scalping in the direction of the trend is an easier way to make money.
-   **Strong Trend (Tight Channel)**: When the market forms a strong trend with very small pullbacks (with gaps, i.e., pullbacks don't touch prior breakout points), the only reasonable strategy is **only trading with the trend** (e.g., buying on pullbacks). Counter-trend trading is extremely dangerous in such environments.
-   **Expert Strategy**: Experts will find any reason to enter with the trend, such as buying on pullbacks, betting that counter-trend reversal attempts will fail.

### Counter-Trend Trading: High-Risk Behavior
-   **Weak Trend (Staircase Pattern)**: When a trend develops in a "staircase" pattern with large pullbacks overlapping prior breakout points, some top traders may engage in counter-trend scalping.
-   **Advanced Skill**: For example, in a staircase decline, placing limit orders to buy at new lows, betting on a quick bounce. This remains high-risk for most traders.

## Setting Realistic Minimum Profit Targets

### Why Minimum Targets Are Needed
-   **Cost Erosion**: Profit targets that are too small will be completely consumed by trading costs (commissions, spreads, slippage, error costs).
-   **Mathematical Trap**: Using ES (S&P E-mini futures) as an example, pursuing 2 ticks profit, after deducting commissions, profits are far less than potential losses, resulting in almost no profit even with win rates over 60%, leaving no room for error.
-   **"Passing Grade" Mindset Is Inadvisable**: Pursuing minimum profit targets is like only aiming for a passing grade in studies; it cannot serve as a long-term livelihood strategy.

### Rules of Thumb for Setting Targets
-   **Absolute Value References**:
    -   **Stock Index Futures (ES)**: Minimum 4-8 ticks (1-2 points).
    -   **Forex (EURUSD)**: Minimum 10-20 pips.
-   **Relative Value References**: Take the **larger** of the following two:
    1. Average size of recent bars.
    2. 5-10% of average true range (ATR).
-   **Dynamic Adjustment**: Targets should adjust with market volatility (bar size). When volatility is high, targets must be correspondingly larger.

## Trading Psychology and Long-Term Survival

### Risk vs. Win Rate Trade-off
-   **Wide Stops and Scaling In**: To achieve the high win rates required for scalping, traders typically need to use **wider initial stops** and **smaller positions**, and be prepared to scale in when price moves against them.
-   **Narrow Stop Paradox**: Seemingly low-risk narrow stops actually lead to lower win rates, which contradicts the core requirement of scalping.

### Trading Must Be Enjoyable
-   **Personality Match**: Trading style must suit your personality. If scalping makes you feel extremely stressed and unhappy, even if technically possible, it cannot serve as a long-term career.
-   **Sustainability**: A successful career requires both skill and happiness.

## Summary Principles
-   **Beginners Should Avoid**: Scalping is extremely unfriendly to beginners; focus first on learning and practicing swing trading.
-   **With-Trend Is King**: Especially in clearly trending markets, firmly trade with the trend, avoiding counter-trend reversal predictions.
-   **Profits Must Cover Costs**: Set profit targets large enough to completely cover all trading costs including commissions and spreads.
-   **Set Reasonable Targets**: Use bar size and intraday range as references to set realistic, dynamic minimum profit targets.
-   **Pursue Larger Returns**: Successful scalpers typically pursue profits far exceeding minimum targets, only considering small targets in extremely dull markets.
-   **Enjoy Trading**: Choosing a trading style that makes you feel happy and enjoyable is a key psychological factor for achieving long-term consistent profitability.
