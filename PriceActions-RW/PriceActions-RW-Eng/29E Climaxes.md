# Causes of Climaxes and Failed Patterns (Part Five)

## Climaxes & Options Market Makers

### The Deeper Cause of Sell Climaxes
-   **Core View**: Violent sell climaxes are not just shorts selling; a large portion (possibly up to 50%) comes from options market makers' hedging behavior.
-   **Mechanism**:
    -   Institutions buy put options to hedge against stock decline risk.
    -   Market makers are sellers of these put options. When the market declines, put option values **accelerate** upward (Gamma effect), causing market makers' losses to rapidly expand.
    -   To offset losses, market makers must **heavily short** stocks or stock index futures for hedging.
    -   This hedging selling greatly exacerbates market decline, forming sell climaxes.
-   **Trading Implication**:
    -   Market makers themselves are not bearish on the market; their behavior is neutral risk management.
    -   Therefore, the bearish sentiment reflected in sell climaxes may **not be as strong as it appears**.
    -   When this hedging selling pressure stops, without enough vacuum, the market easily stops declining or even reverses.

### The Difference in Buy Climaxes
-   **Weaker Intensity**: Buy climaxes (rallies) are usually not as violent as sell climaxes (declines).
-   **Reason**: In rallies, call options sold by market makers face relatively small Gamma risk. Therefore, their pressure to buy stocks for hedging is smaller, making little "contribution" to rally climaxes.

## Failed Consecutive Climax Patterns

### Two Paths After a Sell Climax
-   **Context**: Consecutive sell climaxes usually form down channels or wedge bottoms, which are essentially bull flags with 75% probability of breaking up to reverse.
-   **Path One: Successful Downside Breakout (25% Probability)**
    -   **Characteristics**: Strong bear bar (with good follow-through) breaks below channel or wedge bottom.
    -   **Implication**: This is a low-probability event but indicates extremely strong bear force. It traps all dip-buying longs and attracts breakout shorts to enter.
    -   **Trading Strategy**: Market is likely to enter a new decline leg, at least with a **MM target (Measured Move)** based on pattern height.
-   **Path Two: Exhaustion Climax & Reversal (75% Probability)**
    -   **Characteristics**: After trend persists for a long time (e.g., more than 20 bars), the **largest** sell bar appears.
    -   **Implication**: This is usually not a signal of trend continuation (measuring gap) but an **Exhaustion Gap**. It represents weak longs' final surrender and strong shorts' profit-taking.
    -   **Trading Strategy**: Expect trend to end. Minimum reversal target is **two legs, 10 bars of sideways or rally** (TBTL - Ten Bars, Two Legs). Market is likely to bounce above the starting point of this exhaustion bar.

### Exhaustion After a Buy Climax
-   **Similar Principle**: After a long-persisting uptrend, the largest bull bar appears, especially when testing key resistance (such as prior high, 50% retracement of large decline), also likely an exhaustion signal.
-   **Identification**: This bull bar may be a **vacuum test** of resistance, a sign of trend end, not a breakout.
-   **Subsequent Action**: Market is likely to enter consolidation or start pullback. Traders should beware of **second leg trap** and look for reversal signals such as double tops, bearish ii patterns.

## Summary Principles
-   **Understand Nature of Climaxes**: Violent climaxes (especially sell climaxes) are largely caused by market makers hedging option risk; the true underlying bearish/bullish sentiment may be exaggerated.
-   **Beware "Largest Bar" at Trend Ends**: In long-persisting trends (>20 bars), the largest bar at the end is usually an **Exhaustion** signal, not a signal of trend acceleration.
-   **Identify Failed Patterns**: Channels or wedges formed by consecutive climaxes are key. Upward breakout is high probability, but beware of strong downward breaks with good follow-through, which trigger MM-level moves.
-   **Expectations After Climaxes**: After climaxes (unsustainable behavior) end, the minimum expectation is market entering **sideways or opposite two-legged, 10-bar adjustment**, providing counter-trend trading opportunities.
