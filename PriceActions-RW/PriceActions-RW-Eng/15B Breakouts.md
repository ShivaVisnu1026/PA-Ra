# Breakouts (Part 2): Follow-Through & Confirmation

## Breakouts and Follow-Through Bars

### The Importance of Confirmation
-   **Core Concept**: For any breakout (whether upward or downward) to succeed, it must be followed by **Follow-through Bar** in same direction as confirmation.
-   **Success Signals**:
    -   Follow-through bar is strong same-direction bar (like large bull/bear bar, close near extreme).
    -   Good follow-through significantly increases probability of trend continuation and may guide price to **Measured Move (MM)** target.
-   **Failure Signals**:
    -   Follow-through bar is counter-direction bar (like bear bar appearing after upward breakout).
    -   This indicates breakout likely **failed**; market may reverse or enter range.

## Trader Psychology and Behavior

### The Battle Between With-Trend and Counter-Trend
-   **"80% Rule"**: In trends, approximately 80% of reversal attempts fail. Therefore, with-trend traders use this.
-   **Counter-Trend Trader Strategy**: When counter-direction signal bars appear in trends (like large bear bar in uptrend), counter-trend traders attempt to push reversal, while with-trend traders enter near this bar's close, **betting reversal will fail** (i.e., no follow-through bar appears).
-   **Battle Point**: Follow-through bar location is key battlefield between bulls and bears. With-trend side needs it to confirm trend; counter-trend side fully prevents its appearance.
-   **"Give-up Bar"**: When breakout and follow-through are very strong, counter-trend traders who kept shorting finally "give up" and cover (buy to close). This buying force merges with with-trend traders' buying, causing sharp acceleration upward, forming **high-probability entry point**.

## Forms and Characteristics of Breakouts

### The Diversity of Breakouts
-   **Single Bar Breakout**: Consists of one strong trend bar.
-   **Micro-Channel Breakout**: Consists of series of small bars without pullbacks; together they form a breakout. On higher timeframe charts, this typically appears as one or several strong bars.
-   **Reversal is Breakout**: Every trend reversal is essentially a breakout of prior structure (like trend line, channel).
-   **Gaps**: Especially in stock markets, opening gaps are also a form of breakout, following same success or failure rules.

## Strategy and Management for Breakout Trading

### Entry and Risk
-   **High-Probability Entry**: In strong breakout moves (e.g., consecutive large bull bars), adopting **"Buy The Close"** strategy, i.e., immediately market buying on bull bar close, is high-probability trading method.
-   **Disadvantages & Challenges**:
    -   **Distant Stop**: Strong breakouts usually mean stops need to be placed far away (like trend start point), causing large single-trade risk.
    -   **Emotional Pressure**: Chasing trends is huge psychological test for traders.
-   **Solutions**:
    -   **Adjust Position Size**: Must trade using **very small position size** based on wider stop distance, ensuring even if loss occurs, amount is within controllable range.

### Holding and Targets
-   **Avoid Exiting Too Early**: In strong trends, frequent scalping profit-taking may cause missing subsequent larger moves because re-entry is difficult.
-   **Holding Principle**: As long as trend validity premise isn't broken (i.e., no strong reversal signal appears), should **hold firmly**.
-   **Profit Targets**: A common and high-probability target is **Measured Move (MM)** distance calculated based on breakout formation.

## Summary Principles
-   **Follow-Through is Key**: Follow-through bar after breakout bar is most important basis for judging breakout success or failure.
-   **Understand Battle Psychology**: Recognize market is result of constant battle between with-trend and counter-trend traders, especially at key follow-through bar positions.
-   **Breakout Forms Diverse**: Breakouts can be one large bar, series of small bars, or even gaps.
-   **Risk Management Supreme**: Strong trend breakouts are high-probability trades, but this must be combined with strict **small position size management** to handle inherent wide stops and psychological pressure.
