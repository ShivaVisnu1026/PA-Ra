# Breakouts (Part 3): Second Leg & Range Breakouts

## Strong Breakouts & The Second Leg

### "Unexpected" Breakouts & Trapped Traders
-   **Concept**: When market shows "unexpected" breakout opposite to expectation (e.g., bear flag strongly broken upward), it simultaneously traps two types of traders:
    -   **Trapped In**: Traders shorting (or longing) with original trend get trapped; they eagerly await pullback to exit with smaller losses.
    -   **Trapped Out**: Traders who didn't enter believing reversal probability low missed the move; they also eagerly await pullback to get on board.
-   **Core Logic**: Because both bulls (trapped out) and bears (trapped in) want to buy on pullback, this powerful combined force makes first pullback after breakout extremely likely to get bought up, thus high probability of generating **second leg upward** (vice versa).

### Inevitability of Second Leg & Trading Strategy
-   **High-Probability Event**: Any strong, unexpected breakout almost certainly (extremely high probability) follows with at least second leg in same direction. This is one of core expectations in breakout trading.
-   **Trading Application**:
    -   **Entry**: After confirming strong breakout (e.g., powerful breakout bar + good follow-through), should actively look for entry points on first pullback (usually bull flag or bear flag), targeting capture of second leg.
    -   **Judgment**: Even if second leg doesn't make new high/low, or just brief few bars, it still satisfies "second leg" definition. Key is understanding driving force behind it.

### Identifying Exhaustion Gaps & Measuring Gaps
-   **Measuring Gap**: When breakout occurs in trading range or early trend, strong breakout is usually "measuring gap," predicting trend will continue and at least complete a "second leg" equal to breakout magnitude.
-   **Exhaustion Gap**: When a very strong breakout (usually largest bar in trend) occurs late in trend that has lasted long (like over 20-50 bars), it's more likely an "exhaustion gap."
    -   **Meaning**: This marks trend termination rather than continuation; final frenzy "selling/buying climax."
    -   **Consequence**: Market likely reverses, filling this gap (i.e., price returns to breakout start point).

## Breakouts from Trading Ranges

### Trading Psychology in Trading Ranges
-   **Breakout Failures are Normal**: In clear trading ranges, approximately 80% of breakout attempts fail.
-   **Trader Behavior**: Experienced traders use this for **counter-trading** (fade extremes), shorting near range top, buying near range bottom, with scalping quick profit-taking.
-   **Inertia & Transition**: When range lasts over 20 bars, market inertia disappears; probability of breaking up or down becomes equal 50/50.

### Characteristics of Successful Breakouts
A breakout is more likely to succeed and start new trend if it has one or more of these features:
-   **Strong Breakout Bar**: Body far larger than recent bars, close near extreme (short upper or lower tail).
-   **Clear Close Position**: Close clearly outside trading range; more bars breaking out, stronger the signal.
-   **Good Follow-Through Bar**: Bar immediately following breakout continues confirming breakout direction; no immediate counter-movement.

### Trading Dynamics After Breakout
1.  **Stops & Profits Trigger Pullback**: After successful strong breakout, stops typically placed at breakout leg's start point, far from current price. Huge floating profits and risk make early entrants (including programs) choose **partial profit-taking to control risk**, causing first pullback.
2.  **Waiting & Confirmation**: After pullback appears, both bulls and bears observe. Bears (trapped) hope for higher rally to exit small loss; Bulls (trapped out and new entrants) wait for pullback end signal to buy at better price.
3.  **Second Leg Launch**: If pullback lasts several bars but fails to continue (e.g., unable to form strong counter bar), market reaches consensus: trend will continue. At this point, trapped bears give up hope and stop out (buy), while bulls actively enter (buy), jointly pushing price into second leg.

## Summary Principles
-   **Understand Trapped Psychology**: Core driving force of strong breakouts comes from combined, one-way trading demand of both "trapped in" and "trapped out" traders, making **second leg** a high-probability event.
-   **Distinguish Gap Nature**: Huge breakout bar in late trend is more likely **exhaustion** signal rather than **continuation** signal.
-   **Identify Successful Breakouts**: Successful breakouts from trading ranges need three elements: **"Strength" (large bar), "Distance" (far from range), and "Confirmation" (good follow-through)**.
-   **Trade With-Trend**: Once successful breakout confirmed, should switch thinking from counter-trading within range to with-trend trading, looking for entry opportunities in pullbacks.
