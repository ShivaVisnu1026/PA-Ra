# Mathematical Expectation and Probability (Part Three)

## Z-Score: Assessing System Reliability

### Definition and Meaning
-   **Concept**: Z-score is a mathematical method to measure trading system reliability and consistency. It measures how many standard deviations trading results deviate from the mean.
-   **Function**: High Z-score means system is stable, high probability of continued profitability in the future; low Z-score means system is unstable, high risk of ruin.

### High Z-Score vs. Low Z-Score Systems
-   **High Z-Score System Characteristics (Good System)**:
    -   Higher win rate, relatively consistent profit distribution.
    -   Example: Trading High 2/Low 2 signals in trend pullbacks.
    -   Low risk of ruin, allowing traders to moderately increase position risk (e.g., 2% of account).
-   **Low Z-Score System Characteristics (Bad System)**:
    -   Extremely low win rate, relies on one or two "big wins" to offset many small losses.
    -   Profits are extremely unstable, most profits concentrated in a few trades.
    -   **High Risk**: Once key big wins are missed, the system overall loses, extremely high risk of ruin.
    -   Psychologically difficult to execute, easy to give up after consecutive losses, missing profit opportunities.

## Probability: The Most Important Variable in Trading

### Core Position
-   Among the three trading variables (risk, profit, probability), risk (stop) and profit (target) are known, while **probability** is the most uncertain and hardest to grasp, and is the main cause of novice losses.

### Common Probability Misjudgments by Novices
-   **Wrong Entry Timing**: Buying rallies at trend ends (e.g., top of narrow consolidation range) or attempting counter-trend bottom-picking too early in strong trends.
-   **Missing Opportunities**: Not daring to enter high-probability opportunities like strong breakouts due to fear of risk (e.g., stop appears too far).
-   **Exiting Too Early**: Panicking and closing positions due to normal profit retracements in trend moves, missing most of the profit.

### Market's "90/10" Rule
-   **90% of Time**: Market is in trading ranges or wide channels. At this time, probabilities for both sides range between 40%-60%, direction is unclear, but both-direction trades have opportunities.
-   **10% of Time**: Market is in strong breakouts or tight channels (i.e., "one-sided moves"). At this time, probability in trend direction **far exceeds 60%**; traders should only consider trend trades.

## Assessing Probability in Practice & Trading Decisions

### Identifying High-Probability Scenarios (60%+)
-   **Definition**: When Al Brooks says "high probability" or "likely", he means win rate of at least **60%**.
-   **Identification**: High-probability scenarios usually appear in **strong breakouts** and **strong trends** (the 10% of market time). For example, consecutive large bear bars breaking down in downtrends.

### Reasonable Trading Mathematical Expectation
-   In high-probability (≥60%) trades, even if reward/risk ratio is only **1:1** (profit target = initial risk), the trade's mathematical expectation is positive and reasonable.
-   Traders should not pursue perfection; as long as mathematical expectation is positive, it can be executed.

### Trading Strategies in Strong Trends
-   **Trade with Trend**: In one-sided moves, can enter with trend in various ways (such as breakouts, pullbacks, limit orders, etc.).
-   **Reasonable Stop Placement**: Place stops at reasonable structural points, such as just beyond recent important high/low or above/below breakout starting point.
-   **Set Minimum Target**: Use initial risk (stop distance) as benchmark, set at least one risk distance (1R) as initial profit target.

## Risk Management & Mindset for Novices

### Importance of "Don't Care" Position Size
-   **Core Concept**: Trade with position sizes so small that losses won't affect emotions.
-   **Purpose**: Shift attention from profit/loss amounts to **executing correct operations**, learning and practicing at minimum cost, avoiding emotional decisions.
-   **Recommendation**: Novices should control single-trade risk to around 1% of total account capital.

### Overcoming Emotions and Uncertainty
-   The nature of trading is dealing with uncertainty, especially in the 90% of non-one-sided market time.
-   Must learn to act like a program, free from interference of emotions (fear, greed), following trading logic and systems.

## Summary Principles
-   **Beware "Big Win" Systems**: A healthy trading system should have stable profits, not rely on a few trades.
-   **Probability is Core**: Focus on identifying strong trend moves with probability exceeding 60%; this is when it's easiest to profit.
-   **Accept 1:1 Reward/Risk**: In high-probability (≥60%) trades, pursuing 1R profit is completely reasonable and profitable strategy.
-   **Start with Small Positions**: Practice with "don't care" position sizes until able to consistently execute correct trading processes, then gradually increase size.
