# Trading Range Trading Strategies (Part 2)

## Simultaneous Long & Short Opportunities in Trading Ranges

### Core Concept and Identification
- **Definition**: Within a trading range, the market simultaneously has **reasonable reasons to go long** and **reasonable reasons to go short**. When you feel that both buying and selling make sense, you can basically confirm that the market is in a trading range.
- **80% Rule**: About 80% of trading range **breakout attempts will fail**, and the market tends to continue its sideways state, showing inertia.

### Behavior Differences Between Professionals and Beginners
- **Beginners**: Tend to **chase breakouts** at range edges. Seeing strong bars, they think a trend is about to begin, often buying at the top and selling at the bottom, getting trapped.
- **Professional Traders**: Believe breakouts will likely fail, so they adopt a **"sell high, buy low"** strategy. They buy at the bottom of the range and sell at the top, usually doing **scalping** for quick profits.

### Trading Strategy
- **Sell High, Buy Low**: Look for buying opportunities in the lower third of the range, and selling opportunities in the upper third of the range.
- **Wide Stops and Scaling In**: Professional traders may use wider stops and be prepared to **scale in** at more favorable prices. The prerequisite is that the initial position is small enough, and the scaling-in point is at least one scalping profit distance away from the initial entry point.
- **Quick Profit Taking**: Since the market lacks clear direction, quick profit taking is the main strategy within trading ranges.

## Breakout Mode

### Definition and Identification
- **Concept**: When a trading range continues to narrow, forming patterns such as a **triangle** (a series of lower highs and higher lows), the market enters "breakout mode".
- **Characteristic**: At this point, the 80% rule no longer fully applies, and the probability of successful breakouts increases.

### Trading Probabilities
- **Breakout Direction**: The probability of eventually breaking up or down is **50%** each.
- **First Breakout Failure Rate**: The **first** attempt to break up or down has a **50% probability of failing** and reversing, forming a "false breakout".

## Second Leg Traps

### Definition and Identification
- **Concept**: Refers to a situation within a trading range where, after the market completes the first leg move, a seemingly **exceptionally strong second leg** appears, luring traders into believing a trend is about to begin, but ultimately fails and reverses.
- **Characteristics**:
  - Usually appears in wide channels or trading ranges.
  - **The second leg is usually stronger than the first leg**, making it more deceptive.
  - A typical pattern is an **exhaustion climax breakout**, which has an extremely high failure rate (about 75%).
  - A key identification signal is **poor follow-through** after a strong breakout. For example, a large bull bar followed immediately by a doji or bear bar.

### Trading Implications and Strategy
- **Avoid Chasing**: Never chase seemingly strong second leg moves at trading range edges.
- **Identify Traps**: Always analyze in conjunction with the **context** on the left side. If strong bars are still within a large trading range, it is very likely a trap.
- **Counter-Trend Trading Opportunity**: The failure of a second leg trap is a **high-probability reversal trading signal**. Once a failed breakout is confirmed (e.g., a strong reversal bar appears), you can enter for counter-trend trading, with the target usually being the other end of the range.
- **Stop Loss Immediately**: If you accidentally get trapped (e.g., buying at the high), once you notice poor follow-through or reversal signals, you must **stop out immediately**.

## Summary Principles
- **Think Both Ways Within Ranges**: In trading ranges, bullish and bearish forces are balanced, and there are always simultaneous opportunities to go long and short.
- **Beware of False Breakouts**: Most (80%) breakout attempts will fail. Only in "breakout mode" does the success probability increase to 50%.
- **Identify Second Leg Traps**: Beware of seemingly strong second leg moves at range edges, as they are often **traps** to lure longs or shorts, and are also excellent counter-trend trading opportunities.
- **Context is King**: Trading decisions must be based on the **larger context** in which the market is situated, and not be fooled by the strength of a single bar.
