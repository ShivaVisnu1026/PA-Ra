# Gap Analysis (Part 2): Moving Average Gaps & Exhaustion Gaps

## Moving Average Gap

### Definition & Identification
-   **Concept**: In a mature trend lasting 20+ bars, the first occurrence of one or more bars whose entirety is completely on the other side of the 20-period moving average (EMA20), creating a "gap" between bar bodies and the MA.
-   **Signal Meaning**: This is the first important signal that trend is entering late stages and counter forces are accumulating.

### Trading Implications
-   **MA Gap in Uptrend** (bar high below MA):
    -   **Signal**: Indicates strengthening bear pressure.
    -   **Short-term Expectation**: Despite the gap, uptrend will likely resume and test prior highs upward.
    -   **Later Risk**: After price makes new high, approximately **60%** probability bears will attempt a **Major Reversal**.
    -   **Reversal Success Rate**: This major reversal attempt has approximately **40%** probability of success, forming a down leg; the remaining 60% probability evolves into trading range or bull flag.
-   **MA Gap in Downtrend** (bar low above MA):
    -   **Signal**: Indicates strengthening bull pressure.
    -   **Short-term Expectation**: Downtrend will likely resume and test prior lows downward.
    -   **Later Opportunity**: After price makes new low, approximately **60%** probability bulls will attempt a **Major Reversal**.
    -   **Reversal Success Rate**: Similarly, this reversal attempt has approximately **40%** probability of success, forming an up leg.

## Exhaustion Gap

### Definition & Identification
-   **Concept**: In late trend stage (typically lasting 10-20+ bars), one or a series of abnormally large trend bars appears, breaking prior highs/lows, forming a gap.
-   **Key Characteristic**: This breakout bar is often the **largest bar** in the recent trend.
-   **Probability Assessment**: This formation is more likely (approximately **60%** probability) a signal of trend **exhaustion** (buying/selling climax) rather than trend acceleration signal (measuring gap).
-   **Confirmation**: When price reverses and completely "fills" the gap (i.e., returns within the breakout point), the gap is confirmed as exhaustion gap.

### Trading Implications & Strategy
-   **Initial Warning**: An abnormally large bar in late trend is itself a **high-risk warning**; be alert that trend may be ending.
-   **Main Targets**:
    -   **First Target**: Reversal and gap fill (return to breakout point).
    -   **Second Target**: Test the **start point** of this "climax" move.
-   **Subsequent Movement**:
    -   After gap fills, market typically enters an adjustment lasting at least 10 bars with two legs (TBTL), evolving into **trading range** or **trend reversal**.
    -   **Second Leg Trap**: In trading range context, this seemingly strong breakout is often a bull/bear trap—an excellent counter-trend trading opportunity.

## Summary Principles
-   **MA Gap is Warning Signal**: It predicts that after current trend makes one more move, a high-probability major reversal attempt will likely appear.
-   **Exhaustion Gap is Termination Signal**: The largest bar at trend's end is typically the "final frenzy," marking trend pause or termination rather than acceleration. Traders should be ready for reversal or consolidation.
-   **Focus on Reversal Patterns & Targets**: Regardless of gap type, once reversal signs appear (like strong counter bars, poor follow-through), traders should watch for gap fill possibility and use the climax move's start point as a key potential target.
