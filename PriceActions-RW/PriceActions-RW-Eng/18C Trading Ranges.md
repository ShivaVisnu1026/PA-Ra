# Trading Ranges and Leg Analysis (Part 3)

## Definition of Trading Ranges and Breakout Mode

### Identification and Characteristics
- **Definition**: When a pullback or bounce in a trend lasts too long, typically **more than 20 bars**, the market transitions from a trend to a trading range.
- **Breakout Mode**: Once a trading range forms, the market enters "breakout mode", where the probability of **trend resumption** and **trend reversal** is roughly equal, both at **50/50**.
- **Dual Signals**: Within a trading range, there are always simultaneous reasonable reasons to go long (such as double bottoms, major reversals with higher lows) and reasons to go short (such as double tops, major reversals with lower highs).

### Trading Implications
- **Equal Probability**: Don't over-bias toward the original trend direction. Must be prepared for both upward and downward breakouts.
- **First Breakout Often Fails**: In trading ranges, whether upward or downward, **the first breakout attempt has an 80% probability of failing** and reversing back into the range.
- **Trading Strategy**: Before a clear breakout direction appears, adopt a **"sell high, buy low"** trading range strategy rather than chasing breakouts.

## Market Cycle: From Channels to Trading Ranges

### Evolution Process
- **Typical Cycle**: Markets often follow an evolution path of "**strong breakout → channel → trading range**".
- **Channels are Transitional**: After a strong trend (rapid rise/fall), it usually evolves into a channel.
- **Channels Likely to Break**: Whether it's a rising channel (bear flag) or a falling channel (bull flag), there is approximately a **75% probability** that the trend line will be broken in the opposite direction, eventually evolving into a wider trading range.

### Trading Insights
- **Understanding the Nature of Channels**:
  - **Falling channels** should be viewed as **bull flags**, expecting upward breakouts.
  - **Rising channels** should be viewed as **bear flags**, expecting downward breakouts.
- **Setting Targets**: When a channel is broken, the first price target is usually the **starting point of the channel**, which helps determine the potential boundaries of the future trading range.

## Leg Analysis: Beginning of Trend or Part of Trading Range?

### How to Judge Leg Strength
- **Core Question**: Is the current leg up or down a new strong trend beginning, or merely a component within a trading range?
- **Signals of Weak Trend/Trading Range Legs**:
  - **Poor Follow-through**: Strong trend bars (large bull/bear bars) followed immediately by opposite bars or dojis.
  - **Lack of Continuity**: No consecutive strong trend bars appear.
  - **Deep Pullbacks and Overlap**: Price shows deep pullbacks during advance, with extensive overlap between bars.
  - **Stalling at Key Levels**: Price hesitates when approaching key resistance/support levels such as prior highs or lows.
  - **Buying/Selling Climax**: The largest trend bars appear only after the trend has run for some time, which is often an **exhaustion signal** rather than an initiation signal.
- **Signals of Strong Trends**:
  - **Consecutive Strong Trend Bars**: Consecutive large bull/bear bars with closes near extremes.
  - **Good Follow-through**: Breakout bars followed by bars in the same direction confirming the move.
  - **Breakout Gaps**: Strong breakouts form price gaps (even gaps between bar bodies) that are maintained.

## Trading Behavior Within Trading Ranges

### Core Strategy
- **Belief**: Traders generally believe that movement in any direction won't go too far and will quickly reverse.
- **Method**: **Sell high, buy low, scalp for quick profits**.
  - **At Highs**: Trend longs take profits and exit, counter-trend shorts open positions.
  - **At Lows**: Trend shorts take profits and exit, counter-trend longs open positions.
- **Trading Techniques**:
  - **Wide Stops and Scaling In**: Traders tend to use wider stops and scale in at more favorable positions because they are uncertain about the precise boundaries of the range.
  - **Counter-Trend Operations**: **Sell** when large bull bars close near the top of the range; **buy** when large bear bars close near the bottom of the range, betting that breakouts will fail.
