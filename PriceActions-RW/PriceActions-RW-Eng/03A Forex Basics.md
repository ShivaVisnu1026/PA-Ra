# Forex Trading Fundamentals

## What is the Forex Market?

### Definition & Core Characteristics
-   **Definition**: Forex (Foreign Exchange) is the abbreviation for trading currencies in **pairs**. It is the world's **largest** financial market.
-   **Decentralized**:
    -   No central exchange like the NYSE; trading occurs simultaneously across thousands of banks and platforms globally.
    -   Not regulated by a single institution.
-   **No Unified Data**:
    -   **No Official Close**: Due to decentralized trading, there's no recognized "last trade." Prices vary slightly between platforms.
    -   **No Reliable Volume**: Volume shown on platforms only represents that platform's activity, possibly <1% of total market, thus not meaningful for analysis.

### Market Participants
-   **Main Participants**: Include large speculators (like George Soros), multinational corporations (exchanging currency for international business), governments, tourists, and retail traders.
-   **Trading Costs**: Professional forex platforms have spreads typically far below 1%, while airport exchange counters may charge up to 10%—unsuitable for trading.

## Trading Sessions & Liquidity

### 24-Hour Market
-   **Trading Hours**: From Sunday afternoon open to Friday afternoon close, running nearly 24 hours continuously.
-   **Major Trading Sessions**:
    -   **Asian Session** (Sydney/Tokyo)
    -   **European Session** (London)
    -   **US Session** (New York)
-   **Session Overlap**: Different sessions overlap, and when **London and New York sessions overlap**, the market is typically most active with best liquidity.

### Trading Strategy Points
-   **Choose Fixed Session**: Traders should choose a fixed session to trade, adapting to its specific market behavior and rhythm.
-   **Avoid "Dead Zones"**: Between major sessions (like after US close), the market typically enters **tight trading ranges** with poor liquidity, making profits difficult—avoid trading these times.
-   **Ample Liquidity**: During major sessions, market liquidity is extremely high; retail trader orders don't affect market prices.

## Leverage, Fundamentals & Risks

### Leverage
-   **Advantage**: Allows traders to control large positions with small capital (like 50:1 leverage). This lowers entry barriers; beginners can start with very small accounts (like $30).
-   **Risk**: Leverage is a **double-edged sword**. Over-leveraging is the main cause of rapid account losses and blowouts.
-   **Recommendation**: Traders should use leverage reasonably based on account size, not blindly use the highest leverage offered by platforms.

### Fundamental Analysis
-   **Institutions vs. Retail**: Institutional traders pay close attention to economic news, interest rates, and other fundamentals.
-   **Retail Trader Strategy**: Al Brooks strongly recommends retail traders **ignore fundamentals**.
-   **Price Action is Key**: **The chart tells you everything**. If the market is rising, it means fundamentals are bullish—no need to confirm through news. Trading decisions should be completely based on chart price action.

### Trading Risks & Platform Selection
-   **Main Risks**:
    -   **Over-Leverage**: The primary risk.
    -   **High Trading Costs**: Some platforms charge excessive spreads to small accounts, making profits difficult.
    -   **Platform Risk**: Trading platforms may experience technical errors, and agreements typically stipulate traders bear the losses.
-   **Platform Selection**:
    -   Choose **large, reputable** professional forex platforms (like FXCM, etc.).
    -   Focus on: low commissions, narrow spreads, stable trading systems.
    -   Note: Regulatory changes may affect retail forex operations of some comprehensive brokers (like IB, TradeStation).
