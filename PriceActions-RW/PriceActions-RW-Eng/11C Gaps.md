# Gaps (Part 3)

## Micro Gaps

### Definition & Identification
-   **Concept**: Small but important gaps typically appearing around trend bars.
-   **Identification Method**:
    -   **In Uptrend**: Observe a bull bar where the subsequent bar's low is higher than the prior bar's high.
    -   **In Downtrend**: Observe a bear bar where the subsequent bar's high is lower than the prior bar's low.
-   **Characteristic**: Represents very strong pressure in trend direction, not allowing price to retrace to prior breakout point.

### Trading Implications
-   **Trend Continuation Signal**: Micro gaps increase probability of original trend continuing.
-   **Trend Weakening Signal**: When these micro gaps start getting **filled** by subsequent bars (e.g., in uptrend, pullback breaks below prior high), this indicates trend losing momentum with increasing reversal risk.

## Gap Bars

### Definition & Identification
-   **Concept**: A gap exists between a bar's opening price and the previous bar's closing price.
    -   **Gap Up**: Open > prior close.
    -   **Gap Down**: Open < prior close.
-   **Essence**: Reflects trader **urgency**; many traders entered at market at the close, causing next bar to gap open.

### Trading Implications
-   **Early Trend**: Consecutive same-direction gap bars signal strong trend, predicting possible continuation.
-   **Late Trend**: At trend's end, a with-trend gap that immediately reverses is typically an **exhaustion signal**, predicting trend about to end or enter range.
-   **Failed Breakout**: A gap down (breakout attempt) that quickly reverses closing as bull bar shows bull strength, possibly beginning of reversal. Vice versa.
-   **Market Applicability**: In forex markets, gap bar reliability is relatively low due to non-unified data sources, especially on smaller timeframes.

## Body Gaps

### Definition & Identification
-   **Concept**: Bar tails may overlap but **bar bodies don't overlap**. Also called "negative gaps" by instructor.
-   **Characteristic**: Though not as strong as "true gaps" where tails don't overlap, still an important manifestation of trend strength. On line charts (showing only closes), body gaps appear as true gaps.

### Trading Implications
-   **Trend Strength**: Indicates even though price pulled back (tail overlap), closes still maintain outside breakout zone, showing trend-side control.
-   **Trend Weakening**: When pullback bar bodies start overlapping with pre-breakout bar bodies, i.e., **body gaps get filled**, means trend strength declining, possibly transitioning to range or reversal.

## Gaps and Measured Moves

### Gaps as Starting Points
-   Any type of gap (true gaps, body gaps) can serve as **Measured Move (MM)** starting points.
-   A breakout gap often marks the beginning of a new trend phase.

### Trading Strategy
-   **Calculate Targets**: Measure the magnitude of movement before breakout, project that distance upward or downward from gap location to predict potential profit targets.
-   **Trading Behavior**: Traders take profits near MM targets, while counter-trend traders may also enter at these levels, often causing price to stall or reverse at MM targets.

## Summary Principles
-   **Gaps Equal Strength**: All types of gaps represent one side's urgency and strength, signaling possible trend continuation.
-   **Watch Gap Maintenance**: Whether a gap can be maintained (not filled) is key to judging trend health. Continuously filled gaps signal trend exhaustion.
-   **Distinguish Types & Strength**: Micro gaps, gap bars, and body gaps are all important price action signals, with true gaps (tails don't overlap) strongest, body gaps secondary.
-   **Combine with Measured Targets**: Use gaps to identify potential measured move targets as important profit-taking or decision reference points.
