# Wide Stop Strategy

## Core Concept of Wide Stops

-   **Definition**: Place protective stops at positions farther from entry than standard (e.g., signal bar high/low).
-   **Purpose**: Improve win rate by giving trades more room for volatility, avoiding premature stops from normal, temporary counter-trend moves.
-   **Core Trade-off**: Wide stops trade **risk-reward ratio** for **higher win rate**. Risk (stop distance) increases, potential reward-to-risk ratio worsens.
-   **Key Premise**: When using wide stops, **must reduce position size** to ensure total capital risk per trade remains consistent with using narrow stops.

## Scenarios and Reasons for Using Wide Stops

-   **Late Trend or Early Trading Range**: When market may transition from trend to trading range, price action is chaotic, multiple tests of highs/lows are normal. Wide stops can help traders hold through such volatility.
-   **Coping with Potential Second Tests**: In certain patterns (e.g., wedge bottoms), before true reversal, market may make a final test of prior lows. Wide stops can accommodate such second test behavior.
-   **Confident in Direction, Uncertain in Timing**: Traders have strong confidence in market's medium-to-long-term direction but don't pursue precise entry timing. They're willing to bear some floating losses, waiting for market to eventually move in expected direction.
-   **Avoid Being Washed Out in "Traps"**: When trading high-probability failure patterns (e.g., final flag breakout failures), market may briefly break through signal bar high/low forming a trap; wide stops can avoid being stopped out in such traps.

## Methods for Setting Wide Stops

-   **Based on Price Action**:
    -   **Key Swing Start/End Points**: Place stops beyond the starting point of entire key swing (e.g., starting point of a strong breakout swing). This is the most logical method.
    -   **Beyond Measured Move (MM) Targets**: Place stops beyond expected measured move target levels market may reach. Traders bet market will exhaust and reverse after reaching that target.
    -   **Beyond Key Patterns**: For example, when going long a head and shoulders bottom, place stop below the entire pattern's lowest point (head), not just below the right shoulder.
-   **Based on Fixed Value/Volatility**:
    -   **Fixed Ticks**: Set a fixed number of ticks (ticks/pips) as stop distance.
    -   **Volatility**: Use a percentage of average bar size or average true range as stop distance.

## Risk Management and Trading Psychology

-   **Premise Failure Requires Early Exit**: Wide stops are the last line of defense. If market action (e.g., strong, sustained counter-trend breakout) clearly proves your trading premise wrong, should **immediately manually close position**, not passively wait for distant stop to be triggered.
-   **Reduce Pressure for Precise Entry**: For traders not pursuing perfect entry points, wide stops allow more relaxed market entry without worrying about being stopped out by minor moves.
-   **Provide Possibility for Scaling In**: Wide stop strategies are sometimes combined with scaling in. If market moves to a more favorable price without hitting wide stop, traders can add at that point, but this increases total risk and requires stricter position management.

## Summary Principles
-   **Wide Stops Serve High Win Rates**: Their core value is improving trade success probability, but must accept poorer risk-reward ratios.
-   **Position Management Is Foundation**: Using wide stops must be premised on **reducing position size**; otherwise, single-trade risk becomes excessive.
-   **Stop Placement Should Be Logical**: Stops based on key price structure (e.g., swing start, MM target) are more effective than fixed arbitrary ticks.
-   **Wide Stops Don't Mean Hold Forever**: When trading premise is falsified by market, must exit decisively, accepting smaller losses. Wide stops protect against normal volatility, not fundamental errors.
