# Market Cycle (Part 3)

## Pullbacks

### Definition & Evolution
-   **Basic Concept**: Any pause in a trend where traders expect original trend to continue.
-   **Technical Definition**: In uptrend, when a bar's low breaks below prior bar's low, it constitutes a pullback.
-   **Implicit Pullback**: Even without making new low, a counter-color bar in trend (like bear bar in uptrend) is viewed as a pullback on smaller timeframes.
-   **Evolution to Trading Range**: When pullback lasts too long (typically over 20 bars), market direction becomes uncertain; should redefine as "trading range"; original trend reversal probability rises to 50%.

### Complex Pullbacks
-   Even if pullback breaks below the trend leg's start point, if the original trend start was very strong (like consecutive large bull bars), still view as pullback to strong breakout and look for trend resumption signals.

## Trading Ranges

### Identification & Characteristics
-   **Formation Marker**: Usually appears after market experiences "big down then big up" or "big up then big down"; this strong two-way movement confuses traders, leading to sideways consolidation.
-   **Key Duration**: When sideways movement lasts approximately 20 bars or longer, market bulls and bears reach balance; enter neutral "breakout mode."
-   **Breakout Probability**: After entering "breakout mode," probability of breaking up or down is 50% each.

### Trading Strategy
-   **Core Method**: Two-way trading. Use limit orders to buy in lower third of range, sell in upper third.
-   **Market Behavior**: At range top, shorting bears and profit-taking bulls are both selling; at range bottom, long bulls and covering bears are both buying—this reinforces range boundaries.
-   **Note**: In extremely narrow ranges with tiny profit space (like barbed wire), avoid trading; patiently wait for breakout.

## Binary Decision Framework for Trading

### Core Idea
-   **Reject Perfectionism**: No "perfect trades" exist in market; institutions trade heavily on every bar. Goal isn't waiting for perfection but learning to act based on current market state.
-   **Simplify Decisions**: Through series of binary questions, quickly determine market phase, thus selecting appropriate trading strategy.

### Decision Flow & Corresponding Strategies
1.  **Trend or Trading Range?**
2.  **If Trend → Breakout or Channel?**
    -   **Breakout**: Shows strong momentum.
        -   **Strategy**: Only trade in breakout direction. Can enter by market order, breakout limit, limit order, or any method.
3.  **If Channel → Wide Channel or Narrow Channel?**
    -   **Narrow Channel**: Trend very strong; pullbacks extremely shallow (typically only 1-3 bars); bars run tightly along trend line.
        -   **Strategy**: Only trade in trend direction. If uncertain, first assume it's narrow channel.
    -   **Wide Channel**: Pullbacks deeper and more prolonged.
        -   **Strategy**: Two-way trading becomes possible. Main strategy still with-trend trading, but counter-trend scalping also becomes viable. This is usually a sign of trend weakening, possibly turning to trading range.
4.  **Is this a Reversal? → Major Reversal or Minor Reversal?**
    -   **Minor Reversal**: After strong trend (especially narrow channel), first reversal attempt is likely minor; more likely to evolve into pullback structure (like flag) or trading range than new trend.
        -   **Strategy**: Treat such counter-trend moves as scalping (take profits and exit).

## Summary Principles
-   **Time Distinguishes State**: Key difference between pullbacks and trading ranges is duration. "Pullbacks" exceeding 20 bars should be viewed as entering 50/50 probability trading range.
-   **Binary Decisions Determine Strategy**: Actively use binary framework (Breakout? Channel? Wide/Narrow? Range?) to quickly determine current market state, matching correct trading method.
-   **State Determines Direction**:
    -   **One-Way Trading Only**: Strong breakouts, narrow channels.
    -   **Two-Way Trading Possible**: Wide channels, trading ranges.
-   **Everything is Cycle**: Any chart's price action consists of breakouts, channels, and trading ranges, constantly cycling and evolving.
