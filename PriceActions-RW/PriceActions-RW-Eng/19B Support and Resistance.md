# Support and Resistance (Part 2)

## Which Support and Resistance is Most Effective?
- **Depends on Specific Market Context**: Any type of support or resistance, under specific market conditions and price structure, may become the most important one at that moment.
- There is no fixed "best" support/resistance type.

## Support and Resistance Characteristics in Forex Markets
- **Relative Imprecision (Less Precise)**
  - **Main Reason**: Forex markets lack a unified central exchange.
    - Global banks, brokers, and platforms have slight price differences (usually 1-2 pips).
    - Different data feeds may show slightly different bar highs/lows/closes (e.g., comparing Interactive Brokers and TradeStation historical data).
    - Some platforms' bar charts may be based on "Bid", while others may be based on "Mid-price".
  - **HFT Arbitrage**: Although HFT arbitrage behavior makes prices across platforms tend to converge, it cannot completely eliminate differences.
- **Impact on Trading Practice**
  - Traders usually don't obsess over a precise tick-level price point.
  - More flexibility in bar chart type selection: time charts (e.g., 5-minute, 15-minute), tick charts, volume charts can all be used to analyze price action, with similar core structures revealed.
  - Trend lines and channel lines being "pierced" or "not touched" may be more common than in stock index futures, requiring more flexible drawing and adjustment of these lines, focusing on areas the market "respects" rather than precise lines.
- **Future Trend Outlook**
  - As algorithmic trading and large hedge funds increasingly participate in forex markets, mathematical models and precise price point trading logic will become more important, and the precision of support and resistance is expected to gradually improve.

## Support and Resistance Examples Review (Primarily Forex Market Charts)
- **Moving Average (EMA20)**: Acts as dynamic support or resistance areas, especially in trending markets.
- **Trend Lines and Channel Lines**
  - Need dynamic adjustment when drawing, connecting key reversal highs or lows, looking for "effective" lines that the market repeatedly tests and reacts to.
  - Breakout or reversal signals near trend lines or channel lines are common trading opportunities.
- **Prior Highs and Lows**
  - Form double tops and double bottoms. These patterns are rarely perfect in real markets (i.e., two tops/bottoms at exactly the same level).
  - They can be major trend reversal signals, or part of pullbacks/bounces in trends (such as double top bear flags).
- **Measured Move (MM) Targets**
  - Even in relatively "imprecise" markets like forex, MM targets based on breakout magnitude are still effective, but allow for some error range.
- **Breakout Pullbacks**
  - When price breaks through a key support or resistance, it often retests the breakout point.
  - Original support may transform into resistance after being broken; original resistance may transform into support after being broken.
  - Traders look for entry opportunities when price retests support/resistance and shows reversal signals (e.g., going short when prior support is broken and the bounce retest fails).
- **Wedge Patterns**
  - **Wedge Flags**: Such as three-push pullback attempts in uptrends (wedge bull flags).
  - **Nested Wedges**: Large wedges containing small wedges, which may enhance signal reliability.
  - **Note Narrow Channel Wedges**: If wedge patterns are too narrow (i.e., very small pullback/bounce magnitude), reversal strength may be limited, more likely to evolve into trading ranges or trend continuation flags rather than major reversals.

## Core Trading Principles
- **Accept "Good Enough" Patterns (Good Enough is Good Enough)**: Price patterns in real markets are rarely as perfect as in textbooks. Don't miss reasonable trading opportunities by pursuing perfection.
- **Reasonable Stop Placement**
  - **Wide Stops vs. Narrow Stops**:
    - Narrow stops: May have higher loss frequency, but smaller loss amount per trade.
    - Wide stops: May have lower loss frequency, but larger loss amount per trade.
    - As long as stops are set reasonably (e.g., on the other side of the signal bar, or beyond important prior highs/lows), in the long run, the total profit/loss results of both methods may be similar. The key is consistency and reasonableness, avoiding stops that are too narrow or too wide.
- **Wait for Signal Confirmation**: At potential support or resistance levels, wait for the market to give clear reversal bar signals (such as strong bull/bear bars) or breakout signals before acting.

**Core Summary of This Section:**
1. The effectiveness of support and resistance depends on market context.
2. Forex market support/resistance is relatively imprecise due to its decentralized nature, but core price action patterns still apply.
3. Be flexible when trading, focus on "good enough" patterns, and set stops reasonably.
