# Introduction to Breakouts

## Core Definition of a Breakout

-   **Concept**: A breakout consists of one or series of trend bars (i.e., large bull/bear bars) whose price strongly exceeds a support or resistance level.
-   **Breakout Targets**:
    -   Highs/lows of prior bar(s).
    -   Trend lines or channel lines.
    -   Moving Average (EMA20).
    -   Prior important highs or lows (Swing High/Low).
-   **Universality**: Every large trend bar on chart is a breakout. The more support/resistance levels broken, the stronger the signal.
-   **Bar Tails**: Upper and lower tails of each bar represent failed breakout attempts on smaller timeframes.

## Reversals are Breakouts

-   **Internal Connection**: Every reversal attempt is essentially a counter-direction breakout attempt.
-   **Example**: A strong large bear bar after a rally is both a signal of failed bull breakout and the beginning of a bear breakout (reversal).
-   **Analytical Perspective**: Should analyze reversal bars as breakout bars, focusing on their strength, closing position, and subsequent bar confirmation.

## Market Inertia: The 80% Rule

-   **Core Idea**: Market has strong inertia, tending to maintain current state; approximately 80% of change attempts fail.
-   **Specific Applications**:
    -   **Trading Range**: Approximately 80% of upward or downward breakout attempts fail; price returns to range.
    -   **Trend**: Approximately 80% of reversal attempts fail; original trend continues.
-   **Trading Insight**: This is the foundation of price action analysis. Before trading, first determine whether market is in trend or range, and assume current state will persist.

## Trading Strategy for Breakouts in a Range

-   **Core Strategy**: Based on "80% of breakouts fail" principle, counter-trade (Fading) at trading range boundaries.
-   **Specific Operations**:
    -   **Sell High**: When price surges to range top with strong bull bar (Buying Climax), view as trap, look for short opportunities.
    -   **Buy Low**: When price crashes to range bottom with strong bear bar (Selling Climax), view as trap, look for long opportunities.
-   **Entry Methods**:
    -   **Aggressive**: Directly counter-enter at strong breakout bar's close.
    -   **Conservative**: Wait for reversal signal bar to appear, then enter in reversal direction.
-   **Trading Target**: Since expecting market to continue ranging, main target is scalping for short-term profits.

## Summary Principles
-   **Breakouts Everywhere**: Any strong trend bar is a breakout; reversals are also a form of breakout.
-   **Inertia is Key**: Remember the 80% rule—most breakouts from trading ranges fail; most reversals in trends fail.
-   **Context Determines Strategy**: In trading ranges, should think counter-trend, using failed breakouts for fade-extreme scalping rather than chasing.
-   **Confirmation Supreme**: Breakout strength and subsequent follow-through bars crucial. Poor follow-through is strong signal of breakout failure. If uncertain, best choice is to wait.
