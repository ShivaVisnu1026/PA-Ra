# Buying and Selling Pressure (Part 2)

## Buying/Selling Pressure Analysis Within Trading Ranges

### Assessing Breakout Direction
-   **Core Logic**: By analyzing the contrast between buying and selling pressure within trading ranges, determine the likelihood of upward or downward breakout.
-   **Increased Buying Pressure Signs (Predicting Upward Breakout)**:
    -   Bull bars increasing in number, size, and consecutive appearance.
    -   Bull bars closing high.
    -   Gaps appearing upward (bar close or low higher than prior high).
    -   Forming early bull channel (higher highs and higher lows).
-   **Increased Selling Pressure Signs (Predicting Downward Breakout)**:
    -   Bear bars increasing in number, size, and consecutive appearance.
    -   Bear bars closing low.
    -   Gaps appearing downward (bar close or high lower than prior low).
    -   Forming early bear channel (lower highs and lower lows).
-   **Pressure Transition**: Watch dynamic changes in buying/selling pressure within range. For example, early strong buying pressure followed by smaller bull bars, more and consecutive bear bars indicates strengthening selling pressure, increasing downward breakout probability.
-   **Combine with Patterns**: Buying/selling pressure analysis can be combined with reversal patterns within range (like head and shoulders top, lower high major reversal) for judgment.

## Buying/Selling Pressure Analysis Within Channels

### "Spike and Channel to Trading Range"
-   **Common Pattern**: Market experiences a rapid rise/fall (Spike), then enters a relatively gradual channel in the same direction (Channel).
-   **Pressure Assessment Within Channel**:
    -   **Bull Channel**: Watch for strengthening **selling pressure**. Signs include: bear bars increasing/enlarging, bull bars with poor follow-through (dojis, small bars, bear bars), deeper pullbacks, more bar overlap. If selling pressure strengthens, channel may evolve into trading range or even break downward.
    -   **Bear Channel**: Watch for strengthening **buying pressure**. Signs include: bull bars increasing/enlarging, bear bars with poor follow-through, higher rallies, bars with long lower tails (bear scalper signs), failed bear breakouts (breakout quickly reversed by bull bars). If buying pressure strengthens, channel may evolve into trading range or even break upward.
-   **Channel Breakout Probability**:
    -   **Bull Channel**: 75% probability of breaking **downward** (evolving into range or reversal), only 25% probability of accelerating upward.
    -   **Bear Channel**: 75% probability of breaking **upward** (evolving into range or reversal), only 25% probability of accelerating downward.

### Trading Application
-   **Caution With With-Trend Trading**: When channel shows obvious accumulation of counter pressure, be alert to with-trend trading risks; consider reducing positions or looking for reversal signals.
-   **Reversal Trading Opportunities**: When channel strongly breaks out (strong bars + good follow-through), it's an opportunity for reversal trading (or at least range trading).
-   **Buying/Selling Pressure Guides Entry and Stops**:
    -   **Entry**: Enter after confirming strengthening buy/sell pressure and breakout signal (like entering on breakout bull/bear bar close, or waiting for follow-through confirmation).
    -   **Stops**: Can place stops below/above the recent breakout's start point.

## Summary
-   Continuously assessing relative strength of buying/selling pressure within trading ranges and channels is key to predicting market's next move.
-   Accumulation and transition of buying/selling pressure typically predict direction of range breakouts or channel evolution.
-   Understanding channel breakout probability characteristics helps formulate more reasonable trading strategies (cautious with-trend, watch for reversals).
