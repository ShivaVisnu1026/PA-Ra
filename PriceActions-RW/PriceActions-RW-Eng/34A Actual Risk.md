# Actual Risk vs. Initial Risk (Part 1)

## Initial Risk

### Definition and Characteristics
-   **Concept**: The distance from trade entry price to the trader's initially set stop level.
-   **Subjectivity**: Initial risk varies by person. Different traders choose different stop placements based on their strategies and risk tolerance.
-   **Common Stop Strategies**:
    -   **Price Action Stop**: Place stop below key price structure points, such as signal bar lows, important swing lows, etc.
    -   **Fixed-Amount/Tick Stop**: Set a fixed loss amount or number of ticks (ticks/pips).
    -   **Measured Move Stop**: Set stops based on measured distance of price movement.

## Actual Risk

### Definition and Identification
-   **Concept**: A risk determined after the fact. It refers to the most unfavorable retracement distance from entry price before price **effectively breaks out** in the favorable direction.
-   **Identification**:
    -   Only after price moves in expected direction and forms clear breakout can you look back and determine actual risk size.
    -   It represents the theoretically perfect stop distance that would allow you to **"just avoid being stopped out."**
    -   Common understanding: The maximum floating loss the account experiences before the trade ultimately becomes profitable.
-   **Objectivity**: Unlike subjective "initial risk," for all traders entering at the same price, once the lowest point of price pullback forms, "actual risk" is objective and identical.

## Setting Profit Targets Based on Risk

### Core Principle: Positive Mathematical Expectation
-   **Trading Foundation**: Only trade when mathematical expectation is positive, i.e., `(win rate × potential profit) > (loss rate × risk)`.
-   **Universal Rule**: For most trades with win rates not exceeding 60%, pursuing **2 times risk** profit target is a robust strategy ensuring positive mathematical expectation.

### Two Methods for Setting Profit Targets
1.  **Based on Initial Risk**:
    -   **Application**: When opening position, calculate 2 times distance as profit target based on your set initial risk.
    -   **Advantages**: Simple, easy to execute, ensures consistency in trading logic.

2.  **Based on Actual Risk**:
    -   **Application**: After price moves as expected, calculate 1 times or 2 times profit target based on determined actual risk distance.
    -   **Institutional Behavior**: Many algorithms and institutional traders use actual risk to set targets, making these target levels important **support/resistance levels** in the market. For example, at one times actual risk target, pullbacks often occur due to large profit-taking.
    -   **Strategy**:
        -   **High Win Rate Trades (e.g., >60%)**: Pursuing **1 times actual risk** profit is reasonable.
        -   **Normal Trades (40-60%)**: Pursuing **2 times actual risk** profit is mathematically robust.

## Trade Management and Summary Principles

-   **Distinguish Two Types of Risk**: Clearly understand the difference between **initial risk** (pre-trade plan, subjective) and **actual risk** (post-trade reality, objective).
-   **Utilize Actual Risk**: Use "actual risk" as an important market analysis tool to set profit targets closer to market dynamics and predict potential support/resistance areas.
-   **Flexible Exit**: When trading premise changes (e.g., after buying, market forms clear top reversal pattern), consider exiting early, even if price hasn't reached original profit target.
-   **Mathematics Is Foundation**: Whether using initial risk or actual risk, the core is building positive mathematical expectation to formulate trading plans.
