# Trading in Single-Sided Trends (Part 3)

## Trading Strategies in a Single-Leg Trend

### Core Concept
-   **Trade With-Trend**: When market is in clear single-sided trend (Spike/Channel), traders should trade in trend direction.
-   **"Enter for Any Reason"**: Once trend confirmed, reliability of any entry signal increases. Traders can enter at market, enter on bar close, or enter on pullback—key is choosing direction aligned with trend.

### Risk Management
-   **Position Control**: Use sufficiently small position size to ensure even if stop hit, loss is within acceptable range.
-   **Reasonable Stops**: Place stops beyond reasonable swing highs/lows (like start of strong breakout).

## Scalping vs. Swing Trading

### Scalping
-   **Definition**: In strong trends, make multiple quick entries and exits for small profits.
-   **Method**:
    -   In downtrends, sell on bear bar close or sell rallies.
    -   In uptrends, buy on bull bar close or buy pullbacks.
-   **Challenges**:
    -   **High Psychological and Execution Difficulty**: Requires traders to decisively enter and exit repeatedly.
    -   **Easy to Miss Opportunities**: Most traders struggle to catch all opportunities or exit too early, missing most of the move.
    -   **Limited Actual Returns**: Despite theoretically many opportunities, due to management difficulty, actual total profits often less than swing trading.

### Swing Trading
-   **Definition**: After entry, hold position until clear, strong counter-trend signal appears before exiting; aim to capture most of trend move.
-   **Advantages**:
    -   **Fewer Decisions, Simpler Management**: Only need to make one entry decision and one exit decision.
    -   **Higher Potential Returns**: In strong trends, one successful swing trade profit typically far exceeds sum of multiple scalp trades.
-   **Conclusion**: **For most traders, in clear single-sided trends, swing trading is superior, easier to profit choice.**

## Identifying Trends and Reversal Signals

### Trend Confirmation Signals
-   **Strong Breakout Bar**: A bar with huge body whose close far exceeds recent (like past 10-20) bar range.
-   **Consecutive Trend Bars**: Multiple consecutive bars with closes at their extremes (bull bars closing high, bear bars closing low).

### Key Reversal Patterns
-   **Wedge Pattern**: Three-push exhaustion pattern; important reversal signal. Sometimes third push cannot make new high/low due to opposing force being too strong.
-   **Double Bottom/Top**: Classic reversal pattern.
-   **Major Trend Reversal (Higher Low/Lower High)**: A larger structure composed of multiple bars indicating trend may fundamentally change.

## Multi-Timeframe Perspective

-   **Phenomenon**: A single-sided trend on large timeframe (like 60-minute chart) may appear as complex move containing pullbacks or even brief counter-trends on small timeframe (like 5-minute chart).
-   **Trader Choice**:
    -   **Multi-Timeframe**: Traders can use small timeframe signals to manage large timeframe positions (like early exit or short counter-trend trades).
    -   **Focus on Single Timeframe**: Instructor (Al Brooks) suggests that to avoid confusion, best to focus on one trading timeframe for analysis and decisions.

## Summary Principles
-   **Trend is King**: In single-sided trends, with-trend trading has highest win rate.
-   **Swing Better Than Scalping**: For most people, swing trading in strong trends is simpler and more profitable than frequent scalping.
-   **Identify Key Signals**: Learning to identify strong trend signals and reliable reversal patterns like wedges, double tops/bottoms is key to deciding when to enter and exit.
-   **Stay Focused**: Focusing on price action of single timeframe can simplify trading decisions and improve efficiency.
