# Mathematical Expectation and Probability (Part Four)

## 50% Pullbacks in Strong Trends

### Core Concept and Probability
-   **Definition**: After a strong trend move (whether rally or decline), market pulls back to approximately **50%** of that move.
-   **Core Probability**: In this situation, market has approximately **60%** probability of resuming original trend and at least **testing the prior high/low**, rather than first hitting the trend starting point stop.
-   **Trading Foundation**: This 60% high win rate provides trend traders with reliable probability edge.

### Trading Mathematical Expectation
-   **Reward/Risk Ratio**:
    -   **Risk**: Stop is usually placed just beyond the trend leg's **starting point**.
    -   **Reward**: Minimum target is the prior **high/low** of the trend.
    -   **Ratio**: Risk and reward distances are roughly equal, i.e., reward/risk ratio approximately **1:1**.
-   **Positive Expectation**: Although reward/risk is only 1:1, with **60% high win rate**, this trade's mathematical expectation is **positive**, making it a reasonable trade worth executing.
-   **Psychological Trap**: Even if pullback/bounce looks very strong, seeming to reverse the trend, as long as it stays around the 50% level, it's more likely a **trap** tempting traders to counter-trend trade.

### Trading Strategy
-   **Entry Methods**:
    -   **Place limit order** at 50% level.
    -   Wait for pullback to end, enter after **reversal signal bar** appears.
-   **Applicability**: This principle applies to various markets and timeframes, whether stocks, forex, or futures.

## Evolving Trader's Equation in Strong Breakouts

### Initial Breakout Trade
-   **Entry**: When a very strong breakout bar appears (e.g., large full-bodied bull/bear bar, close near extreme), can enter at its **closing price**.
-   **Initial Mathematical Expectation**:
    -   **Risk**: Place stop at the other end of breakout bar (e.g., when long, stop below bar low).
    -   **Reward**: Minimum target is **equal-distance Measured Move (MM)** calculated based on initial risk size, i.e., achieving 1:1 reward/risk ratio.
    -   **Probability**: Due to strong breakout, probability of reaching this minimum target is similarly as high as **60%**, making this trade's mathematical expectation positive.

### Holding and Expectation Optimization
-   **Core Idea**: For traders who enter **early** in breakouts and choose to **hold**, their mathematical expectation becomes **better and better** as the breakout continues.
-   **Dynamic Evolution**:
    -   **Fixed Risk**: Trader's initial risk remains unchanged.
    -   **Stable Probability**: Probability of market continuing toward new, further MM targets roughly maintains at **60%**.
    -   **Growing Profit**: As breakout continues, price moves further from entry, new MM target levels also move further, causing potential **profit space to expand dramatically**.
-   **Trading Insight**:
    -   In strong breakouts, entering early and holding patiently can maximize trade's mathematical expectation.
    -   Latecomers can still find positive-expectation entry points, but their risk is higher, reward/risk ratio not as good as early entrants.
    -   When reaching MM targets calculated based on latest bars, many traders usually take profits, causing market pullback or consolidation.

## Summary Principles
-   **Utilize 50% Pullbacks**: 50% pullbacks/bounces in strong trends are high-probability (approximately 60%) trend trade opportunities; their positive mathematical expectation makes them reliable trade setups.
-   **Hold in Strong Breakouts**: In strong breakout moves, early entrants' mathematical expectation continuously optimizes as trend continues. Risk is fixed, potential profit continuously grows; should hold to capture larger profits.
-   **Probability is Foundation**: Whether pullbacks or breakouts, trading decisions should be based on understanding probability. Utilizing 60% high-probability edge is the core of building positive-expectation trading systems.
