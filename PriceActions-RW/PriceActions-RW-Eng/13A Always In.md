# Single-Sided Trends (Part 1)

## Basic Market State: Trend vs. Trading Range

-   **Binary State**: Any market at any timeframe is in only one of two states: **Trend** or **Trading Range**.
-   **Market Inertia**: Market tends to maintain current state. In trends, approximately **80% of reversal attempts fail**; in trading ranges, approximately 80% of breakout attempts fail.
-   **Basic Trading Strategy**:
    -   **In Trends**: Must trade in trend direction.
    -   **In Ranges**: Sell near range top, buy near range bottom for reversal trading.
    -   **When Uncertain**: If chart looks messy, unable to determine direction, assume it's in trading range.

## Definition & Trading Logic of Single-Sided Trends

### Concept
-   **Definition**: Refers to market phases where directionality is so strong that traders **should only consider one direction** (long or short) for trading. At this time, probability of making money with-trend far exceeds counter-trend.
-   **"Must Be In" Thinking**: Imagine if you must always hold a position, which direction would you choose? This direction is the single-sided trend direction.

### Trading Principles
-   **Only Trade With-Trend**: In clear single-sided trends (like narrow channels), should abandon counter-trend ideas; only look for with-trend entry opportunities.
-   **Don't Over-Wait for Pullbacks**: In extremely strong trends, pullbacks may be very shallow or even non-existent. Waiting for deep pullbacks may cause missing entire move. Market buying or buying on any signal is reasonable.
-   **From Trend to Range**: When consolidation (pullback) after a trend exceeds **20 bars**, market enters 50/50 state, but probability of resuming original trend still slightly higher. Before clear reversal signal appears, should prioritize following original trend direction.

## Identifying Single-Sided Trend Start

-   **Key is "Surprise"**: Single-sided trends usually start with an unexpected, powerful price movement that traps counter-trend traders and attracts with-trend traders to chase.
-   **Start Formations**:
    1.  **Single Super Bar**: One bar with huge body, far exceeding recent bars—bull or bear—sufficient to change market expectations.
    2.  **Two Strong Bars**: Two consecutive strong, same-direction bars with good closes.
    3.  **Multiple Consecutive Bars**: 3-6 or more consecutive small same-direction bars; cumulative effect equals one large bar.
-   **Psychological Effects**:
    -   **Trapped Traders Exit**: Counter-trend traders (like shorting in bull breakout) recognize error, hope to reduce losses on any pullback (bears buy).
    -   **Non-Participants Chase**: With-trend traders who missed start eager to enter on any pullback (bulls buy).
    -   **Dual Force**: These two forces combine, jointly pushing price to continue in trend direction.

## Trader's Equation: Mathematical Foundation of Trading

-   **Core Three Elements**: All trading decisions based on tradeoff between three variables:
    -   **Risk**: Stop distance.
    -   **Reward**: Target distance.
    -   **Probability**: Likelihood of reaching target before stop.
-   **Application in Single-Sided Trends**:
    -   In single-sided uptrends, probability of price rising one unit before falling same unit is **significantly above 50%** (like 60%).
    -   This means even pursuing reward equal to risk (1:1 risk/reward ratio), trade has **positive mathematical expectation**, is profitable.
    -   Therefore, trading with single-sided trend aligns with mathematical logic.

## Summary Principles
-   **Determine Direction**: Before trading, first determine whether market is in trend or range, and identify single-sided trend direction.
-   **Identify Start Signals**: Focus on "surprise" bars or bar combinations sufficient to change market expectations; they are the beginning of single-sided trends.
-   **Trade With-Trend**: In single-sided trends, with-trend trading has higher probability, easier to make money.
-   **Decide Based on Mathematics**: Understand and apply "Trader's Equation" logic (Risk, Reward, Probability) to ensure trading strategy has positive expected value.
