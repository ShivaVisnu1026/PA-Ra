# Gaps: Trend Strength & Stepping Pattern

## Unfilled Gaps: A Sign of Trend Strength

### Identifying "Almost No Pullback/Rally" Trends
-   **Core Characteristic**: Trend shows a series of gaps that remain open (unfilled). Pullback/rally bars fail to touch prior breakout point prices.
-   **Formation**: Even if trend appears composed of small bars lacking consecutive large bull/bear bars, as long as gaps persist, it indicates a very strong trend ("almost no pullback/rally trend").
-   **Psychology**: One market side completely controls the situation while the other side is powerless, causing extremely weak rallies/pullbacks.

### Trading Implications
-   **Firmly With-Trend**: In such markets, the only reasonable strategy is with-trend trading. Don't attempt any counter-trend operations.
-   **Signal Interpretation**: Large bars appearing in late trend are more likely **measuring gaps** (predicting trend will double the distance) rather than **exhaustion gaps** (predicting reversal).
-   **Mindset**: Trust the trend's strength. Such trends' duration often exceeds traders' expectations. On larger timeframe charts, this pattern typically manifests as a clear breakout.

## Filled Gaps: The Stepping Pattern & Trend Weakness

### Definition & Identification (Stepping Pattern)
-   **Core Characteristic**: Breakout gaps in trend quickly get filled by subsequent bars. After price breaks prior lows/highs, it quickly reverses back above/below the breakout point.
-   **Market State**: This is called the "Stepping Pattern," a clear signal that trend is weakening.

### Trader Behavior Transformation
-   **Counter-Trend Side Becomes Active**: In downtrend stepping pattern, bulls start buying low at and below prior lows for scalping, believing price will rally.
-   **With-Trend Side Becomes Cautious**: Bears no longer chase downward breakouts but shift to selling rallies or taking profits at new lows.
-   **Key Signal**: When counter-trend traders (like bulls in downtrend) can consistently profit, it proves the original trend has serious problems.

### Market Structure Evolution
-   **From Channel to Range**: Stepping pattern appearance typically predicts market will evolve from a strong tight channel to wide channel or trading range.
-   **Increased Reversal Possibility**: As stepping pattern continues, probability of market eventually reversing into new trend greatly increases.

## Summary Principles
-   **Gaps are Trend's Touchstone**: Whether gaps can remain open (unfilled) is key to judging trend strength.
-   **Unfilled Gaps = Strong Trend**: Should firmly trade with trend, not counter-trend. Such trends have strong persistence.
-   **Filled Gaps = Stepping Pattern = Weak Trend**: Original trend is exhausting, market may enter range or reversal. Counter-trend scalping becomes viable; with-trend trading needs more caution.
-   **Observe Trader Behavior**: Through whether gaps get filled, can judge the power contrast and strategy changes between with-trend and counter-trend sides, adjusting your own trading plan accordingly.
