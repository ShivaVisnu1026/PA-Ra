# Forex Fundamentals (Part 4): Charts & Trading Styles

## Approximate Nature of Forex Charts

### Why Charts Are Not Exact
-   **Data Source Differences**: Different trading platforms (brokers) use different banking data sources, so their candlestick charts, while very close, are not completely identical.
-   **Platform-Internal Trading**: Charts typically only reflect that platform's own trading activity, not the full global market picture.
-   **Different Charting Methods**: Platforms have multiple choices when drawing candles, leading to potentially different bar formations for the same time period.
    -   **Based on Ask Price**: All price points (high, open, low, close) on the chart are based on the seller's asking price.
    -   **Based on Bid Price**: All chart prices are based on the buyer's bid.
    -   **Based on Midpoint**: Using the middle price between bid and ask to draw charts, like Interactive Brokers (IB) default setting.

### Trading Execution Implications
-   **Chart vs. Execution Price Differences**: Because chart prices (like ask charts) and actual execution prices (like bid when buying) differ, the following situations occur:
    -   **Execution Outside Chart Range**: On an ask chart, your buy execution price (bid) may be higher than the bar's high, making this trade appear not to have occurred on the chart.
    -   **Orders Not Filled**: On an ask chart, even if the bar's low drops below your limit buy order position, the order may not fill because the actual bid never touched your order.
-   **Core Insight**: View forex candlestick charts as an **approximate guide** to the market, not an absolutely precise record. Don't obsess over minor price differences, especially when executing orders.

## Scalping vs. Swing Trading

### Definition & Characteristics
-   **Concept Ambiguity**: In actual forex trading, the boundary between the two is unclear; many traders call both simply "trading." The core distinction lies in **trading intent and management approach**.
-   **Scalping**:
    -   **Holding Time**: Extremely short, typically 1-5 bars.
    -   **Profit Target**: Pursuing quick small profits (like 5-20 pips).
    -   **Risk Tolerance**: Almost no tolerance for profit retracement; exit immediately once profitable or if conditions turn.
-   **Swing Trading**:
    -   **Holding Time**: Longer, possibly lasting dozens of bars.
    -   **Trading Premise**: Hold as long as the original trading logic (premise) remains valid.
    -   **Risk Tolerance**: Willing to endure price pullbacks and minor reversal attempts to capture larger market moves.
    -   **Exit Timing**: Only close position when clear exit signals appear (like key reversal patterns, end of trading session).

### Trading Examples & Applications
-   **Intent Determines Type**: Even if a "swing trade" ultimately only profits 18 pips, if the trader maintained the trading logic throughout and endured drawdown, it's still swing trading.
-   **Cross-Timeframe Applicable**: These concepts apply to all timeframes. On a 60-minute chart, a trade held for 1-2 bars targeting 20-50 pips can also be considered "scalping."

## Summary Principles
-   **Accept Chart Imperfection**: Understand that forex candlestick charts have approximations due to different data sources and charting methods, directly affecting order execution and display.
-   **Clarify Your Trading Style**: Scalping is opportunity-driven quick in-and-out, while swing trading is logic-driven patient holding. Manage trades according to your style.
-   **Adjust Trading Mindset**: Don't be troubled by minor differences between charts and executions. Focus on trading logic and price action itself.
