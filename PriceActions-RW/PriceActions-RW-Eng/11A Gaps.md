# Gaps (Part 1)

## Al Brooks' Broad Definition of Gaps

### Core Concept
-   **Definition**: A gap is the "space" between current price and any significant prior support or resistance level. This definition is far broader than traditional gap definitions.
-   **Support/Resistance Examples**:
    -   Prior bar highs or lows
    -   Bar opening or closing prices
    -   Moving Averages (MA)
    -   Trend lines or channel lines

### Trading Philosophy
-   **Gaps Equal Momentum**: The core idea of this broad definition is that gaps represent strong momentum. They show the market believes current price is "wrong" and needs rapid correction, leaving space between price and a reference point.

## Traditional Gap Types & Significance

### Definition
-   **Up-Gap**: A bar's low is higher than the previous bar's high.
-   **Down-Gap**: A bar's high is lower than the previous bar's low.
-   **Prevalence**: Very common on stock daily charts but relatively rare in high-liquidity markets (like forex) or short timeframes (like 5-minute charts).

### Main Types & Interpretation
-   **Breakaway Gap**:
    -   **Characteristics**: Appears at the beginning of a new trend.
    -   **Note**: Difficult to judge in real-time because at that moment it may just be a meaningless gap within a trading range, only confirmed as breakaway in hindsight.
-   **Measuring Gap (Runaway Gap)**:
    -   **Characteristics**: Appears mid-trend, typically marking the halfway point of the trend.
    -   **Application**: Can be used to predict trend targets (Measured Move, MM). Measure distance from trend start to gap, project that distance from gap upward (or downward) to estimate target.
-   **Exhaustion Gap**:
    -   **Characteristics**: Appears at trend's end (like after 20-40 bars), typically the largest bar in the trend, and quickly "filled" (price reverses back into gap area).
    -   **Meaning**: Signals trend strength exhausted, market may soon enter range or reversal rather than new acceleration.
-   **Common Gap**:
    -   **Characteristics**: Appears frequently within trading ranges.
    -   **Meaning**: Usually has no predictive value and gets filled quickly.

## Gaps and Trend Bars Equivalence

### Core Idea
-   **Gap = Trend Bar**: A large gap and one or several strong trend bars are equivalent in trading terms.
-   **Internal Logic**: Both represent market moving quickly and directionally in a short time, reflecting strong buyer or seller pressure. One shows as space, the other as bar bodies, but the momentum information behind them is identical.

### Trading Application
-   View every strong trend bar as a "breakout," a "climax," and recognize it creates a gap in the broad sense (its close is far from prior support/resistance).
-   Therefore, analyze a large bull/bear bar using the same method as analyzing a gap (determining if it's breakaway, measuring, or exhaustion).

## Island Reversals

### Definition
-   **Island Top**: Market gaps up, trades for one or more bars, then gaps down.
-   **Island Bottom**: Market gaps down, trades, then gaps up.
-   **Al Brooks' View**: He's flexible with the definition, believing the "island" can consist of many bars and the two gaps needn't be at the same level.

### Trading Value
-   **Not Special Signal**: Island reversals aren't more important or reliable than any other form of strong reversal pattern.
-   **Focus on Essence**: The key is identifying strong reversal of market momentum, not the "island" label itself. Most reversal attempts fail, and island reversals are no exception.

## Summary Principles
-   **Adopt Broad Definition**: Understanding gaps as any space between price and key support/resistance helps capture market momentum.
-   **View Equivalently**: Strong trend bars (or bar combinations) functionally equal gaps; analyze with same logic.
-   **Context Determines Meaning**: Gap nature (breakaway, measuring, exhaustion) depends on its position in overall market structure (early, mid, or late trend).
-   **Use MM Targets**: Use measuring gaps/breakout bars as basis for calculating Measured Move (MM) targets for profit-taking.
-   **Be Alert to Late Trend Signals**: In mature trend's later stages, large gaps or the largest trend bar are likely exhaustion signals, not strength continuation.
