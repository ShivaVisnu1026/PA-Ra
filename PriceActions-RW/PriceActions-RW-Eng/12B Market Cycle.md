# Market Cycle: Trends, Trading Ranges & Key Terminology

## Market State: Trend vs. Trading Range

### Basic Definitions
-   **Core Viewpoint**: Market is always in only one of two states: **Trend** or **Trading Range**.
-   **Trend**: Price continuously moves in one direction, showing series of rising highs and lows (uptrend) or falling highs and lows (downtrend).
-   **Trading Range**: Price moves sideways, lacking clear direction. Its interior often contains seemingly reasonable long and short signals simultaneously, causing market direction ambiguity.

## Market Inertia: The 80% Rule

### Concept
-   Market has strong **inertia**, tending to continue its current behavior pattern. To predict future movement, the best reference is recent movement.

### Application
-   **In Trends**: 80% of reversal attempts fail and evolve into pullbacks within trend (like bear flags or bull flags); original trend continues.
-   **In Trading Ranges**: 80% of breakout attempts fail; price reverses back into range.

## Relationship Between Pullbacks & Trading Ranges

### Time Dimension Conversion
-   **Core Relationship**: Pullbacks and trading ranges are the same phenomenon at different timeframes.
    -   All **pullbacks** seen on current timeframe are **trading ranges** on smaller timeframes.
    -   All **trading ranges** seen on current timeframe may just be **pullbacks** on larger timeframes.

### Probability Evolution (The 20-Bar Rule)
-   When pullbacks (pauses) in trends last too long (like **over 20 bars**), they evolve from "pullback" to "trading range."
-   At this point, market directionality weakens; bulls and bears tend toward balance. Original trend resumption probability and trend reversal probability become approximately **50/50**; market enters **Breakout Mode**, either direction possible.

## Key Terminology Definitions

Al Brooks categorizes market movement sizes hierarchically for precise description:

-   **Leg**: Most basic movement unit; refers to any rise or fall on chart, even if just one bar.
-   **Swing**: A "leg" large enough for traders to capture substantial swing profits (typically 3-5 times scalping profits), usually lasting 10-20 bars.
-   **Trend**: A "swing" that occupies most of the screen, very strong. If a screen shows three or more "trends," market is actually wide range; should call them "swings."
-   **Pullback**: A counter-directional "leg" in a trend, with market expected to resume original trend.

## Trend Structure: Major vs. Minor Highs & Lows

### Definition & Identification
-   **Major High/Low**: A high or low followed by a **strong breakout** typically making new highs/lows. This is a key point confirming trend health.
-   **Minor High/Low**: A high or low not followed by strong breakout. They are usually part of trading ranges or complex pullbacks.

### Trading Implications
-   **Stop Placement**: With-trend traders should place stops beyond the most recent **major** high/low.
-   **Trend Assessment**: As long as price remains protected within major highs/lows, original trend is likely intact. Breaking a **minor** high/low doesn't mean trend ended, but breaking a **major** high/low means trend may have ended or turned to trading range.

## Summary Principles
-   **Binary State**: Always determine whether market is in trend or trading range.
-   **Trust Inertia**: Use 80% rule to guide trading—trade with trend; fade extremes in trading ranges.
-   **Watch Pullback Duration**: Be alert to "pullbacks" exceeding 20 bars; they've become 50/50 trading ranges.
-   **Distinguish Key Levels**: Identify trend health and set reasonable stops by recognizing "major" vs. "minor" highs/lows.
