# Starting Your Trading Career (Part 1)

## Common Myths for New Traders

### Myth 1: There Exists a Perfect "Holy Grail" Strategy
-   **False Belief**: Many beginners believe there exists some secret, low-risk, high-win-rate "golden goose" perfect strategy in the market.
-   **Reality**: There is no perfect trade or strategy. For any trade, there are smart institutions on the other side. Trading is competing with the world's best traders—there are no secrets.

### Myth 2: There Exists a Perfect "Entry Timing"
-   **False Belief**: Wait for a perfect candlestick pattern to appear before entering.
-   **Reality**: Every bar has huge volume, meaning at any moment there are institutions buying and institutions selling. Therefore, there's no single "best" entry point; if you miss the first, there will always be another. The key is learning to read price action, understanding both bulls' and bears' reasoning, and entering with a plan.

### Myth 3: Trading Can Make You Rich Easily and Quickly
-   **False Belief**: Learning a dozen candlestick patterns is enough for stable profitability.
-   **Reality**: Trading is extremely difficult work. Simply trading based on isolated patterns doesn't work and is even a "scam." Becoming a successful trader requires tremendous effort and time in learning and practice.

## Core Trading Principles & The Big Picture

### Trading with Strong Trends
-   **Core Idea**: The big picture of trading is "buy low, sell high," but in strong trends, interpretation needs adjustment.
-   **Strong Uptrend**:
    -   Should ignore all minor resistance levels (like double tops, wedge tops).
    -   Assume all reversal attempts will fail and evolve into bull flags.
    -   Only look for reasons and opportunities to go long.
-   **Strong Downtrend**:
    -   Should ignore all minor support levels (like double bottoms, wedge bottoms).
    -   Assume all rallies will fail and evolve into bear flags.
    -   Only look for shorting opportunities; don't try to catch bounces in tight bear channels.

### Management Over Entry
-   **Importance**: In most market conditions (about 90% of the time), proper trade management is more important than precise entry points.
-   **Key Elements**:
    -   **Proper Stops**: Set reasonable stops based on price action structure, not arbitrary tight stops.
    -   **Position Management**: If stop distance is far, reduce initial position size and consider adding at favorable locations.
    -   **Risk Control**: For higher-risk trades, consider using options to limit maximum loss.

## Choosing Charts and Timeframes

### Universality of Price Action
-   **Cross-Market Universal**: Whether stocks, forex, futures, or bonds, price action analysis techniques are universal because they're all based on rational human behavior.
-   **Chart Type Doesn't Matter**: Whether using time charts (like 5-minute), volume charts, or tick charts, they reveal essentially the same price action patterns. Traders should choose the chart type they feel most comfortable with.

### Timeframe Selection & Pitfalls
-   **Beginner Starting Point**: 5-minute chart is a good starting point for day trading. Avoid trading charts with overly fast pace (like less than 3 minutes).
-   **Small Timeframe Trap (like 1-minute chart)**:
    -   **Problem**: While individual trade risk seems very small, it requires traders to make numerous decisions in extremely short time, very easy to make mistakes, leading to low win rates.
    -   **Conclusion**: This is unsustainable "manual high-frequency trading"; the vast majority of traders cannot achieve stable profits long-term this way and will eventually deplete accounts.
-   **Correct Path to Profit Growth**: After achieving stable profits on a timeframe (like 5-minute chart), the **best way to increase profits is to gradually increase position size**, not shifting to smaller timeframes to pursue more trade frequency.

### Multi-Timeframe Analysis (TBTL Rule)
-   **Concept**: When discovering an important reversal pattern (like a large wedge spanning hundreds of bars), should switch to a higher timeframe (like 60-minute chart) to observe.
-   **Application**: Trading target should be "at least Ten Bars, Two Legs" (TBTL) correction on this **higher timeframe**, not exiting too early with small profits on the original smaller timeframe chart.

## Summary & Additional Notes
-   **Core Concept**: Break the illusion of "perfect trading," recognize that trading is a difficult battle against top opponents.
-   **Trading Method**: Success lies in understanding price action and market context, trading with strong trends, and prioritizing trade management (stops, positions).
-   **Tool Selection**: Beginners should start with slower charts like 5-minute, and after proving stable profitability, increase profits by **increasing position size** rather than shortening timeframes.
-   **Liquidity & Slippage**: For mainstream markets (like S&P futures, large stocks, major currency pairs), liquidity is extremely high; retail traders need not worry about their position sizes causing slippage issues.
