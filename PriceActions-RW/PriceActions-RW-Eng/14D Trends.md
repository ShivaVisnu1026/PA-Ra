# Three Types of Trends

## Trend from the Open

### Definition & Characteristics
-   **Concept**: A strong trend typically starting early in trading session (like first hour) but not necessarily from first bar.
-   **Identification Features**:
    -   **Opening Urgency**: Opening shows consecutive, strong trend bars (like large bull/bear bars) showing one-sided force.
    -   **Extremely Shallow Pullbacks**: In initial trend phase (Spike), pullbacks very small or almost none; subsequent bar lows/highs continue breaking prior bar.
    -   **Subsequent Development**: After strong opening trend, market typically consolidates (entering channel or trading range) for hours, then decides whether to continue trend or reverse.
-   **24-Hour Market Application**:
    -   Most applicable in markets with clear opening times like stocks or stock index futures.
    -   For 24-hour markets like forex with no unified opening time, Al Brooks tends to call it "breakout" or "spike and channel" trend, but core logic (strong one-sided movement in early active session) is the same.

## Trending Trading Range Day

### Structure & Identification
-   **Concept**: A trend composed of series of trading ranges. Overall direction moving but contains large amounts of two-way trading.
-   **Typical Structure**: `Trading Range → Brief Breakout → New Trading Range`, cycling like this.
-   **Characteristics**:
    -   Opening forms an initial range, its height typically about half of average daily range.
    -   Breakouts connecting ranges usually brief (lasting only a few bars).

### Trading Implications & Probabilities
-   **Range Overlap**: 60% probability later range overlaps with prior range. **If no overlap (forming gap), it's a signal of strong trend**.
-   **Breakout Mode**: When a trading range lasts over 20 bars, market enters breakout mode; probability of breaking up or down becomes 50/50.
-   **End Pattern**: Triangle pattern in late trend is usually final flag; 60-70% probability leads to trend reversal.

## Trend in a Broad Channel

### Definition & Characteristics
-   **Concept**: A trend with clear overall direction (like rising highs and lows) but contains very deep, strong pullbacks.
-   **Identification Features**:
    -   **Stepping Pattern**: After breakout makes new high/low, pullback deepens into breakout point. This shows trend is weaker; counter-trend traders can profit.
    -   **Two-Way Trading Characteristics**: Due to deep pullbacks, market has both trend and trading range dual characteristics. When uncertain, can trade using trading range strategy (fade extremes).
    -   **Shrinking Steps**: Important trend exhaustion signal. Refers to later trend legs (steps) being shorter than prior ones, showing momentum weakening; trend may soon turn to range or reversal.

### Trading Strategy
-   **With-Trend Traders**: In deep pullback to middle area of prior trend leg, look for reversal signals (like double bottoms/tops, signal bars) to enter, taking profits near prior highs/lows.
-   **Counter-Trend Traders**: At channel edges or near new highs/lows, perform counter scalping because "stepping pattern" provides profit space.
-   **Channel Lines**: Channel lines rarely perfect; need constant redrawing based on new highs and lows.

## Summary Principles
-   **Trend from Open**: Identify opening "urgency"; this is early signal of strong trend, but prepare for subsequent consolidation.
-   **Trending Trading Range Day**: Judge trend strength by observing "overlap" or "gaps" between ranges; note ranges exceeding 20 bars enter 50/50 breakout mode.
-   **Broad Channel Trend**: Identify "stepping pattern" and "shrinking steps." Former is opportunity for two-way trading; latter is warning trend is ending.
