# Trading Single-Sided Trends (Spike and Channel)

## Core Concepts of Spike & Channel Trends

-   **Definition & Identification**: Single-sided trend (Spike and Channel) refers to market led by a strong initial breakout (Spike, "peak"), followed by entering a relatively gradual channel continuing to run. This shows market has overwhelming force in one direction (buying or selling pressure); price likely continues in that direction.
-   **Core Trading Principle**: In single-sided uptrend, should only consider buying; in single-sided downtrend, should only consider selling. Following market's dominant force is key.

## Trading Strategy for Single-Sided Uptrend

### Entry Timing
-   **Trade With-Trend**: Can buy for any reason, such as buying pullbacks, buying breakout above prior high, or buying on strong bull bar close.
-   **Identify Signals**: Focus on bullish formations in pullbacks, like High 2 buy (H2), wedge bottoms, etc.

### Stop-Loss Management
-   **Initial Stop**: Place below the start point of entire single-sided uptrend (major swing low).
-   **Risk Control**: Due to initial stops typically far, **must use small position size** to control risk.
-   **Moving Stops**: After price makes new highs, can move stop up to below the most recent "major low" (the low that triggered new strong rally).

## Trading Strategy for Single-Sided Downtrend

### Entry Timing
-   **Sell Rallies**: At rallies to moving average (EMA) or near prior highs, look for any sell signals, like Low 1/Low 2 sells (L1/L2), bear flags, head and shoulders tops, etc.
-   **"Sell Close" Strategy**: In very strong bear channels, traders can immediately market sell on strong bear bar close.

### Stop-Loss Management
-   **Initial Stop**: Place above the start point of entire single-sided downtrend (major swing high).
-   **Moving Stops**: After price makes new lows, can move stop down to above the most recent "major high" (the high that triggered new strong decline).

## Identifying Trend Transitions

### Assessing Rally/Pullback Quality
-   **Weak Rally Signal**: In downtrend, even if rally appears, if lacking consecutive strong bull bars and bars have long upper tails, this is likely just bear pullback (bear flag) rather than trend reversal.
-   **Exhaustion Signal**: In downtrend, if a bar appears completely above moving average (MA gap bar), this shows strengthening bull force; decline momentum may be about to exhaust; market may turn to trading range.

### Reliable Reversal Signals
-   **Pattern Confirmation**: Trend termination usually requires a strong, clear large-scale reversal pattern, like **Higher Low Major Trend Reversal**, wedge bull flags, or head and shoulders bottoms.
-   **Hold Firm**: Before reliable reversal signals above appear, with-trend traders should hold positions firmly, ignoring small counter-trend swings.
-   **Exit Timing**: When a reliable counter-trend reversal pattern is confirmed (e.g., strong signal bar with good follow-through), it's a reasonable time for with-trend positions to exit.

## Summary Principles
-   **Trade With-Trend is Foundation**: Identify market's dominant force (buying or selling pressure) and only trade in that direction.
-   **Position Management is Key**: Due to initial stops in single-sided trends typically far, must use small position size to manage risk; can consider scaling in.
-   **Manage Stops Dynamically**: Initial stops at trend start; as trend continues, move stops behind new major highs/lows for protection.
-   **Assess Reversals Rationally**: Don't get scared out by small counter-trend swings. Only consider exiting or reversing when strong, trustworthy large-scale reversal patterns appear.
