# Program Trading

## What is Program Trading?

### Definition & Market Dynamics
-   **Concept**: Institutions use computer programs (algorithms) for automated trading. These algorithms, based on various strategies, continuously seek opportunities in the market.
-   **Market Game**: The market is constantly in a battle between bullish algorithms and bearish algorithms. One side's programs try to push price through breakouts while the other tries to prevent it. When one side is strong enough and succeeds in breaking out, the losing side exits with stops, accelerating price movement in the breakout direction.
-   **Arbitrage Trading**: This is an important component of program trading (about 30% of trading volume). Algorithms monitor highly correlated instruments (like SPY and ES); once a small price discrepancy appears, they buy the cheaper and sell the more expensive for hedging until the spread disappears. This makes related markets move in high synchronization.

### Implications for Retail Traders
-   **Follow Institutions**: Retail traders cannot influence the market; trading success lies in learning price action, understanding institutional intent, and "riding the coattails."
-   **Understanding Price Action**: For example, consecutive strong bull bars mean institutions are buying heavily, and the market will likely continue higher—time to look for buying opportunities with the trend. Conversely, if a strong trend fails to continue, it shows bulls disappointed and bears entering, potentially signaling a reversal.

## High-Frequency Trading (HFT)

### Definition & Characteristics
-   **Concept**: A special type of program trading characterized by extreme speed, huge trading volume, and tiny profit per trade (usually just one minimum tick).
-   **Volume Dominance**: HFT contributes the majority of market volume (like 70% in ES market), other institutions about 25%, while retail traders represent a tiny fraction (less than 5%).
-   **Trade Size**: HFT individual trade sizes are typically small (like average 6 ES contracts or 100 shares of stock), but accumulates profit by executing millions or tens of millions of trades daily.
-   **Business Model**: Relies on slight win rate advantage (like 55%) and massive trade frequency, not high returns per trade. Like a casino, profiting steadily through probability advantage.

### Relationship with Retail Traders
-   **Minimal Impact**: HFT trades at millisecond or even microsecond levels, operating in a completely different dimension from retail traders on minute or hour charts. Therefore, should not blame personal trading losses on HFT.
-   **Competition Target**: HFT's main competition is other institutions, not retail traders, because retail capital is too small to be their main profit source.

## Illegal Trading Practices

### Spoofing
-   **Mechanism**: Trader places a huge order with no intent to fill (like a fake sell order), luring other traders to believe the market will fall and follow with sells. When price drops, the trader cancels the fake order and buys at lower prices.
-   **Purpose**: Manipulate the market by creating false supply/demand information to gain favorable entry prices.
-   **Status**: Illegal practice.

### Front-Running
-   **Mechanism**: Brokers or institutions exploit information advantage (like seeing clients' large orders ahead of time) to trade for their own accounts before client orders execute. For example, seeing a large buy order, they buy for themselves first, pushing prices higher, then sell their positions to clients at higher prices, capturing the spread.
-   **Basis**: Relies on extreme speed advantage, like server co-location near exchanges and using the fastest computers.
-   **Status**: Illegal practice.

## Summary Principles
-   **Institutions Dominate Markets**: Markets are dominated by institutional program trading (especially HFT); retail trader power is negligible.
-   **Learning Price Action is Key**: Retail trader success lies in learning to read price action on charts, understanding the institutional battle behind it, and trading accordingly.
-   **Reject Excuses, Focus on Learning**: Blaming losses on HFT or market manipulation is unhelpful. Becoming a consistently profitable trader requires investing thousands of hours in hard study and practice—there are no shortcuts.
