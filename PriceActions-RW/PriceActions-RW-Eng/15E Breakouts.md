# Breakouts (Part 5): Gaps, Strength & Testing

## Exhaustion vs. Measuring Gaps

### Definition & Identification
-   **Core Question**: When a very large bar appears in trend, is it a signal of trend continuation (measuring gap) or trend termination (exhaustion gap)?
-   **Key Factor: Context**
    -   **Measuring Gap**: Usually appears **early** in trend or just after breakout from trading range. Strong breakout bar traps counter-trend traders, shows strong momentum; trend likely continues, often with measured move target.
    -   **Exhaustion Gap**: Usually appears **late in trend** (e.g., after lasting over 20 bars). The largest bar in entire trend appearing at this time is more likely "final frenzy" (buying/selling climax) rather than start of new trend.

### Trading Implications
-   **Identification Signal**: Seeing huge bar late in trend, be alert to possibility of exhaustion gap, especially when quickly reversed by subsequent bars.
-   **Expected Movement**: Measuring gap predicts trend continuation. Exhaustion gap often doesn't immediately cause reversal but first enters **trading range** (big up then big down, representing market confusion).
-   **Target**: If exhaustion gap begins reversing, first target is usually this huge bar's **start point**.

## Breakout Strength & Pullback Depth

### Strong Breakout
-   **Characteristics**: Composed of consecutive large-body bars, closes near extremes, little overlap between bars, strong follow-through willingness.
-   **Trading Implications**: Shows one market side has strong conviction. Pullbacks after breakout usually **shallow** and **brief**, good opportunity for with-trend scaling, better for swing trading.

### Weak Breakout
-   **Characteristics**: Small bar bodies, obvious upper/lower tails, much overlap between bars, poor follow-through willingness (e.g., doji or counter bar after breakout).
-   **Trading Implications**: Shows market lacks conviction; bulls and bears balanced, confusion exists. After breakout usually **deep pullback** (like 50%+) or evolves into **sideways range**.

## The Breakout Test

### Core Concept
-   **Breakout is Surprise**: Strong breakouts are low-probability events; market usually retraces, **testing** breakout validity.
-   **Test Purpose**: Examines whether traders who initiated breakout, when price returns to breakout area, still willing to enter at same price again, confirming their conviction.

### Two Outcomes
-   **Test Successful (Trend Resumes)**: Original breakout side re-enters, repels counter side, forms flag (like High 2/Low 1), trend continues. This usually leaves an unfilled **gap** near breakout point (becoming measuring gap).
-   **Test Failed (Trend Reverses)**: Original breakout side hesitates or lacks strength; counter side seizes control, causing breakout failure; trend may **reverse**.

### Trader Psychology
-   **Breakout Side**: Re-enters on test, confirming trend.
-   **Trapped Counter Side (Limit Order Traders)**: They entered counter-trend at breakout, got trapped; hope price tests breakout point to **exit at breakeven**. Their exit behavior increases pressure in original trend direction.

## Summary Principles
-   **Context is Key**: Huge bars late in trend (>20 bars) more likely exhaustion signal (climax) rather than acceleration signal.
-   **Strength Determines Depth**: Strong breakouts bring shallow pullbacks, signal of trend continuation; weak breakouts bring deep pullbacks or range, signal of market confusion.
-   **Breakouts Always Tested**: Breakouts are "surprises," always face testing. Watching whether original breakout side re-enters on test is key to judging whether trend can continue.
